## Thomas-Kilmann Conflict Modes

### Card 1
**Front**: What are the 5 Thomas-Kilmann conflict resolution modes?
**Back**: 1) Collaborate/Problem Solve, 2) Compromise, 3) Force/Direct, 4) Smooth/Accommodate, 5) Withdraw/Avoid.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Manage conflicts

---

### Card 2
**Front**: Which two dimensions map the 5 conflict modes?
**Back**: Assertiveness (concern for self) and Cooperativeness (concern for others).
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Manage conflicts

---

### Card 3
**Front**: Describe the **Collaborate / Problem Solve** conflict mode.
**Back**: High assertiveness + high cooperation. Win/Win. Finding a solution that fully satisfies both parties.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Manage conflicts

---

### Card 4
**Front**: Describe the **Compromise** conflict mode.
**Back**: Moderate assertiveness + moderate cooperation. Lose/Lose (partially). Both sides give something up to reach a quick deal.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Manage conflicts

---

### Card 5
**Front**: Describe the **Force / Direct** conflict mode.
**Back**: High assertiveness + low cooperation. Win/Lose. Using authority to force a decision.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Manage conflicts

---

### Card 6
**Front**: Describe the **Smooth / Accommodate** conflict mode.
**Back**: Low assertiveness + high cooperation. Lose/Win. Emphasizing agreement to preserve the relationship.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Manage conflicts

---

### Card 7
**Front**: Describe the **Withdraw / Avoid** conflict mode.
**Back**: Low assertiveness + low cooperation. Yield/Retreat. Postponing the issue or backing away.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Manage conflicts

## Choosing the Right Conflict Mode

### Card 8
**Front**: When is **Collaborate** the best choice?
**Back**: When the root cause matters, you have time, trust is high, and you want buy-in from both sides. It's the default "best" answer on the PMP exam for non-emergencies.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Manage conflicts

---

### Card 9
**Front**: When is **Compromise** the best choice?
**Back**: When a temporary solution is needed, there is time pressure, or two parties of equal power are deadlocked.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Manage conflicts

---

### Card 10
**Front**: When is **Force / Direct** the best choice?
**Back**: For emergencies, safety issues, severe compliance violations, or when immediate action is required ("stop the bleeding").
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Manage conflicts

---

### Card 11
**Front**: When is **Smooth / Accommodate** the best choice?
**Back**: When the issue is minor, you realize you are wrong, or preserving the relationship is more important than the outcome.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Manage conflicts

---

### Card 12
**Front**: When is **Withdraw / Avoid** the best choice?
**Back**: When emotions are too high (cooling off period needed), you need more information, or the issue is trivial/symptomatic of something else.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Manage conflicts

---

### Card 13
**Front**: Why is "Compromise" often a trap answer on the PMP exam?
**Back**: It is often a "Lose-Lose" where real problems remain unsolved. Always look for Collaboration (Problem Solving) first unless time is critical.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Manage conflicts

---

### Card 14
**Front**: In a safety-critical emergency, which conflict mode should you use?
**Back**: Force / Direct.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Manage conflicts

---

### Card 15
**Front**: Two team members are shouting at each other. What is your immediate conflict response?
**Back**: Withdraw / Avoid (temporarily) to let them cool down, or separate them physically. Do not try to solve the root cause while emotions are high.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Manage conflicts

**Back**: Task Conflict (disagreements about ideas, technical approaches).
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Manage conflicts

### Card 18

**Domain**: People | **Task**: Manage conflicts

**Domain**: People | **Task**: Manage conflicts

## Conflict Resolution Process

### Card 21
**Front**: What is Step 1 in the Conflict Resolution Escalation Ladder?
**Back**: Self-Correction: Let team members try to solve it themselves first. Monitor but don't intervene immediately.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Manage conflicts

---

### Card 22
**Front**: What is Step 2 in Conflict Resolution if self-correction fails?
**Back**: Private Intervention: Meet 1-on-1 with each party to understand perspectives.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Manage conflicts

---

### Card 23
**Front**: What is Step 3 in Conflict Resolution?
**Back**: Collaborative Session: Bring parties together to facilitate a solution using data.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Manage conflicts

---

### Card 24
**Front**: When should a PM use Formal Authority (Step 4) to resolve conflict?
**Back**: Only when the team remains deadlocked after facilitation. The PM breaks the tie/decides.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Manage conflicts

---

### Card 25
**Front**: When should you Escalate (Step 5) to a sponsor or functional manager?
**Back**: Only if the decision requires authority outside the project (e.g., budget increase, resource reassignment across depts).
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Manage conflicts

---

### Card 26
**Front**: What is the "Collaboration Agenda" used for?
**Back**: A structured 15-30 minute meeting to move conflicting parties from positions to a shared decision. Steps: Problem -> Interests -> Options -> Criteria -> Decide.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Manage conflicts

## Negotiation Fundamentals

### Card 27
**Front**: What is "Interest-Based Negotiation" (Principled Negotiation)?
**Back**: A negotiation strategy that focuses on underlying needs (interests) rather than rigid demands (positions) to find win-win solutions.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Manage conflicts

---

### Card 28
**Front**: What is the difference between a Position and an Interest?
**Back**: Position = What they say they want ("I need it Friday"). Interest = Why they want it ("I need to prep for Monday's meeting").
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Manage conflicts

---

### Card 29
**Front**: Why is uncovering Interests crucial?
**Back**: It opens up multiple solution options. Positions are binary (yes/no), but Interests can be satisfied in many ways.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Manage conflicts

---

### Card 30
**Front**: What is ZOPA?
**Back**: Zone of Possible Agreement. The overlap between the Buyer's max price and Seller's min price (or generally, the overlap in acceptable terms).
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Manage conflicts

---

### Card 31
**Front**: What is BATNA?
**Back**: Best Alternative to a Negotiated Agreement. Your "Plan B" if the negotiation fails.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Manage conflicts

---

### Card 32
**Front**: Why must you know your BATNA before negotiating?
**Back**: It gives you leverage. If the offer on the table is worse than your BATNA, you should walk away.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Manage conflicts

---

### Card 33
**Front**: What is a "Reservation Price" (or Point)?
**Back**: Your "walk away" point. The worst outcome you are willing to accept.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Manage conflicts

---

### Card 34
**Front**: What is the difference between Distributive and Integrative negotiation?
**Back**: Distributive = Win-Lose ("Fixed Pie", competing for value). Integrative = Win-Win ("Expand the Pie", creating mutual value).
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Manage conflicts

---

### Card 35
**Front**: Which negotiation strategy is preferred for project relationships?
**Back**: Integrative (Win-Win). Projects rely on long-term relationships and trust.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Manage conflicts

---

## Negotiation Tactics

### Card 36
**Front**: What is "Anchoring" in negotiation?
**Back**: Throwing out the first number (often extreme) to set the reference point for the discussion.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Manage conflicts

---

### Card 37
**Front**: How do you respond to "Anchoring"?
**Back**: Ignore the anchor or counter-anchor immediately. Do not let the extreme number frame the discussion. Focus on objective criteria.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Manage conflicts

---

### Card 38
**Front**: What is the "Good Cop/Bad Cop" tactic?
**Back**: One person is tough/unreasonable, the other is friendly/reasonable. Goal is to make you concede to the "nice" one.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Manage conflicts

---

### Card 39
**Front**: What is the "Silence" tactic?
**Back**: The other party stays quiet after your offer to make you uncomfortable and trigger you to bid against yourself. Response: Wait them out.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Manage conflicts

---

### Card 40
**Front**: What is "Nibbling"?
**Back**: Asking for small extras (e.g., "throw in free training") just as the deal is closing. Response: Document scope clearly; trade, don't give.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Manage conflicts

---

### Card 41
**Front**: What is "Objective Criteria" in negotiation (from Getting to Yes)?
**Back**: Using independent standards (market rates, laws, industry benchmarks) to decide terms, rather than a battle of wills.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Manage conflicts

### Card 44
**Front**: What is Mediation?
**Back**: A neutral third party facilitates the discussion but DOES NOT have decision-making power. They help parties find their own solution.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Manage conflicts

---

### Card 45
**Front**: What is Arbitration?
**Back**: A neutral third party hears both sides and DOES make a decision (usually binding). Less formal/costly than court.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Manage conflicts

---

### Card 46
**Front**: What is Litigation?
**Back**: Resolving the dispute in court. The most expensive, slowest, and damaging method. Last resort.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Manage conflicts

## Decision Making Models

### Card 47
**Front**: What is Consensus decision-making?
**Back**: "I can live with it." High buy-in. It does NOT mean everyone loves it, but everyone agrees to support it.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Manage conflicts

---

### Card 48
**Front**: What is Unanimity decision-making?
**Back**: 100% agreement. Very slow, high risk of deadlock. Use for core values only.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Manage conflicts

---

### Card 49
**Front**: What is Majority decision-making?
**Back**: >50% wins. Fast, creates a clear decision, but risks alienating the minority.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Manage conflicts

---

### Card 50
**Front**: What is Plurality decision-making?
**Back**: The largest block wins (even if <50%). Good for choosing among many options.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Manage conflicts

---

### Card 51
**Front**: What is Autocratic decision-making?
**Back**: One person decides. Fastest method. Low buy-in. Best for emergencies.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Manage conflicts

---

### Card 52
**Front**: What is "Fist of Five" voting?
**Back**: A quick consensus check. 5 fingers = full support, 1 finger = major concern/block.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Manage conflicts

---

### Card 53
**Front**: What is "Dot Voting"?
**Back**: A visual prioritization technique where participants place dots (sticky notes) on their preferred options.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Manage conflicts

## De-escalation Techniques

### Card 54
**Front**: What are 4 verbal de-escalation techniques?
**Back**: 1) Acknowledge ("I see you're frustrated"), 2) Validate ("Your concern is real"), 3) Reframe ("Let's focus on the goal"), 4) Pause ("Let's take a break").
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Manage conflicts

---

### Card 55
**Front**: What are 2 physical de-escalation techniques?
**Back**: 1) Move to a private space (remove audience), 2) Sit down (standing signals aggression/dominance).
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Manage conflicts

---

### Card 56
**Front**: Why should you move a heated argument to a private space?
**Back**: It removes the "theater" (audience), lowers stakes, and allows for psychological safety to back down or explain positions.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Manage conflicts

## Exam Scenarios: Manage Conflicts & Negotiation

### Card 61
**Front**: Exam Scenario: A vendor says "Take it or leave it." How do you respond?
**Back**: Test the rigidity. Focus on interests. Do not react emotionally. Check your BATNA.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Manage conflicts

---

### Card 62
**Front**: Exam Scenario: Two senior stakeholders have opposing requirements for the same feature. What do you do?
**Back**: Facilitate a collaborative session. Identify the business value/interests behind each requirement. Use objective criteria to prioritize.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Manage conflicts

---

### Card 63
**Front**: Exam Scenario: A team member refuses to use the new software tool. What do you do?
**Back**: Meet privately (Step 2). Understand their resistance (interest). Coaching/Training might be the answer, or there might be a valid technical blocker.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Manage conflicts

---

### Card 64
**Front**: Exam Scenario: The team is debating a technical solution and cannot agree. The deadline is tomorrow. What do you do?
**Back**: Move to a decision-making model (like Majority or Autocratic/Expert) to unblock the team, since time is a constraint. (Compromise might also apply here).
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Manage conflicts

---

### Card 65
**Front**: Exam Scenario: You overhear a heated argument about politics between two team members. What do you do?
**Back**: Intervene. This is Relationship Conflict (not Task). Remind them of Ground Rules regarding respect and focus.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Manage conflicts
