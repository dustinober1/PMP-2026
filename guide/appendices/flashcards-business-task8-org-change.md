## ü´Ç Change Models

### Card 1
**Front:**
What is **Organizational Change Management (OCM)**?

**Back:**
A structured approach to ensure that changes are thoroughly and smoothly implemented, and that the lasting benefits of change are achieved.

### Card 2
**Front:**
What does **ADKAR** stand for?

**Back:**
**Awareness, Desire, Knowledge, Ability, Reinforcement**.

### Card 3
**Front:**
**Scenario**: "I know how to use the new system, but I don't see why we need it." Which ADKAR element is missing?

**Back:**
**Desire**. (They have Knowledge, but lack Desire/Awareness).

### Card 4
**Front:**
**Scenario**: "I want to do it, but I keep making mistakes." Which ADKAR element is missing?

**Back:**
**Ability**. (They need coaching/practice).

### Card 5
**Front:**
What are **Kotter's 8 Steps** (First 3)?

**Back:**
1. Create Urgency.
2. Form a Guiding Coalition.
3. Create a Vision.

### Card 6
**Front:**
What is **Lewin's 3-Stage Model**?

**Back:**
**Unfreeze** (Prepare), **Change** (Execute), **Refreeze** (Sustain).

### Card 7
**Front:**
**Scenario**: "Unfreeze"?

**Back:**
Breaking the status quo and preparing the organization to accept that change is necessary.

### Card 8
**Front:**
**Scenario**: Peter Drucker's famous quote about culture?

**Back:**
"Culture eats strategy for breakfast."

### Card 9
**Front:**
What is the difference between **Project Management** and **Change Management**?

**Back:**
-   **PM**: Focuses on Technical delivery (Time, Cost, Scope). Output.
-   **CM**: Focuses on People side (Adoption, Usage). Outcome.

### Card 10
**Front:**
**Scenario**: You deliver the tool on time/budget, but nobody uses it. Was the project successful?

**Back:**
**No**. Value was not realized due to poor Change Management.

## üõë Managing Resistance

### Card 11
**Front:**
What is the primary reason for **Resistance**?

**Back:**
Fear (of job loss, of looking incompetent, of the unknown).

### Card 12
**Front:**
**Scenario**: "WIIFM"?

**Back:**
**What's In It For Me?** You must explain the personal benefit to the user to build Desire.

### Card 13
**Front:**
**Scenario**: An employee resists because they don't know *how* to use the new tool.

**Back:**
Provide **Training** and **Coaching**.

### Card 14
**Front:**
**Scenario**: An employee resists because they think the old way is better.

**Back:**
Address **Awareness** (Why the old way is failing) and **Desire** (Incentives/Benefits).

### Card 15
**Front:**
Who is best positioned to manage resistance from a specific employee?

**Back:**
Their **Direct Supervisor** (manager). The PM supports the manager.

### Card 16
**Front:**
**Scenario**: "Change Saturation"?

**Back:**
When an organization is going through too many changes at once, causing fatigue and failure.

### Card 17
**Front:**
**Scenario**: "Adoption Gap"?

**Back:**
The period between the "Go-Live" (Output) and the actual value realization (Outcome).

### Card 18
**Front:**
What is a **Change Champion**?

**Back:**
A person within the business unit who advocates for the change, helps peers, and provides feedback to the project team.

### Card 19
**Front:**
**Scenario**: You identify a cultural mismatch (e.g., Agile in a rigid hierarchy).

**Back:**
You cannot "install" a tool that violates culture. You must address the culture (Executive support, training, slow rollout) or adapt the strategy.

### Card 20
**Front:**
How do you measure **Adoption**?

**Back:**
Usage metrics (logins, transactions), Surveys, Proficiency tests.

## üõ†Ô∏è Change Plans & Tactics

### Card 21
**Front:**
What is a **Change Readiness Assessment**?

**Back:**
A survey or analysis done *before* the change to determine if the organization is ready and where resistance might lie.

### Card 22
**Front:**
What goes in a **Stakeholder Engagement Plan** for OCM?

**Back:**
Key messages, communication channels, timing, and feedback mechanisms for each group.

### Card 23
**Front:**
**Scenario**: "Reinforcement" in ADKAR?

**Back:**
Making the change stick. (e.g., stopping the old system, giving bonuses, celebrating success).

### Card 24
**Front:**
**Scenario**: "Burning Platform"?

**Back:**
Creating a sense of **Urgency**. "We cannot stay here; we must move or we perish."

### Card 25
**Front:**
**Scenario**: "Train the Trainer"?

**Back:**
Training a group of Super Users/Champions who then train their local teams. Scalable OCM strategy.

### Card 26
**Front:**
How does **Agile** handle Organizational Change?

**Back:**
Iteratively. Frequent demos reduce fear. Stakeholders co-create the solution (High Desire).

### Card 27
**Front:**
**Scenario**: The Sponsor is not visibly supporting the change.

**Back:**
Major Risk. The PM must coach the Sponsor to be an active **Coalition Builder** and communicator.

### Card 28
**Front:**
**Scenario**: "Sustainment Plan"?

**Back:**
The plan for who will support the tool/process *after* the project team disbands (Operations).

### Card 29
**Front:**
**Scenario**: Using "Stories" for change?

**Back:**
Telling success stories of early adopters to influence the rest of the organization (Social Proof).

### Card 30
**Front:**
**True or False**: Communication alone is enough to manage change.

**Back:**
**False**. You need Training, Coaching, and Sponsorship too.

### Card 36

**Back:**
Resistance will be high.

### Card 37
**Front:**
**Exam Tip**: Can a PM be a Change Manager?

**Back:**
**Yes**. On smaller projects, the PM wears both hats. On large ones, there may be a dedicated OCM lead.

### Card 38
**Front:**
**Scenario**: "Vision"?

**Back:**
A clear picture of the future state that inspires people to change.

### Card 39
**Front:**
**Scenario**: "Quick Wins"?

**Back:**
Showing early success to build momentum and silence critics.

### Card 40
**Front:**
**Scenario**: A stakeholder fears losing power/authority.

**Back:**
