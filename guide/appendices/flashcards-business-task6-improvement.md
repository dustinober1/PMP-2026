## üìà Continuous Improvement Models

### Card 1
**Front:**
What is **Kaizen**?

**Back:**
A philosophy of continuous, small incremental improvements involving everyone in the organization.

### Card 2
**Front:**
What is **PDCA** (Deming Cycle)?

**Back:**
**Plan-Do-Check-Act**. The fundamental cycle for process improvement.

### Card 3
**Front:**
What are the phases of **DMAIC** (Six Sigma)?

**Back:**
**Define, Measure, Analyze, Improve, Control**.

### Card 4
**Front:**
What is the primary goal of **Six Sigma**?

**Back:**
To reduce variation and defects (Target: 3.4 defects per million opportunities).

### Card 5
**Front:**
What is the primary goal of **Lean**?

**Back:**
To eliminate **Waste** (Muda) and improve flow.

### Card 6
**Front:**
What is the **Retrospective** in Agile?

**Back:**
A meeting held at the end of each iteration to discuss what went well, what didn't, and what to improve (Kaizen Event).

### Card 7
**Front:**
What is the **Pareto Principle**?

**Back:**
The **80/20 Rule**. 80% of effects come from 20% of causes. Focus your improvement efforts on the "Vital Few."

### Card 8
**Front:**
What is **Total Quality Management (TQM)**?

**Back:**
A management approach where everyone in the organization participates in improving processes, products, and culture.

### Card 9
**Front:**
What is a **Kaizen Blitz** (or Event)?

**Back:**
A dedicated, short-term (e.g., 3-5 days) project to improve a specific process area rapidly.

### Card 10
**Front:**
What is **Benchmarking**?

**Back:**
Comparing your project practices/metrics to those of other projects or organizations to identify best practices.

## üõ†Ô∏è Improvement Tools

### Card 11
**Front:**
What tool helps you find the **Root Cause** of a problem?

**Back:**
**Fishbone Diagram** (Ishikawa / Cause-and-Effect) or **5 Whys**.

### Card 12
**Front:**
What tool helps you prioritize which problems to fix first?

**Back:**
**Pareto Chart**.

### Card 13
**Front:**
What tool tells you if a process is **Stable** (in control)?

**Back:**
**Control Chart**.

### Card 14
**Front:**
What tool visualizes the steps in a process to find bottlenecks?

**Back:**
**Flowchart** (Process Map).

### Card 15
**Front:**
What is the **Rule of Seven** in Control Charts?

**Back:**
If 7 consecutive data points fall on one side of the mean, the process is **Out of Control** (even if within limits). Investigate.

### Card 16
**Front:**
What is a **Checksheet**?

**Back:**
A simple tally sheet used to collect data in real-time (e.g., counting defects).

### Card 17
**Front:**
What is a **Scatter Diagram** used for?

**Back:**
To show the relationship (correlation) between two variables.

### Card 18
**Front:**
What is **Value Stream Mapping**?

**Back:**
A Lean tool to map the flow of materials and information, identifying value-add vs non-value-add steps.

### Card 19
**Front:**
What is **DoE** (Design of Experiments)?

**Back:**
A statistical method to test multiple variables simultaneously to pinpoint optimal conditions.

### Card 20
**Front:**
What is **SIPOC**?

**Back:**
Suppliers, Inputs, Process, Outputs, Customers. A high-level process map used in Six Sigma (Define phase).

## üèõÔ∏è Quality Assurance vs Control

### Card 21
**Front:**
What is the difference between **QA** and **QC**?

**Back:**
-   **QA (Manage Quality)**: Managing the **Process** (Prevention).
-   **QC (Control Quality)**: Checking the **Product** (Inspection).

### Card 22
**Front:**
**Scenario**: You are auditing the team's adherence to the Safety Procedure. Is this QA or QC?

**Back:**
**QA**. You are checking the process.

### Card 23
**Front:**
**Scenario**: You are testing the software code for bugs. Is this QA or QC?

**Back:**
**QC**. You are checking the product.

### Card 24
**Front:**
**Scenario**: "Continuous Improvement" belongs to which Process Group?

**Back:**
**Executing** (Manage Quality).

### Card 25
**Front:**
What is a **Quality Audit**?

**Back:**
A structured review to determine if project activities comply with organizational and project policies/procedures.

### Card 26
**Front:**
What acts as the baseline for improvement?

**Back:**
**Metrics** (KPIs). You cannot improve what you do not measure.

### Card 27
**Front:**
**Scenario**: The team learns a new, faster way to code during the project. What should they do?

**Back:**
Update the **OPAs** (Organizational Process Assets) and share it (Knowledge Management).

### Card 28
**Front:**
What is **Just-in-Time (JIT)**?

**Back:**
A Lean inventory strategy to decrease waste by receiving goods only as they are needed.

### Card 29
**Front:**
What is **5S**?

**Back:**
Sort, Set in order, Shine, Standardize, Sustain. A methodology for workplace organization.

### Card 30
**Front:**
**Scenario**: "Gold Plating"?

**Back:**
Giving the customer extra features not requested. This is **Waste** (not improvement).

## üß† Situational Improvement

### Card 31
**Front:**
**Scenario**: A process is "In Control" but produces defects (doesn't meet spec).

**Back:**
The process is **Stable but not Capable**. You need to improve the process (QA), not just fix the outliers.

### Card 32
**Front:**
**Scenario**: The Retrospective turns into a "blame game."

**Back:**
The facilitator needs to re-focus on **Process** not People. Use data. Ensure Psychological Safety.

### Card 33
**Front:**
**Scenario**: Improve a process that you don't understand?

**Back:**
Impossible. Use a **Flowchart** or GEMBA walk (go see) to understand the "As-Is" state first.

### Card 34
**Front:**
**Scenario**: "Analysis Paralysis" in improvement?

**Back:**
Don't wait for perfect data. Use **PDCA**. Try a small change, check result, adjust.

### Card 35
**Front:**
**Scenario**: A stakeholder suggests a change to improve efficiency.

**Back:**
Evaluate it. Calculate ROI. If good, implement via Change Control (if needed) or continuous improvement loops.

### Card 36
**Front:**
**True or False**: Lessons Learned are only done at the end of the project.

**Back:**
**False**. They should be continuous (Retrospectives).

### Card 37
**Front:**
**Scenario**: "Retrospective fatigue"?

**Back:**
Use varied formats (e.g., Sailboat, Starfish, Mad/Sad/Glad) to keep engagement high.

### Card 38
**Front:**
What is the "Cost of Quality" trade-off?

**Back:**
Investing in **Prevention** (QA) costs money now but saves much more money later (Failure Costs).

### Card 39
**Front:**
**Scenario**: You find 100 defects. 80 are caused by "Bad Requirements."

**Back:**
Fix the Requirements Process. (Pareto Principle).

### Card 40
**Front:**
**Scenario**: Team ignores the process because "it's too slow."

**Back:**
The process is broken. **Improve it** (Streamline). Do not force compliance to a bad process.
