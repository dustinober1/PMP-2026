## üöß Impediments vs. Issues vs. Risks

### Card 1
**Front:**
What is the difference between a **Risk** and an **Issue**?

**Back:**
-   **Risk**: Uncertain *future* event (has not happened yet).
-   **Issue**: Current *present* problem (is happening now).

### Card 2
**Front:**
What is an **Impediment** (Blocker)?

**Back:**
Anything that prevents the team from performing work or meeting objectives (e.g., missing license, down server). Key term in Agile.

### Card 3
**Front:**
**Scenario**: A risk event you identified has occurred. What does it become?

**Back:**
An **Issue**. Move it from the Risk Register to the Issue Log.

### Card 4
**Front:**
**Scenario**: The team is blocked by a missing server access. Who is responsible for removing this impediment?

**Back:**
The **Scrum Master** (or Project Manager/Servant Leader).

### Card 5
**Front:**
**Scenario**: An issue arises that exceeds the Project Manager's authority to resolve (e.g., funding). What do you do?

**Back:**
**Escalate** it to the Sponsor or Steering Committee.

### Card 6
**Front:**
What is the **Issue Log** used for?

**Back:**
To document, track, and assign owners to problems that are currently affecting the project.

### Card 7
**Front:**
**Scenario**: You find a problem. You fix it immediately. Do you log it?

**Back:**
**Yes**. Log it as closed to capture the history and lessons learned.

### Card 8
**Front:**
What is the "Daily Standup" role in impediment management?

**Back:**
To **Identify** blockers. (Not necessarily to solve them right there).

### Card 9
**Front:**
**Scenario**: A stakeholder is blocking a decision.

**Back:**
Treat it as an **Impediment**. Discuss with the stakeholder, understand the root cause, and facilitate a decision. Escalate if deadlocked.

### Card 10
**Front:**
What is the **Escalation Process**?

**Back:**
Documented steps in the Communications Management Plan defining when and how to raise issues to higher levels of authority.

## ‚öîÔ∏è Conflict Management

### Card 11
**Front:**
What is the **Best** conflict resolution technique for long-term solutions?

**Back:**
**Collaborate / Problem Solve** (Win-Win). Addresses the root cause.

### Card 12
**Front:**
**Scenario**: Two team members disagree on a technical approach. Time is NOT critical. What technique do you use?

**Back:**
**Collaborate**. Use a whiteboard/data to find the best technical solution.

### Card 13
**Front:**
**Scenario**: Two stakeholders are fighting. You are in an emergency and need a decision NOW to ensure safety.

**Back:**
**Force / Direct**. (Win-Lose). Use authority.

### Card 14
**Front:**
**Scenario**: Both parties need to give up something to reach a temporary agreement.

**Back:**
**Compromise**. (Lose-Lose).

### Card 15
**Front:**
**Scenario**: The issue is trivial, or emotions are too high to discuss.

**Back:**
**Withdraw / Avoid**. (Retreat). Wait for cool-down.

### Card 16
**Front:**
**Scenario**: You want to preserve the relationship, and the issue is more important to them than to you.

**Back:**
**Smooth / Accommodate**. (Lose-Win).

### Card 17
**Front:**
**Scenario**: "Personality Clash" vs "Process Issue"?

**Back:**
Most "personality" conflicts are actually **process** conflicts (unclear roles, goals). Fix the process first.

### Card 18
**Front:**
**True or False**: Conflict is bad and should be suppressed.

**Back:**
**False**. Constructive conflict (Task Conflict) leads to better ideas. Relationship conflict is bad.

## üõ†Ô∏è Issue Management Toolkit

### Card 19
**Front:**
What goes in an **Issue Log**?

**Back:**
Issue ID, Description, Priority, Owner, Target Date, Status.

### Card 20
**Front:**
**Scenario**: An issue is assigned to a team member. They haven't fixed it by the due date.

**Back:**
**Follow Up** (Control). Ask: "What do you need to unblock this?"

### Card 21
**Front:**
What is a **Decision Log**?

**Back:**
A record of all key decisions made, who made them, and when. Prevents "re-litigating" old decisions.

### Card 22
**Front:**
**Scenario**: The team generates too many impediments.

**Back:**
Look for the **Root Cause**. Use a Retrospective to fix the systemic issue.

### Card 23
**Front:**
What is **Root Cause Analysis (RCA)**?

**Back:**
A technique (e.g., 5 Whys, Fishbone) to find the underlying reason for a defect or issue.

### Card 24
**Front:**
**Scenario**: A vendor is non-responsive. This is an issue.

**Back:**
Check the **Contract** (SLA). Escalate according to the procurement terms.

### Card 25
**Front:**
**Scenario**: You have an issue with valid solutions A and B.

**Back:**
Use **Decision Making** techniques (e.g., Voting, Multi-Criteria Decision Analysis) to choose. Record in Decision Log.

### Card 26
**Front:**
**Scenario**: An impediment requires money to fix.

**Back:**
Check **Reserves**. If within PM authority (Contingency), use it. If not, submit **Change Request** for Management Reserve.

### Card 27
**Front:**
**Scenario**: A "Swarm" or "Mob" approach?

**Back:**
Agile technique where the whole team focuses on fixing a critical blocked item together.

### Card 28
**Front:**
What is **Technical Debt** dealing with?

**Back:**
It's an issue of "future rework" accumulating. It impedes velocity over time.

### Card 29
**Front:**
**Scenario**: A team member hides an issue because they are afraid.

**Back:**
Lack of **Psychological Safety**. The PM must build trust so bad news can travel fast.

### Card 30
**Front:**
What is the relationship between **Quality Control** and Issues?

**Back:**
QC identifies defects. Defects are issues.

## üß† Situational Issues

### Card 31
**Front:**
**Scenario**: The client refuses to sign off on a deliverable.

**Back:**
Check **Acceptance Criteria**. If met, show them. If not met, fix it (Defect Repair).

### Card 32
**Front:**
**Scenario**: You take over a project. No Issue Log effectively exists.

**Back:**
**Create one immediately**. Interview the team to find what is blocking them.

### Card 33
**Front:**
**Scenario**: An issue affects the Critical Path.

**Back:**
**High Urgency**. It will delay the project end date. Crash/Fast Track or Escalate.

### Card 34
**Front:**
**Scenario**: You are spending 100% of your time fighting fires (issues).

**Back:**
You are failing at **Risk Management**. Effective Risk Management reduces Issues.

### Card 35
**Front:**
**Scenario**: A stakeholder complains about "Lack of Communication."

**Back:**
This is an Issue. Review the **Communications Management Plan** and adjust.

### Card 36
**Front:**
**Scenario**: "Resource Contention" - Functional manager pulls your expert.

**Back:**
**Negotiate**. If that fails, Escalate. This is a Resource Issue.

### Card 37
**Front:**
**Scenario**: Team morale is low. Velocity is dropping.

**Back:**
This is an Issue (People). Conduct a **Retrospective** or **Team Assessment** to find the cause.

### Card 38
**Front:**
**Scenario**: The test environment is unstable.

**Back:**
Impediment. Fix the infrastructure. It impacts quality and schedule.

### Card 39
**Front:**
**Scenario**: "Analysis Paralysis" - Team can't decide.

**Back:**
PM must facilitate or **Make the Decision** (if empowered) to unblock progress.

### Card 40
**Front:**
**Scenario**: External dependency (Permit) is delayed.

**Back:**
Impediment. Check the specific Risk Response if planned. If not, update schedule and communicate impact.

### Card 41

**Back:**
**True**. "Clearing the path."

### Card 42
**Front:**

**Back:**
**False**. Be transparent. "Bad news does not improve with age."

### Card 43
**Front:**

**Back:**
**True**.

### Card 44
**Front:**
What is the "Win-Win" conflict technique?

**Back:**
**Collaborate**.

### Card 45
**Front:**
What is the "Lose-Lose" conflict technique?

**Back:**

### Card 46
**Front:**
**Scenario**: "Let's cool off and talk tomorrow."

**Back:**
**Withdraw / Avoid**.

### Card 47
**Front:**
**Scenario**: "We will do it my way."

**Back:**
**Force / Direct**.

### Card 48
**Front:**
**Scenario**: "Your relationship is more important than this issue."

**Back:**
**Smooth / Accommodate**.

### Card 49
**Front:**
Who owns the Issue Log?

**Back:**
The **Project Manager**. (Though team members can edit/update).

### Card 50
**Front:**
Does "Removing Impediments" mean doing the work for the team?

**Back:**
**No**. It means removing the obstacle so *they* can do the work (e.g., buying a tool, getting approval).

## üß† Final Exam Tips

### Card 51
**Front:**
**Exam Tip**: Team is arguing about a technical detail.

**Back:**
**Collaborate**. Let the experts decide. Do not "Force" a technical decision unless you are the expert (unlikely).

### Card 52
**Front:**
**Exam Tip**: Blocker surfaces in Daily Scrum.

**Back:**
Scrum Master notes it. Discusses it **After** the meeting (Parking Lot). Do not solve during the 15 min.

### Card 53
**Front:**
**Exam Tip**: Stakeholder resistance is an issue.

**Back:**
Meet with them. Understand their **Interests** (not just positions).

### Card 54
**Front:**
**Exam Tip**: "Fail Fast"?

**Back:**
Finding issues early (testing) is cheaper than fixing them later.

### Card 55
**Front:**
**Exam Tip**: PM's primary role in Agile execution?

**Back:**
Removing impediments (Servant Leadership).

### Card 56
**Front:**
**Exam Tip**: When to escalate?

**Back:**
Only when the issue is beyond your control/authority or resources. Try to solve at the lowest level first.

### Card 57
**Front:**
**Exam Tip**: "Root Cause Analysis" tool?

**Back:**
Fishbone Diagram (Ishikawa) or 5 Whys.

### Card 58
**Front:**
**Exam Tip**: "Pareto Chart"?

**Back:**
80/20 Rule. Focus on the 20% of causes creating 80% of the issues.

### Card 59
**Front:**
**Exam Tip**: "Trend Analysis"?

**Back:**
Are issues increasing or decreasing? Use to forecast.

### Card 60
**Front:**
**Exam Tip**: A solved issue helps the organization how?

**Back:**
By becoming a **Lesson Learned** (Asset) to prevent it on future projects.
