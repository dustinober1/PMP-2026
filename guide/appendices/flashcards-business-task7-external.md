## üåç PESTLE Framework

### Card 1
**Front:**
What does the acronym **PESTLE** stand for?

**Back:**
**Political, Economic, Social, Technological, Legal, Environmental**.

### Card 2
**Front:**
**Scenario**: New trade tariffs increase the cost of your steel. Which PESTLE category is this?

**Back:**
**Political** (or Economic depending on the nuance, but tariffs are often political/legal).

### Card 3
**Front:**
**Scenario**: Inflation raises labor rates by 10%. Which PESTLE category is this?

**Back:**
**Economic**.

### Card 4
**Front:**
**Scenario**: Customers now demand "Sustainable Packaging" due to cultural shifts. Which PESTLE category is this?

**Back:**
**Social**.

### Card 5
**Front:**
**Scenario**: A competitor releases a new AI tool that makes your product obsolete. Which PESTLE category is this?

**Back:**
**Technological**.

### Card 6
**Front:**
**Scenario**: GDPR regulations change data privacy requirements. Which PESTLE category is this?

**Back:**
**Legal**.

### Card 7
**Front:**
**Scenario**: New carbon emission reporting standards are enforced. Which PESTLE category is this?

**Back:**
**Environmental**.

### Card 8
**Front:**
Why should a Project Manager perform **Environmental Scanning**?

**Back:**
To identify external threats and opportunities early, before they become crises that derail the project.

### Card 9
**Front:**
**Scenario**: You identify a new regulation that affects your project. What is the **First Action**?

**Back:**
**Assess the Impact** (Scope, Schedule, Cost). Do not just implement it immediately without analysis.

### Card 10
**Front:**
**Scenario**: A new regulation makes your current project scope illegal/non-compliant.

**Back:**
This is a **Stop-Work Trigger**. Assess immediately, Notify Sponsor, and likely initiate a Change Request to comply (or cancel project).

## üîÑ Impact Assessment

### Card 11
**Front:**
What are the steps in an **Impact Assessment** for external changes?

**Back:**
Identify ‚Üí Evaluate ‚Üí Escalate (if needed) ‚Üí Respond ‚Üí Update Plans.

### Card 12
**Front:**
**Scenario**: A change in the external environment affects the **Business Case** (viability).

**Back:**
**Escalate** to the Sponsor or Steering Committee. The project may need to be cancelled or pivoted.

### Card 13
**Front:**
**Scenario**: "Target Market Drift"?

**Back:**
When user needs change during a long project. Use **Agile** or iterative cycles to adapt to these Social/Market changes.

### Card 14
**Front:**
**Scenario**: Detailed Supply Chain analysis is required due to what external factor?

**Back:**
Could be **Economic** (stability), **Political** (trade wars), or **Environmental** (sustainability).

### Card 15
**Front:**
**Scenario**: "Pivot"?

**Back:**
Changing the project's direction or goals to deliver value in response to a major external change (e.g., market shift).

### Card 16
**Front:**
Who is responsible for monitoring the external environment?

**Back:**
The **Project Manager** (often with help from the Sponsor, Legal, or PMO).

### Card 17
**Front:**
**Scenario**: A regulatory change is "Proposed" but not passed.

**Back:**
**Monitor** it. Create a contingency plan. Do not execute the change until it is mandated (unless there is strategic advantage).

### Card 18
**Front:**
**Scenario**: The currency exchange rate fluctuates wildly.

**Back:**
**Economic Risk**. Use "Transfer" (hedging) or "Mitigate" (reserves) strategies.

### Card 19
**Front:**
What is **Regulatory Compliance**?

**Back:**
Ensuring the project and product adhere to laws, regulations, and standards (e.g., HIPAA, OSHA).

### Card 20
**Front:**
**Scenario**: "Technological Obsolescence"?

**Back:**
The risk that the technology you are building on becomes outdated before the project finishes. Mitigate with shorter release cycles.

## üß™ Compliance & Standards

### Card 21
**Front:**
What is the difference between a **Regulation** and a **Standard**?

**Back:**
-   **Regulation**: Mandatory law (Government).
-   **Standard**: Best practice guideline (Industry), often voluntary unless written into a contract.

### Card 22
**Front:**
**Scenario**: Your project spans multiple countries. Which laws apply?

**Back:**
**All of them**. You must comply with regulations in every jurisdiction where you operate/sell.

### Card 23
**Front:**
**Scenario**: "GDPR"?

**Back:**
General Data Protection Regulation (EU). Affects data privacy and user rights. Huge impact on software projects.

### Card 24
**Front:**
**Scenario**: "OSHA"?

**Back:**
Occupational Safety and Health Administration (USA). Affects construction/safety projects.

### Card 25
**Front:**
What is **ESG** in the context of external environment?

**Back:**
**Environmental, Social, and Governance**. Investors and regulators increasingly demand compliance with ESG criteria.

### Card 26
**Front:**
**Scenario**: A new law passes with an effective date in 6 months.

**Back:**
Add the compliance work to the **Backlog** or **Schedule** to ensure readiness by the date.

### Card 27
**Front:**
**Scenario**: Non-compliance penalty is cheaper than compliance cost.

**Back:**
**Ethical Trap**. PMP Code of Ethics requires following the law. Do not choose to break the law to save money.

### Card 28
**Front:**
**Scenario**: "Force Majeure"?

**Back:**
Unforeseeable external circumstances (Act of God) that prevent fulfilling a contract (e.g., Pandemic, Natural Disaster).

### Card 29
**Front:**
How does **Agile** help with external changes?

**Back:**
Short iterations allow the team to adapt quickly to new info/regulations rather than being locked into a long-term plan.

### Card 30
**Front:**
**Scenario**: "Teaming Agreement" or "Joint Venture"?

**Back:**
Strategic responses to external opportunities or threats (Sharing the risk/reward).

### Card 31
**Front:**
**True or False**: Environmental scanning happens only at project initiation.

**Back:**
**False**. It is continuous.

### Card 32
**Front:**
**True or False**: The PM can ignore a new law if it causes a schedule delay.

**Back:**
**False**. Compliance is usually mandatory (a Constraint).

### Card 33
**Front:**
**Exam Tip**: Best tool to analyze the external environment?

**Back:**
**PESTLE Analysis**.

### Card 34
**Front:**
**Exam Tip**: Best tool to analyze Internal Strengths/Weaknesses vs External Opps/Threats?

**Back:**
**SWOT Analysis**.

### Card 35
**Front:**
**Exam Tip**: If a new regulation affects the backlog...

**Back:**
The **Product Owner** (with the Team) reprioritizes the backlog to include the compliance items.

### Card 36
**Front:**
**Exam Tip**: "Enterprise Environmental Factors (EEF)"?

**Back:**
Conditions not under the control of the team (e.g., Market conditions, Regulations). PESTLE analyzes these.

### Card 37
**Front:**
**Exam Tip**: Who approves a change to the Business Case triggered by external factors?

**Back:**
**Sponsor** or Steering Committee.

### Card 38
**Front:**
**Scenario**: "Geopolitical Risk"?

**Back:**
Instability in a region affecting supply chain or safety.

### Card 39
**Front:**
**Scenario**: "Data Sovereignty"?

**Back:**
Legal requirement that data gathered in a country is subject to the laws of that country (and often must stay there).

### Card 40
**Front:**
**Scenario**: "Accessibility Standards" (e.g., WCAG)?

**Back:**
Legal/Social requirement to ensure products are usable by people with disabilities.
