## ðŸŽ¯ Quality Fundamentals

### Card 1
**Front:**
What is the difference between **Quality** and **Grade**?

**Back:**
-   **Quality**: The degree to which a set of inherent characteristics fulfills requirements (No defects).
-   **Grade**: A category assigned to deliverables having the same functional use but different technical characteristics (Features/Luxury).
-   *Low Grade is acceptable; Low Quality is not.*

### Card 2
**Front:**
What is the difference between **prevention** and **inspection**?

**Back:**
-   **Prevention**: Keeping errors out of the process (Quality Assurance).
-   **Inspection**: Keeping errors out of the hands of the customer (Quality Control).
-   *Prevention is preferred over inspection.*

### Card 3
**Front:**
What is **Kaizen**?

**Back:**
A Japanese term for "continuous improvement." It implies small, ongoing changes to improve efficiency and quality.

### Card 4
**Front:**
What is **Total Quality Management (TQM)**?

**Back:**
A management approach where **everyone** in the organization (from CEO to janitor) is responsible for quality.

### Card 5
**Front:**
Who is **W. Edwards Deming**?

**Back:**
A quality guru known for the PDCA cycle (Plan-Do-Check-Act) and the 14 Points for Management. He emphasized that 85% of quality problems are management/process problems.

### Card 6
**Front:**
Who is **Joseph Juran**?

**Back:**
A quality guru known for the **Pareto Principle** (80/20 rule) and defining quality as **"Fitness for Use"** (meeting customer needs).

### Card 7
**Front:**
Who is **Philip Crosby**?

**Back:**
A quality guru known for the concepts of **"Zero Defects"** and "Quality is Free" (meaning the cost of non-conformance is higher than the cost of getting it right).

### Card 8
**Front:**
What is the difference between **Precision** and **Accuracy**?

**Back:**
-   **Precision**: Consistency; data points are close to each other (e.g., arrows all hitting the same spot, even if it's the wrong spot).
-   **Accuracy**: Correctness; data points are close to the true value (e.g., arrows hitting the bullseye).

### Card 9
**Front:**
What is **Gold Plating**?

**Back:**
Giving the customer more than they asked for (extra features). It is **bad practice** in PMI because it wastes resources and adds risk.

### Card 10
**Front:**
What is **Six Sigma**?

**Back:**
A methodology to reduce defects to 3.4 defects per million opportunities. It focuses on reducing **variation**.

## ðŸ—ï¸ Quality Processes (QA vs QC)

### Card 11
**Front:**
What is the main focus of **Plan Quality Management**?

**Back:**
Identifying which quality standards are relevant to the project and determining how to satisfy them. (Output: Quality Management Plan).

### Card 12
**Front:**
What is the main focus of **Manage Quality** (Quality Assurance)?

**Back:**
Translating the quality plan into executable quality activities. It focuses on the **Process** to prevent defects. (Audits, Design for X).

### Card 13
**Front:**
What is the main focus of **Control Quality** (Quality Control)?

**Back:**
Monitoring specific project results to determine if they comply with relevant quality standards. It focuses on the **Product/Deliverables** to find defects. (Inspections, Testing).

### Card 14
**Front:**
**Scenario**: You are auditing the team's code review process to see if it is effective. Are you doing QA or QC?

**Back:**
**Manage Quality (QA)**. You are checking the *process*, not the code itself.

### Card 15
**Front:**
**Scenario**: You are testing the software module to see if it bugs out. Are you doing QA or QC?

**Back:**
**Control Quality (QC)**. You are checking the *product* (deliverable).

### Card 16
**Front:**
What is a **Quality Audit**?

**Back:**
A structured, independent process to determine if project activities comply with organizational and project policies, processes, and procedures. (Part of Manage Quality).

### Card 17
**Front:**
What is **Design for X** (DfX)?

**Back:**
A set of technical guidelines that may be applied during the design of a product for the optimization of a specific aspect of the design (e.g., Design for Assembly, Design for Safety).

### Card 18
**Front:**
What is a **Quality Checklist**?

**Back:**
A structured tool used to verify that a set of required steps has been performed. (Used in Control Quality).

### Card 19
**Front:**
What is **Attribute Sampling**?

**Back:**
The result is binary: Conforming or Nonconforming (Pass/Fail).

### Card 20
**Front:**
What is **Variable Sampling**?

**Back:**
The result is rated on a continuous scale that measures the degree of conformity (e.g., the temperature is 98.6 degrees).

## ðŸ’° Cost of Quality (CoQ)

### Card 21
**Front:**
What are the two main categories of **Cost of Quality**?

**Back:**
1.  **Cost of Conformance** (Money spent to avoid failures).
2.  **Cost of Nonconformance** (Money spent because of failures).

### Card 22
**Front:**
What are examples of **Prevention Costs**?

**Back:**
Training, documenting processes, equipment, time to do it right. (Conformance).

### Card 23
**Front:**
What are examples of **Appraisal Costs**?

**Back:**
Testing, destructive testing loss, inspections. (Conformance).

### Card 24
**Front:**
What are examples of **Internal Failure Costs**?

**Back:**
Rework, scrap. (Nonconformance defined *before* the customer sees it).

### Card 25
**Front:**
What are examples of **External Failure Costs**?

**Back:**
Liabilities, warranty work, lost business, lawsuits. (Nonconformance defined *after* the customer sees it).

### Card 26
**Front:**
Which Cost of Quality category is the most expensive?

**Back:**
**External Failure Costs**. (Damage to reputation is often immeasurable).

### Card 27
**Front:**
**Scenario**: investing $10k in training saves $50k in rework. Is this a good investment?

**Back:**
**Yes**. Prevention costs are almost always lower than failure costs.

### Card 28
**Front:**
Is **Destructive Testing** a cost of conformance or non-conformance?

**Back:**
**Conformance** (Appraisal Cost). You planned to destroy the unit to prove it works (e.g., crash testing a car).

### Card 29
**Front:**
What is the **Optimal Cost of Quality**?

**Back:**
The balance point where investing more in prevention/appraisal no longer yields a significant reduction in failure costs (Law of Diminishing Returns).

### Card 30
**Front:**
Does Agile reduce Cost of Quality?

**Back:**
Typically **Yes**, by finding defects early (Shift Left) through TDD and CI/CD, reducing the cost of rework.

## ðŸ› ï¸ The 7 Basic Quality Tools

### Card 31
**Front:**
What is a **Cause-and-Effect Diagram** (aka Fishbone or Ishikawa)?

**Back:**
A tool that traces a problem's statement to its root cause using categories like Man, Machine, Material, Method, Mother Nature (Environment), and Measurement.

### Card 32
**Front:**
What is a **Flowchart** (Quality)?

**Back:**
A graphical representation of a process showing relationships among process steps. Helps identify where quality problems might occur.

### Card 33
**Front:**
What is a **Check Sheet** (Tally Sheet)?

**Back:**
A structured form for collecting and analyzing data (e.g., counting how many times a specific defect happens).

### Card 34
**Front:**
What is a **Pareto Diagram**?

**Back:**
A vertical bar chart used to identify the vital few sources that are responsible for the most effects. Based on the 80/20 rule.

### Card 35
**Front:**
What is a **Histogram**?

**Back:**
A bar chart that describes the central tendency, dispersion, and shape of a statistical distribution. (Does not rank causes like Pareto).

### Card 36
**Front:**
What is a **Control Chart**?

**Back:**
A graphic display of process data over time and against control limits, used to determine if a process is "in control."

### Card 37
**Front:**
What is the **Rule of Seven** in Control Charts?

**Back:**
If **seven consecutive data points** fall on one side of the mean, the process is considered "out of control" and requires investigation (Assignal Cause), even if all points are within the control limits.

### Card 38
**Front:**
What is a **Scatter Diagram**?

**Back:**
A plot of ordered pairs (X, Y) showing the relationship between two variables (Correlation).

### Card 39
**Front:**
**Scenario**: You want to focus your limited resources on fixing the defects that occur most frequently. Which tool do you use?

**Back:**
**Pareto Chart**. (Focus on the "Vital Few").

### Card 40
**Front:**
**Scenario**: You want to see if your manufacturing process is stable over time or if it's drifting. Which tool do you use?

**Back:**
**Control Chart**.

## ðŸ”¬ Advanced Quality Tools

### Card 41
**Front:**
What is **Design of Experiments (DOE)**?

**Back:**
A statistical method for identifying which factors may influence specific variables of a product or process. It changes multiple factors at once to see their combined effect.

### Card 42
**Front:**
What is **Statistical Sampling**?

**Back:**
Choosing part of a population of interest for inspection. Used when testing the whole population is too expensive, time-consuming, or destructive.

### Card 43
**Front:**
What is **Benchmarking**?

**Back:**
Comparing actual or planned project practices to those of comparable projects (internal or external) to identify best practices.

### Card 44
**Front:**
What is **Root Cause Analysis (RCA)**?

**Back:**
An analytical technique used to determine the basic underlying reason that causes a variance or defect or risk.

### Card 45
**Front:**
What is an **Affinity Diagram**?

**Back:**
A technique that allows large numbers of ideas to be classified into groups for review and analysis. (Grouping sticky notes on a wall).

### Card 46
**Front:**
What are **Control Limits**?

**Back:**
The area composed of three standard deviations on either side of the centerline (mean) of a normal distribution. They are calculated statistically.

### Card 47
**Front:**
What are **Specification Limits**?

**Back:**
The limits defined by the **Customer** (requirements). They may be wider or narrower than control limits.

### Card 48
**Front:**
**Scenario**: A process is "In Control" but produces products outside "Specification Limits." What does this mean?

**Back:**
The process is stable/predictable, but it is **not capable** of meeting customer needs. You need to re-engineer the process (Manage Quality).

### Card 49
**Front:**
What is a **SIPOC** model?

**Back:**
Suppliers, Inputs, Process, Outputs, Customers. A tool used in Six Sigma to map a process at a high level.

### Card 50
**Front:**
What is **PDCA**?

**Back:**
Plan-Do-Check-Act. The cycle of continuous improvement defined by Shewhart and popularized by Deming.

## âš¡ Quick-Fire Quality Review

### Card 51
**Front:**
**True or False**: Quality is planned, not inspected in.

**Back:**
**True**. You cannot inspect quality into a product; it must be built in.

### Card 52
**Front:**
**True or False**: The Project Manager is responsible for the quality of the project.

**Back:**
**True**. While everyone has a role, the PM holds ultimate accountability for the quality management plan.

### Card 53
**Front:**
**True or False**: Upper and Lower Control Limits are set by the Customer.

**Back:**
**False**. Control Limits are statistical (process capability). Specification Limits are set by the Customer.

### Card 54
**Front:**
**True or False**: A datapoint outside the control limits indicates a "Common Cause" variation.

**Back:**
**False**. It indicates a "**Special Cause**" variation (something unusual happened).

### Card 55
**Front:**
What is **Mutually Exclusive**?

**Back:**
Two events that cannot happen at the same time (e.g., flipping a coin leads to heads or tails).

### Card 56
**Front:**
What is **Normal Distribution**?

**Back:**
A bell curve. (68% of data within 1 sigma, 95% within 2 sigma, 99.7% within 3 sigma).

### Card 57
**Front:**
What is **Just-in-Time (JIT)**?

**Back:**
An inventory management method that brings inventory down to zero (or near zero). It forces quality issues to surface immediately because there is no buffer stock to hide bad parts.

### Card 58
**Front:**
What are **Mind Maps**?

**Back:**
Diagrams used to visually organize information. In Quality, they can link requirements to quality metrics.

### Card 59
**Front:**
What is **Brainstorming**?

**Back:**
A data-gathering technique used to identify a list of ideas in a short period of time.

### Card 60
**Front:**
What is **Nominal Group Technique**?

**Back:**
Brainstorming with a voting process to rank the most useful ideas.

## ðŸ† Agile Quality

### Card 61
**Front:**
What is **Test-Driven Development (TDD)**?

**Back:**
Writing the test *before* writing the code. Ensures the code meets the requirement and nothing more.

### Card 62
**Front:**
What is **Refactoring**?

**Back:**
Improving the internal structure of code without changing its external behavior. (Paying down technical debt).

### Card 63
**Front:**
What is **Continuous Integration (CI)**?

**Back:**
Developers merge their changes back to the main branch as often as possible (daily). Automated tests run to detect defects immediately.

### Card 64
**Front:**
How does a **Retrospective** support Quality?

**Back:**
It is a "Manage Quality" (process improvement) event. The team discusses what went wrong and how to improve the process for the next Sprint.

### Card 65
**Front:**
What is the **Definition of Done (DoD)**?

**Back:**
A checklist of quality criteria that every User Story must meet to be considered complete.

### Card 66
**Front:**
Who approves the DoD?

**Back:**
The Team and the Product Owner agree on it.

### Card 67
**Front:**
What is **Technical Debt**?

**Back:**
The implied cost of additional rework caused by choosing an easy solution now instead of using a better approach that would take longer.

### Card 68
**Front:**
What is **Pair Programming**?

**Back:**
Two developers working at one workstation. One writes code (Driver), the other reviews it in real-time (Navigator). It is a continuous **Inspection** technique.

### Card 69
**Front:**
What is **Acceptance Test-Driven Development (ATDD)**?

**Back:**
Writing acceptance tests (from the user's perspective) before coding begins. Collaborative effort between PO, Dev, and QA.

### Card 70
**Front:**
What is **Spike**?

**Back:**
A short, time-boxed research task in Agile to learn about a new technology or reduce risk (often related to quality/feasibility).

## ðŸš¦ Closing Quality

### Card 71
**Front:**
What is the output of Control Quality that goes into Validate Scope?

**Back:**
**Verified Deliverables**. (Internal QC checks them first; then the Customer validates them).

### Card 72
**Front:**
What happens if the customer finds a defect during User Acceptance Testing (UAT)?

**Back:**
The deliverable is rejected. A Change Request (Defect Repair) is logged. The team fixes it, and it goes back through Control Quality.

### Card 73
**Front:**
What defines the "Standard of Quality"?

**Back:**
The **Project Management Plan** (specifically the Quality Management Plan) and the Requirements Documentation.

### Card 74
**Front:**
**Scenario**: A team member says "Let's skip the code review to meet the deadline." What should the PM do?

**Back:**
**Refuse**. Skipping quality checks typically leads to higher costs (rework) and missed deadlines later. (Grade can be cut; Quality cannot).

### Card 75
**Front:**
What is **Standard Deviation**?

**Back:**
A measure of how spread out the numbers in a distribution are. (Sigma).

### Card 76
**Front:**
What is **Common Cause Variation**?

**Back:**
Normal, expected variation inherent in the process (noise). (e.g., commute takes 20-25 mins).

### Card 77
**Front:**
What is **Special Cause Variation**?

**Back:**
Unexpected variation due to a specific event. (e.g., commute takes 2 hours because of a flat tire).

### Card 78
**Front:**
What is **Problem Solving**?

**Back:**
A structured element of Quality Assurance. Steps: Define -> Root Cause -> Generate Solutions -> Choose -> Implement -> Verify.

### Card 79
**Front:**
What is **Force Field Analysis**?

**Back:**
Analysis of forces for and against a change. Used in quality improvement to understand barriers to better processes.

### Card 80
**Front:**
What is the core message of **Modern Quality Management**?

**Back:**
Customer Satisfaction and Continuous Improvement.
