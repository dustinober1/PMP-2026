## Defining Knowledge Transfer & Closure

### Card 1
**Front**: What is the primary goal of "Project Closure" in the PMP 2026 mindset?
**Back**: Not just ending work, but **Transferring Value** (to Operations/Users) and **Transferring Knowledge** (to the Organization).
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Help ensure knowledge transfer

### Card 2
**Front**: When does "Knowledge Transfer" effectively begin?
**Back**: Throughout the project lifecycle (via reviews, documentation), but it is *finalized* at Closure.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Help ensure knowledge transfer

### Card 3
**Front**: What defines a project as "Closed"?
**Back**: 1) Deliverables accepted, 2) Contracts closed, 3) Records archived (OPAs), 4) Resources released.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Help ensure knowledge transfer

### Card 4
**Front**: Who is responsible for ensuring knowledge transfer occurs?
**Back**: The **Project Manager**. It is a mandatory part of the Project Integration Management.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Help ensure knowledge transfer

### Card 5
**Front**: What is the "Final Report"?
**Back**: The formal document summarizing project performance (Objectives met, Schedule/Cost actuals, Lessons Learned) for stakeholders.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Help ensure knowledge transfer

## Lessons Learned (The Knowledge Engine)

### Card 6
**Front**: What is a "Lessons Learned Register"?
**Back**: A document capturing what went well, what went wrong, and recommendations for future projects.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Help ensure knowledge transfer

### Card 7
**Front**: What happens to the Lessons Learned Register at the end of the project?
**Back**: It is transferred to the **Organizational Process Assets (OPA)** library for reuse by the whole company.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Help ensure knowledge transfer

### Card 8
**Front**: What is the difference between a "Retrospective" and "Lessons Learned"?
**Back**: Retrospectives are for the *Team* (Process improvement *during* the project). Lessons Learned are for the *Organization* (Future projects).
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Help ensure knowledge transfer

### Card 9
**Front**: A lesson learned should always be...
**Back**: **Actionable** and **Blameless**. Focus on the process, not the person.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Help ensure knowledge transfer

### Card 10
**Front**: When should Lessons Learned be captured?
**Back**: Throughout the project (after every phase/milestone), so details haven't faded from memory at the end.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Help ensure knowledge transfer

### Card 11
**Front**: What is "Explicit Knowledge"?
**Back**: Knowledge that can be easily documented (manuals, specs).
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Help ensure knowledge transfer

### Card 12
**Front**: What is "Tacit Knowledge"?
**Back**: Knowledge difficult to document (beliefs, insights, "muscle memory"). Requires shadowing or mentoring to transfer.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Help ensure knowledge transfer

**Domain**: People | **Task**: Help ensure knowledge transfer

## Scenarios: Canceled Projects

### Card 28
**Front**: Scenario: The project is canceled due to budget cuts. Do you just stop working?
**Back**: No. You must perform **Administrative Closure**. (Archive work, Pay vendors, Document reason).
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Help ensure knowledge transfer

### Card 29
**Front**: Scenario: A project is canceled because the market changed. Is this a "failure"?
**Back**: Not necessarily. It is a strategic decision to save resources. "Sunk Cost" should not drive continuation.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Help ensure knowledge transfer

### Card 30
**Front**: What is the most important output of a Canceled Project?
**Back**: **Lessons Learned** (Why was it canceled?) and Archived Partial Deliverables (Can we reuse this code?).
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Help ensure knowledge transfer

### Card 31
**Front**: How do you handle the Team's disappointment in a canceled project?
**Back**: Be transparent about the business reason. Focus on what was achieved/learned. Protect their morale for the next project.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Help ensure knowledge transfer

## Scenarios: Acceptance & Sign-off

### Card 32
**Front**: Scenario: The customer refuses to sign off because they "don't like the color," but the color meets the specific requirement.
**Back**: Refer to the **Acceptance Criteria**. If it meets spec, it is done. Negotiate or treat the color change as a new Project/Change Request.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Help ensure knowledge transfer

### Card 33
**Front**: Scenario: The customer finds a "Major" bug during User Acceptance Testing (UAT).
**Back**: Log it. Fix it (Defect Repair). You cannot close until meaningful acceptance criteria are met.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Help ensure knowledge transfer

### Card 34
**Front**: Scenario: The customer finds a "Minor" typo during final sign-off.
**Back**: Create a **Punch List**. Get "Conditional Acceptance" agreeing these minor items will be fixed in the warranty period.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Help ensure knowledge transfer

### Card 35
**Front**: Scenario: You are closing a "Phase" (Phase 1). What happens to the Lessons Learned?
**Back**: They are captured now and used to improve Phase 2 immediately.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Help ensure knowledge transfer

### Card 42

### Card 43

**Domain**: People | **Task**: Help ensure knowledge transfer

### Card 44
**Front**: Sequence: Handover to Ops vs Final Report. Which comes first?
**Back**: Usually **Handover**. The Final Report confirms that handover was successful.

## Exam "Traps" to Avoid

### Card 49
**Front**: Trap: "The project ran out of money so we stopped." Is it closed?
**Back**: Not until you do the paperwork (Administrative Closure). Otherwise it is "Abandoned," which is bad practice.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Help ensure knowledge transfer

### Card 50
**Front**: Trap: "Everyone is busy, let's skip the final meeting."
**Back**: Closure is mandatory. If you skip it, you never formally transfer ownership/liability.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Help ensure knowledge transfer

### Card 51
**Front**: Trap: "PM writes the Lessons Learned alone to save time."
**Back**: Wrong. Lessons Learned must be a team effort to capture diverse perspectives (Dev, QA, Business).
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Help ensure knowledge transfer

### Card 52
**Front**: Trap: "The final product has a bug, but we are out of time. Just close it."
**Back**: Unethical. You must either fix it or get the customer to sign off on the "Known Issue" (Accepted Defect).
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Help ensure knowledge transfer

## Agile Closure Nuances

### Card 61
**Front**: Does an Agile project have a "Closure Phase"?
**Back**: Not exactly a phase, but a "Closure Activity." When the backlog is empty or value is maximized, the team performs closure steps (Retrospective, Archive).
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Help ensure knowledge transfer

### Card 62
**Front**: In Agile, "Knowledge Transfer" happens...
**Back**: ...Continuously via Pair Programming, Daily Standups, and Reviews. It is less dependent on "Final Docs."
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Help ensure knowledge transfer

### Card 63
**Front**: What is the "Definition of Done" role in closure?
**Back**: It ensures that every increment was potentially shippable, making final closure easier (no huge "integration/fix" phase at the end).
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Help ensure knowledge transfer

## Advanced Scenarios II

### Card 64
**Front**: Scenario: A vendor claims they did extra work 6 months ago and wants payment now (at closure).
**Back**: Check the Change Log. If no Change Order exists, the claim is likely invalid. "Constructive Changes" must be documented when they happen.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Help ensure knowledge transfer

### Card 65
**Front**: Scenario: You find a box of confidential physical files during office cleanup.
**Back**: Compliance issue. Destroy or Archive them according to the document retention policy. Do not throw in trash.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Help ensure knowledge transfer

### Card 66
**Front**: Scenario: The Sponsor has left the company right before sign-off.
**Back**: Identify the *new* Sponsor or someone with delegated authority immediately. You cannot close without a signature.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Help ensure knowledge transfer

### Card 67
**Front**: Scenario: The team wants to keep the test servers running "just in case."
**Back**: Cost waste. Decommissioning resources (hardware/software) is part of closure to stop the billing meter.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Help ensure knowledge transfer

### Card 68
**Front**: Scenario: A stakeholder wants to "Wait and see" before signing off.
**Back**: Negotiation needed. Define a specific "Observation Period" (e.g., 2 weeks in Prod) after which acceptance is automatic if no critical bugs found.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Help ensure knowledge transfer
