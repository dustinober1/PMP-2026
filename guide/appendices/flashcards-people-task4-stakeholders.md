## Stakeholder Identification & Analysis

### Card 1
**Front**: What is the "Snowball Technique" for stakeholder identification?
**Back**: Asking every identified stakeholder: "Who else should I be talking to?" to uncover shadow stakeholders.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Engage stakeholders

### Card 2
**Front**: When does Stakeholder Analysis happen?
**Back**: It is a continuous cycle throughout the project, not just a one-time initiation activity.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Engage stakeholders

### Card 3
**Front**: What is a "Power/Interest Grid"?
**Back**: A tool to classify stakeholders based on their authority (Power) and level of concern (Interest) to determine engagement strategy.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Engage stakeholders

### Card 4
**Front**: How should you treat a stakeholder with **High Power** and **High Interest**?
**Back**: Manage Closely. They are key players who must be fully engaged and satisfied.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Engage stakeholders

### Card 5
**Front**: How should you treat a stakeholder with **High Power** and **Low Interest**?
**Back**: Keep Satisfied. Meet their needs but don't over-communicate. Watch out for them becoming resistant.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Engage stakeholders

### Card 6
**Front**: How should you treat a stakeholder with **Low Power** and **High Interest**?
**Back**: Keep Informed. They can be valuable allies or sources of noise.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Engage stakeholders

### Card 7
**Front**: How should you treat a stakeholder with **Low Power** and **Low Interest**?
**Back**: Monitor. Spend minimal effort but check in periodically.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Engage stakeholders

### Card 8
**Front**: What is the **Salience Model**?
**Back**: Classifies stakeholders based on Power, Urgency, and Legitimacy. Used for complex stakeholder environments.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Engage stakeholders

### Card 9
**Front**: What are "Shadow Stakeholders"?
**Back**: People who have informal influence but do not appear on the official org chart. You must identify them to avoid surprises.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Engage stakeholders

### Card 10
**Front**: What is the primary output of the Identity Stakeholders process?
**Back**: The Stakeholder Register.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Engage stakeholders

## Stakeholder Register & Plans

### Card 11
**Front**: What information goes into the **Stakeholder Register**?
**Back**: Identification info (Name, Role), Assessment info (Requirements, Expectations, Influence), and Classification (Internal/External, Supporter/Resistor).
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Engage stakeholders

### Card 12
**Front**: What is the **Stakeholder Engagement Plan**?
**Back**: A strategy document detailing *how* you will move stakeholders from their current engagement level to the desired level.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Engage stakeholders

### Card 13
**Front**: What is the **Stakeholder Engagement Assessment Matrix**?
**Back**: A table comparing Current (C) vs. Desired (D) engagement levels (Unaware, Resistant, Neutral, Supportive, Leading).
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Engage stakeholders

### Card 14
**Front**: If a stakeholder's attitude changes from Supportive to Resistant, what is your first action?
**Back**: Update the Stakeholder Register and Re-analyze the root cause. (Do not ignore it).
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Engage stakeholders

### Card 15
**Front**: What is the difference between the **Stakeholder Register** and the **Stakeholder Engagement Plan**?
**Back**: Register = "Who they are" (List). Plan = "What we will do" (Strategy).
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Engage stakeholders

## RACI Matrix

### Card 16
**Front**: What does **RACI** stand for?
**Back**: Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, Informed.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Engage stakeholders

### Card 17
**Front**: In a RACI matrix, who is "**Responsible**"?
**Back**: The person who does the work.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Engage stakeholders

### Card 18
**Front**: In a RACI matrix, who is "**Accountable**"?
**Back**: The "one throat to choke." The person with final decision authority. (Only 1 per task).
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Engage stakeholders

### Card 19
**Front**: In a RACI matrix, who is "**Consulted**"?
**Back**: SMEs or stakeholders who provide input *before* a decision is made. Two-way communication.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Engage stakeholders

### Card 20
**Front**: In a RACI matrix, who is "**Informed**"?
**Back**: Stakeholders who need to know *after* a decision is made. One-way communication.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Engage stakeholders

### Card 21
**Front**: What is a common error in RACI assignments?
**Back**: Assigning more than one "Accountable" person (causes confusion) or having no "Accountable" person (no ownership).
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Engage stakeholders

## Engagement Strategy & Influence

### Card 22
**Front**: What is "Reciprocity" in Cialdini's influence principles?
**Back**: People feel obliged to give back if you have given to them first. (Give value to build influence).
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Engage stakeholders

### Card 23
**Front**: What is "Social Proof"?
**Back**: People follow the lead of others similar to them. "Peer pressure" used positively.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Engage stakeholders

### Card 24
**Front**: What is the **Trust Equation**?
**Back**: Trust = (Credibility + Reliability + Intimacy) / Self-Orientation.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Engage stakeholders

### Card 25
**Front**: High "Self-Orientation" generally does what to trust?
**Back**: Destroys it. If people think you only care about yourself, they won't trust you even if you are competent.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Engage stakeholders

### Card 26
**Front**: What is the best way to handle a **Resistant** stakeholder?
**Back**: Find out "Why" (WIIFM). Meet privately using Active Listening to understand their fear or concern.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Engage stakeholders

### Card 27
**Front**: What is "Radical Transparency"?
**Back**: Sharing bad news as quickly as good news. It builds credibility and trust.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Engage stakeholders

### Card 28
**Front**: If you lack formal authority, what type of power is most effective?
**Back**: Expert Power or Referent Power (Relationship/Influence).
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Engage stakeholders

### Card 29
**Front**: What is a "Champion" stakeholder?
**Back**: A supportive stakeholder with influence who advocates for the project when you are not in the room.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Engage stakeholders

### Card 30
**Front**: How do you deal with a stakeholder who says "I don't have time"?
**Back**: Make it easy for them. Summarize data, ask specific questions, and show the risk of their non-participation.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Engage stakeholders

## Agile vs. Predictive Engagement

### Card 31
**Front**: In Agile, do stakeholders only attend the beginning and end of the project?
**Back**: No. Engagement is continuous. They attend Sprint Reviews and are invited to collaborate daily if needed.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Engage stakeholders

### Card 32
**Front**: What is the primary stakeholder event in Scrum?
**Back**: The Sprint Review (Demo).
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Engage stakeholders

### Card 33
**Front**: Can stakeholders speak during the Daily Standup?
**Back**: generally No (or only as observers). The meeting is for the Development Team.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Engage stakeholders

### Card 34
**Front**: In Predictive projects, how is feedback typically handled?
**Back**: Through formal Change Requests or phase gate reviews.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Engage stakeholders

### Card 35
**Front**: In Agile, how is feedback handled?
**Back**: Immediate feedback on working software during the Sprint Review, leading to Backlog updates.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Engage stakeholders

### Card 36
**Front**: Who represents the stakeholders' voice in a Scrum team?
**Back**: The Product Owner.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Engage stakeholders

### Card 37
**Front**: What is "Co-creation"?
**Back**: Involving stakeholders in the design process (Agile) rather than just having them approve the final output.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Engage stakeholders

## Communication & Expectations

### Card 38
**Front**: What is the difference between "Communication" and "Engagement"?
**Back**: Communication is sending a message. Engagement is the result (buy-in, support, trust).
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Engage stakeholders

### Card 39
**Front**: What is the #1 rule of Expectation Management?
**Back**: No Surprises.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Engage stakeholders

### Card 40
**Front**: How do you prevent "Expectation Drift"?
**Back**: Document agreements, regular demos to visualize progress, and definition of done.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Engage stakeholders

### Card 41
**Front**: What defines the "What, When, and How" of stakeholder information?
**Back**: The Communications Management Plan.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Engage stakeholders

### Card 42
**Front**: What is "Pull Communication"?
**Back**: Information placed in a central location (e.g., SharePoint, Wiki) for stakeholders to access at their own pace.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Engage stakeholders

### Card 43
**Front**: What is "Push Communication"?
**Back**: Information sent directly to specific recipients (e.g., Email, Reports). Ensures they receive it.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Engage stakeholders

### Card 44
**Front**: What is "Interactive Communication"?
**Back**: Real-time exchange (e.g., Meetings, Calls). Best for consensus building and resolving conflict.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Engage stakeholders

### Card 45
**Front**: If a stakeholder complains about "too many emails," what do you do?
**Back**: Check the Communications Management Plan. Adjust the channel (maybe switch to Pull communication or a weekly summary).
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Engage stakeholders

## Exam Scenarios: Engage Stakeholders

### Card 50
**Front**: Exam Scenario: You are new to a project. What is your first step regarding stakeholders?
**Back**: Review the Project Charter and existing documents to Identify Stakeholders and create the Register.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Engage stakeholders

### Card 51
**Front**: Exam Scenario: A key stakeholder refuses to approve the requirements.
**Back**: Meet privately to understand their concerns (Needs/Expectations). Do not just escalate.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Engage stakeholders

### Card 52
**Front**: Exam Scenario: Stakeholders are arguing in a meeting about direction.
**Back**: Facilitate. Remind them of the Project Vision/Charter. Use a decision-making model.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Engage stakeholders

### Card 53
**Front**: Exam Scenario: You discover a stakeholder midway through execution.
**Back**: Add them to the register immediately. Assess their impact. Update plans. (Do not ignore or blame).
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Engage stakeholders

### Card 54
**Front**: Exam Scenario: A low-power stakeholder is very vocal and negative on social media.
**Back**: Manage them. Even "Low Power" stakeholders can gain influence (social proof). Monitor and engage to neutralize.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Engage stakeholders

### Card 55
**Front**: Exam Scenario: The Sponsor is not responding to your emails.
**Back**: Change the method. Try a phone call or "Interactive" meeting. Don't just keep emailing.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Engage stakeholders

### Card 56
**Front**: Exam Scenario: Team members are bypassing you and talking directly to the client.
**Back**: If Agile: This is good! Encouraged. If Predictive: Check the Comm Plan to see if this violates protocol, but generally encourage direct collaboration if controlled.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Engage stakeholders

### Card 57
**Front**: Exam Scenario: A stakeholder wants a change.
**Back**: Log it. Assess impact. Follow Change Control (or Backlog Refinement).
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Engage stakeholders

### Card 58
**Front**: Exam Scenario: Stakeholder satisfaction is low despite meeting all deadlines.
**Back**: You missed "Expectation Management." You delivered the Output but not the Outcome/Value. Engage to realign.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Engage stakeholders

### Card 59
**Front**: Exam Scenario: Dealing with a "Bureaucratic" stakeholder.
**Back**: Respect their process. Provide the data/compliance they need. Don't fight the structure; navigate it.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Engage stakeholders

### Card 60
**Front**: Exam Scenario: Dealing with a "Visionary" stakeholder who changes their mind often.
**Back**: Use Agile/Iterative approaches. Show demos frequently. Use short cycles to limit the cost of changes.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Engage stakeholders

## Tools & Vocabulary Drill

### Card 61
**Front**: Define "Sender-Receiver Model".
**Back**: Communication loop: Enroll -> Transmit -> Decode -> Acknowledge -> Feedback. Noise can interrupt any step.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Engage stakeholders

### Card 62
**Front**: Define "Noise" in communication.
**Back**: Anything that interferes with the message (Language, culture, distance, technology issues, bias).
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Engage stakeholders

### Card 63
**Front**: Define "Active Listening".
**Back**: Listening to understand, reflecting back what was heard, and confirming meaning (not just waiting to speak).
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Engage stakeholders

### Card 64
**Front**: Define "Stakeholder Cube".
**Back**: A 3D model (Power, Interest, Attitude) used for more complex analysis than a 2D grid.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Engage stakeholders

### Card 65
**Front**: Define "Brainstorming" in identification.
**Back**: A group creativity technique to list all possible stakeholders without judgement.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Engage stakeholders

### Card 66
**Front**: Define "Directions of Influence".
**Back**: Upward (Senior), Downward (Team), Outward (External), Sideward (Peers). You must manage all directions.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Engage stakeholders

### Card 67
**Front**: What is the most effective form of communication for complex problems?
**Back**: Face-to-Face (Interactive).
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Engage stakeholders

### Card 68
**Front**: How much of a PM's time is spent communicating?
**Back**: ~90%.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Engage stakeholders

### Card 69
**Front**: What shows the reporting relationships in the project?
**Back**: The Project Organization Chart.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Engage stakeholders

### Card 70
**Front**: What document contains stakeholder contact details and "sensitive" notes?
**Back**: The Stakeholder Register (Warning: Keep sensitive notes private or separate; the Register is often public to the team).
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Engage stakeholders

## Advanced Scenarios

### Card 71

**Back**: This is a "Resistant" stakeholder. Use "Upward" influence (Sponsor) or "Reciprocity" to align goals.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Engage stakeholders

### Card 72
**Front**: You have a stakeholder who is a "Subject Matter Expert" but has no decision power.
**Back**: Consult them (C in RACI). Use their expertise to build Credibility.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Engage stakeholders

### Card 73
**Front**: Stakeholders are geographically dispersed and speak different languages.
**Back**: Update Comm Plan. Use translation, visuals, and asynchronous tools. Account for time zones.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Engage stakeholders

### Card 74
**Front**: A stakeholder says "I don't trust the data."
**Back**: Involve them in the data collection/creation. "Shared Ownership" builds trust.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Engage stakeholders

### Card 75
**Front**: The project is politically sensitive. How do you manage the Stakeholder Register?
**Back**: Be careful with written classification labels (e.g., "Resistant"). Consider keeping sensitive analysis confidential.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Engage stakeholders

### Card 76
**Front**: What is "Stakeholder Analysis" vs "Stakeholder Engagement"?
**Back**: Analysis = Thinking/Planning. Engagement = Doing/Acting.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Engage stakeholders

### Card 77
**Front**: Why is "Early Involvement" a key principle?
**Back**: People support what they help create. Early involvement reduces resistance later.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Engage stakeholders

### Card 78
**Front**: What is the risk of "Over-Engaging" a stakeholder?
**Back**: Fatigue, annoyance, micromanagement focused on the wrong level.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Engage stakeholders

### Card 79
**Front**: What is the risk of "Under-Engaging"?
**Back**: Surprise resistance, lack of support, missed requirements, project failure.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Engage stakeholders

### Card 80
**Front**: When should you use "Push" communication?
**Back**: When you need to send information to a large audience but don't need immediate feedback (e.g., Newsletters).
