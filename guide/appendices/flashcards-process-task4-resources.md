## ðŸŽ¯ Resource Fundamentals

### Card 1
**Front:**
What are the two main categories of "Resources" in project management?

**Back:**
1.  **Team Resources** (Human: skills, people).
2.  **Physical Resources** (Material: equipment, materials, facilities, infrastructure).

### Card 2
**Front:**
What is a **Resource Breakdown Structure (RBS)**?

**Back:**
A hierarchical representation of resources by category and type. It helps in organizing and reporting on resource costs and utilization.

### Card 3
**Front:**
What is the key output of the **Plan Resource Management** process?

**Back:**
The **Resource Management Plan**. It defines how to categorize, allocate, manage, and release resources.

### Card 4
**Front:**
What is a **Resource Calendar**?

**Back:**
A calendar that identifies the working days, shifts, start/end dates, and holidays for specific resources (people or equipment).

### Card 5
**Front:**
What is the difference between a **RACI Chart** and a **RAM**?

**Back:**
A RACI chart is a specific type of **RAM (Responsibility Assignment Matrix)** that uses the roles Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, and Informed. RAM is the generic term.

### Card 6
**Front:**
In a RACI chart, what does **Accountable (A)** mean?

**Back:**
The person who is ultimately answerable for the correct and thorough completion of the deliverable or task. There must be **only one** "A" per task.

### Card 7
**Front:**
In a RACI chart, what does **Responsible (R)** mean?

**Back:**
The person who actually **does the work**. There can be multiple "R"s for a single task.

### Card 8
**Front:**
What is **Consulted (C)** in RACI?

**Back:**
Subject Matter Experts (SMEs) whose opinions are sought **before** and during the work. It implies two-way communication.

### Card 9
**Front:**
What is **Informed (I)** in RACI?

**Back:**
Stakeholders who are kept up-to-date on progress or completion, often **after** the fact. It implies one-way communication.

### Card 10
**Front:**
What is **Bottom-Up Estimating** for resources?

**Back:**
Estimating team/physical resources at the lowest level of the WBS (Work Packages) and aggregating them up to get the total project resource needs. It is the most accurate but time-consuming method.

## ðŸ“Š Resource Optimization

### Card 11
**Front:**
What is **Resource Leveling**?

**Back:**
A technique to resolve resource conflicts (over-allocations) by adjusting start/end dates. It **often extends the critical path** (changes the project end date).

### Card 12
**Front:**
What is **Resource Smoothing**?

**Back:**
A technique to balance resource demand that adjusts activities only within their **free and total float**. It **does not** change the project end date.

### Card 13
**Front:**
Which technique typically changes the critical path: Resource Leveling or Smoothing?

**Back:**
**Resource Leveling**.

### Card 14
**Front:**
What is the primary constraint in **Resource Smoothing**?

**Back:**
The **Schedule** (Time). You cannot delay the project completion date.

### Card 15
**Front:**
What is the primary constraint in **Resource Leveling**?

**Back:**
The **Resources**. You cannot exceed the available resource limit, so time must slip.

### Card 16
**Front:**
What is a **Resource Histogram**?

**Back:**
A bar chart that shows the amount of time that a resource is scheduled to work over a series of time periods. It visually highlights over-allocation (peaks).

### Card 17
**Front:**
What is **Analogous Estimating**?

**Back:**
Using a previous, similar project as a basis for estimating the resources/duration of the current project. It is fast but less accurate (Gross value estimation).

### Card 18
**Front:**
What is **Parametric Estimating**?

**Back:**
Using a statistical relationship between historical data and other variables (e.g., sq. ft. of construction) to calculate resource needs (e.g., "We need 2 painters per 1,000 sq. ft.").

### Card 19
**Front:**
What is **Data Analysis** in resource planning?

**Back:**
Reviewing alternatives (e.g., Make-or-Buy analysis) to determine the best way to acquire resources.

### Card 20
**Front:**
What is the purpose of **Control Resources**?

**Back:**
To ensure that the **physical** resources assigned and allocated are available as planned, and to monitor the planned vs. actual utilization. (Note: "Manage Team" is for people; "Control Resources" is for things).

## ðŸ¤ Acquiring Resources

### Card 21
**Front:**
What is **Pre-Assignment**?

**Back:**
When specific resources are guaranteed or promised in the Project Charter or Contract before the comprehensive planning begins.

### Card 22
**Front:**
What is the key challenge in a **Matrix Organization** regarding resource acquisition?

**Back:**
The PM must **negotiate** with Functional Managers to secure resources, as the PM does not have full authority over them.

### Card 23
**Front:**
What is a **Virtual Team**?

**Back:**
A group of people with a shared goal who fulfill their roles with little or no time spent meeting face-to-face.

### Card 24
**Front:**
What is the biggest risk for Virtual Teams?

**Back:**
**Communication** breakdown and feelings of isolation.

### Card 25
**Front:**
What is **Multi-Criteria Decision Analysis** in acquiring resources?

**Back:**
Using a weighted matrix to rate potential team members or vendors based on criteria like experience, cost, availability, and location to choose the best fit.

### Card 26
**Front:**
What is **Negotiation** in resource management?

**Back:**
The dialogue between the PM and Functional Managers (or vendors) to reach an agreement on resource assignments and terms.

### Card 27
**Front:**
What is a **Generalizing Specialist** (or T-Shaped person)?

**Back:**
An Agile team member who has deep skill in one area (the vertical bar) but broad ability to help in other areas (the horizontal bar). Preferred in Agile to reduce bottlenecks.

### Card 28
**Front:**
What is **Acquire Resources** focused on?

**Back:**
Obtaining team members, facilities, equipment, materials, supplies, and other resources necessary to complete project work.

### Card 29
**Front:**
What happens if you fail to acquire the necessary resources?

**Back:**
It impacts the schedule, budget, customer satisfaction, quality, and risks. It may require re-baselining or project cancellation.

### Card 30
**Front:**
What is **Just-in-Time (JIT)** manufacturing?

**Back:**
A resource management strategy to receive materials only as they are needed in the production process, reducing inventory costs (Physical Resources).

## ðŸ§  Scenarios: Managing Resources

### Card 31
**Front:**
**Scenario**: A functional manager pulls a critical resource off your project for "higher priority" operational work. What do you do?

**Back:**
Analyze the impact. Negotiate with the manager explaining the consequences. If unresolved, refer to the **Resource Management Plan** (governance) or escalate to the Sponsor.

### Card 32
**Front:**
**Scenario**: You have two team members who are both "Responsible" (R) for the same task, and they are conflicting. What is the root cause?

**Back:**
Ambiguous roles. Clarify who is doing what. While multiple 'R's are allowed, clear divisions of labor are needed. Check the **RACI**.

### Card 33
**Front:**
**Scenario**: Equipment ordered for the project has been delayed by 2 weeks due to customs. This delays the critical path. What do you do?

**Back:**
Log an **Issue**. Communicate the delay. Look for alternatives (rent locally? fast track?). If unavoidable, submit a **Change Request** to update the schedule baseline.

### Card 34
**Front:**
**Scenario**: You notice that the actual usage of concrete is 20% higher than planned. What process helps you catch this?

**Back:**
**Control Resources**. You are monitoring planned vs. actual utilization of physical resources.

### Card 35
**Front:**
**Scenario**: Your team is working 60 hours a week to meet a deadline and morale is dropping. What resource technique should you use?

**Back:**
**Resource Leveling**. It helps smooth out the peaks in workload to prevent burnout, even if it means extending the schedule (or negotiating scope reduction).

### Card 36
**Front:**
**Scenario**: A specialized architect is only available for 2 days next month, but your plan requires 5 days. What do you do?

**Back:**
Update the **Resource Calendar** to reflect reality and adjust the schedule (Task sequencing or splitting) to accommodate their limited window.

### Card 37
**Front:**
**Scenario**: A virtual team member in a different completion time zone is consistently missing meetings. What is the first step?

**Back:**
Review the **Team Charter** or communication plan to see if meeting times were agreed upon. Have a private discussion to understand constraints and find a compromise.

### Card 38
**Front:**
**Scenario**: The client asks for a change. You realize you don't have the internal skills to implement it. What is this analysis called?

**Back:**
**Make-or-Buy Analysis**. You are deciding whether to train/hire (Make) or contract out (Buy).

### Card 39
**Front:**
**Scenario**: You are managing a large construction project. You need to ensure the crane is available only when the steel arrives to save rental costs. What are you doing?

**Back:**
Managing **logistics** and using **Resource Calendars** to optimize physical resource utilization/costs.

### Card 40
**Front:**
**Scenario**: An Agile team is constantly blocked because only one person knows how to test the database. What is the solution?

**Back:**
Encourage **cross-training** to create **T-Shaped skills** (Generalizing Specialists). This reduces the single point of failure.

## âš¡ Quick-Fire Resource Review

### Card 51
**Front:**
**True or False**: Resource Leveling never changes the critical path.

**Back:**
**False**. It often extends it because it delays tasks to wait for resources.

### Card 52
**Front:**
**True or False**: You should assign the "Accountable" role to the whole team to build ownership.

**Back:**
**False**. "Accountable" should belong to **one** person to ensure clarity. The whole team can be "Responsible."

### Card 53
**Front:**
**True or False**: Physical resources include software licenses.

**Back:**
**True**. Software licenses are considered material/infrastructure assets (non-human).

### Card 54
**Front:**
**True or False**: Soft skills negotiation is part of "Acquire Resources."

**Back:**
**True**. You must negotiate with functional managers to get the people you want.

### Card 55
**Front:**
**True or False**: A resource histogram shows who is responsible for a task.

**Back:**
**False**. A histogram shows **usage/hours** over time. A RAM/RACI shows responsibility.

### Card 56
**Front:**
What does **Halo Effect** often lead to?

**Back:**
Promoting a good technical expert to a Project Manager role without checking if they have PM skills (a common resource selection error).

### Card 57
**Front:**
What is **Self-Organization** in Agile resources?

**Back:**
The team decides **how** to do the work and **who** does which task, rather than being assigned by a manager.

### Card 58
**Front:**
What is resource **Utilization Rate**?

**Back:**
The percentage of time a resource is billable or working on project tasks vs. idle or administration.

### Card 59
**Front:**
When do you release resources?

**Back:**
As defined in the **Resource Management Plan**. Releasing them too early creates unfinished work; too late burns budget.

### Card 60
**Front:**
What is **Plan-Do-Check-Act (PDCA)** applied to resources?

**Back:**
A cycle for control. Plan resource use -> Use them -> Check actuals vs plan -> Adjust usage or plan.

## ðŸš§ Physical Resource Control

### Card 61
**Front:**
Why distinguish between "Manage Team" and "Control Resources"?

**Back:**
People (Team) require leadership, EQ, and feedback ("Manage"). Physical things require logistics, tracking, and inventory ("Control").

### Card 62
**Front:**
What is the risk of "Hoarding" resources?

**Back:**
Managers keeping resources they don't currently need "just in case," which artificially inflates shortages for other projects.

### Card 63
**Front:**
What is **Inventory Management**?

**Back:**
Tracking materials to ensure you have enough to work, but not so much that you pay high storage/spoilage costs (Lean).

### Card 64
**Front:**
What is the impact of **Supply Chain** issues on a project?

**Back:**
Delays in physical resource acquisition can halt execution. Requires **Risk Management** and contingency buffers.

### Card 65
**Front:**
How does **Quality** relate to Resource Management?

**Back:**
Using low-quality resources (untrained staff or cheap materials) often leads to high defect rates and rework (Cost of Quality).

### Card 66
**Front:**
What is **Reverse Logistics**?

**Back:**
The process of moving goods key from their typical final destination for the purpose of capturing value, or proper disposal (e.g., returning rented equipment).

### Card 67
**Front:**
What is **Life Cycle Costing** for physical resources?

**Back:**
Looking at the cost of acquisition + operation + disposal. Buying a cheap machine that breaks often may cost more long-term.

### Card 68
**Front:**
What is a **Safety Stock**?

**Back:**
An extra quantity of a product/material which is stored in the warehouse to prevent an out-of-stock situation (Buffer).

### Card 69
**Front:**
What is the **Resource Sheet** in Microsoft Project (or similar tools)?

**Back:**
The view where you define available resources, their costs (hourly/fixed), and max units (availability).

### Card 70
**Front:**
What is **Overtime**?

**Back:**
Increasing resource capacity by working more hours. It is a "Crashing" technique but increases cost and burnout risk.

## ðŸš¦ Closing Resource Management

### Card 71
**Front:**
What is the final step in resource management?

**Back:**
**Releasing resources**. Updating records, finalizing contracts, and returning team members to functional roles.

### Card 72
**Front:**
Why perform a **Team Performance Assessment** at the end?

**Back:**
To evaluate the team's effectiveness and identify lessons learned for future resource planning.

### Card 73
**Front:**
What happens to the **Lessons Learned** about a specific vendor's performance?

**Back:**
They are updated in the **Organizational Process Assets (OPAs)** to inform future source selection decisions.

### Card 74
**Front:**
Can a **Project Coordinator** assign resources?

**Back:**
Typically no. In weak matrix/functional orgs, they have little authority. They must request/coordinate, not assign.

### Card 75
**Front:**
What is **Resource Allocation**?

**Back:**
The process of validly assigning available resources to specific tasks in the schedule.

### Card 76
**Front:**
What is **Effort** vs. **Duration**?

**Back:**
-   **Effort**: Labor units (e.g., 80 man-hours).
-   **Duration**: Calendar time (e.g., 2 weeks).
-   *Adding resources may reduce duration but usually keeps effort constant (or increases it due to communication overhead).*

### Card 77
**Front:**
What is **Brooks' Law**?

**Back:**
"Adding manpower to a late software project makes it later." (Due to ramp-up time and communication complexity).

### Card 78
**Front:**
What is the **Critical Chain Method**?

**Back:**
A schedule network analysis technique that modifies the project schedule to account for limited resources, using "Resource Buffers."

### Card 79
**Front:**
What is **Monte Carlo Analysis** for resources?

**Back:**
A simulation to predict the probability of having sufficient resources or finishing on time given resource uncertainty.

### Card 80
**Front:**
What is **Benchmarking** in resource planning?

**Back:**
Comparing your resource estimates or productivity rates to industry standards to validate your plan.
