## Stakeholder Experience & Expectations

### Card 1
**Front**: What is the PMP definition of "managing expectations"?
**Back**: Proactively increasing the likelihood that project outcomes will be accepted by ensuring stakeholders understand what will be delivered and when.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Align stakeholder expectations

### Card 2
**Front**: What is the "Engagement Gap"?
**Back**: The difference between a stakeholder's **Current** engagement level and the **Desired** engagement level needed for project success.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Align stakeholder expectations

### Card 3
**Front**: What are the 5 levels of the Stakeholder Engagement Assessment Matrix?
**Back**: 1) Unaware, 2) Resistant, 3) Neutral, 4) Supportive, 5) Leading.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Align stakeholder expectations

### Card 4
**Front**: What is the most dangerous stakeholder engagement level?
**Back**: **Unaware** (because they can be surprised later) or **Resistant** (if they have high power).
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Align stakeholder expectations

### Card 5
**Front**: What defines the "Leading" engagement level?
**Back**: The stakeholder is actively engaged in ensuring the project is a success, not just supportive. They are a Champion.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Align stakeholder expectations

### Card 6
**Front**: If a stakeholder is currently "Neutral" but you need them to be "Supportive", what do you do?
**Back**: Execute the planned strategies in the Stakeholder Engagement Plan (e.g., show value/WIIFM, involve in decisions).
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Align stakeholder expectations

### Card 7
**Front**: What is "Direction of Influence"?
**Back**: Classifying stakeholders based on their relationship to the PM: Upward (Senior), Downward (Team), Outward (External), Sideward (Peers).
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Align stakeholder expectations

### Card 8
**Front**: How should you communicate "Upward" (to Executives/Sponsors)?
**Back**: Be concise. Focus on strategic value, ROI, risks, and decisions. Avoid burying the lead.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Align stakeholder expectations

### Card 9
**Front**: How should you communicate "Sideward" (to Peers/Functional Managers)?
**Back**: Focus on negotiation, resource sharing, and mutual support. You usually lack authority here.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Align stakeholder expectations

### Card 10
**Front**: What is the "Stakeholder Register" primarily used for in this task?
**Back**: To document *specific* expectations, requirements, and success criteria for each stakeholder so they can be tracked.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Align stakeholder expectations

## Communication Planning

### Card 11
**Front**: What is the **Communications Management Plan**?
**Back**: A document describing *how*, *when*, and *by whom* information will be administered and disseminated.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Align stakeholder expectations

### Card 12
**Front**: What is "Push Communication"?
**Back**: Sending information to specific recipients (e.g., Email, Reports). Ensures receipt but not understanding.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Align stakeholder expectations

### Card 13
**Front**: What is "Pull Communication"?
**Back**: Placing information in a central repository for audiences to access at their own discretion (e.g., Wiki, SharePoint).
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Align stakeholder expectations

### Card 14
**Front**: What is "Interactive Communication"?
**Back**: Real-time experimentation exchange (Meetings, Calls). The most efficient way to ensure common understanding.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Align stakeholder expectations

### Card 15
**Front**: What is the formula for Communication Channels?
**Back**: N * (N - 1) / 2. (Where N is the number of people).
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Align stakeholder expectations

### Card 16
**Front**: If you add one person to a team of 4 (making it 5), how many channels do you add?
**Back**: You go from 6 to 10 channels. (Add 4). Complexity grows exponentially.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Align stakeholder expectations

### Card 17
**Front**: What is "Noise" in the communication model?
**Back**: Anything that distorts the message (language barriers, cultural differences, bad connection, jargon).
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Align stakeholder expectations

### Card 18
**Front**: In the Sender-Receiver model, what confirms the message was understood?
**Back**: **Feedback**. (Acknowledgment only confirms receipt).
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Align stakeholder expectations

### Card 19
**Front**: What is "Paralingual" communication?
**Back**: The tone, pitch, and volume of voice (how something is said), not the words themselves.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Align stakeholder expectations

### Card 20
**Front**: Theoretically, what percentage of communication is non-verbal?
**Back**: ~55% (Body language) + ~38% (Tone) = ~93%. Words are only ~7%.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Align stakeholder expectations

## Delivery Strategy Awareness

### Card 21
**Front**: Why does choosing a Delivery Strategy (Agile vs Predictive) matter for stakeholders?
**Back**: It sets the expectation for *how* and *when* they will receive value and give feedback.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Align stakeholder expectations

### Card 22
**Front**: If stakeholders expect "frequent changes" and "rapid feedback," which strategy should you choose?
**Back**: Adaptive / Agile.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Align stakeholder expectations

### Card 23
**Front**: If stakeholders expect "fixed budget" and "guaranteed date" for a known scope, which strategy might be best?
**Back**: Predictive (Waterfall).
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Align stakeholder expectations

### Card 24
**Front**: What is the **Stacey Matrix** used for?
**Back**: To select a project approach based on Technical Uncertainty (Y-axis) and Requirements Uncertainty (X-axis).
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Align stakeholder expectations

### Card 25
**Front**: According to the Stacey Matrix, if requirements are unclear and technology is unknown, the project is...
**Back**: **Complex** (or Chaotic). Best suited for Agile/Empirical process.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Align stakeholder expectations

### Card 26
**Front**: What is the **Cynefin Framework**?
**Back**: A complexity model comprising 5 domains: Obvious/Clear, Complicated, Complex, Chaotic, and Disorder.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Align stakeholder expectations

### Card 27
**Front**: In the **Complex** domain of Cynefin, how do you act?
**Back**: Probe - Sense - Respond. (Experimentation).
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Align stakeholder expectations

### Card 28
**Front**: In the **Chaotic** domain of Cynefin, how do you act?
**Back**: Act - Sense - Respond. (Triage/Crisis management).
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Align stakeholder expectations

### Card 29
**Front**: What is a "Hybrid" approach?
**Back**: Combining Predictive and Agile elements (e.g., Fixed milestones with agile execution) to match stakeholder needs.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Align stakeholder expectations

### Card 30
**Front**: If a stakeholder demands a "detailed project plan" upfront but the scope is unknown, what do you do?
**Back**: Educate them on the risk of false certainty. Propose a Hybrid approach (Rolling Wave planning) to align expectations with reality.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Align stakeholder expectations

## Global & Virtual Communication

### Card 31
**Front**: What is "High-Context" communication?
**Back**: Meaning is embedded in the relationship, history, and non-verbal cues (e.g., Japan, Arab cultures). "Yes" might just mean "I hear you."
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Align stakeholder expectations

### Card 32
**Front**: What is "Low-Context" communication?
**Back**: Meaning is explicit in the words (e.g., USA, Germany). "Yes" means "Yes."
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Align stakeholder expectations

### Card 33
**Front**: How do you aligning expectations in a global team with time zone differences?
**Back**: Use asynchronous communication (Pull) and rotate meeting times so one region doesn't always suffer.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Align stakeholder expectations

### Card 34
**Front**: Ideally, what communication channel should be used for sensitive/conflict topics in a virtual team?
**Back**: Video Call (Interactive with high bandwidth) to capture non-verbal cues. Never email.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Align stakeholder expectations

### Card 35
**Front**: What is "Virtual Facilitation"?
**Back**: Managing meetings online using tools (chat, polls, breakout rooms) to ensure engagement and equal voice.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Align stakeholder expectations

## Managing Expectations in Practice

### Card 36
**Front**: A stakeholder says, "I thought this feature was included." How do you prevent this?
**Back**: Requirement Traceability Matrix and clear Scope Baseline (or Backlog refinement). No ambiguity.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Align stakeholder expectations

### Card 37
**Front**: A stakeholder asks for a change. You say "Yes" immediately. Is this good?
**Back**: No. It is "Scope Creep." You must analyze the impact (Time/Cost) first to align expectations on the *consequence* of the Yes.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Align stakeholder expectations

### Card 38
**Front**: What is the "No Surprises" rule?
**Back**: Stakeholders should never learn about a major issue for the first time in a formal status meeting. Communicate bad news early.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Align stakeholder expectations

### Card 39
**Front**: How does an "Iteration Review" help align expectations?
**Back**: It provides a regular "Reality Check" by showing working product. Stakeholders can verify if it matches their mental model.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Align stakeholder expectations

### Card 40
**Front**: What is the best way to handle a stakeholder with unrealistic expectations?
**Back**: Use data. Show the impact of their request on the schedule/budget. "We can do X, but it will delay Y."
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Align stakeholder expectations
