# 4.2 Communication Planning

**ECO Task**: Manage communication
**ECO Task**: Align stakeholder expectations

If you spend 90% of your time communicating, you better have a plan for it. The **Communications Management Plan** is the formal strategy for ensuring the right information reaches the right people at the right time.

---

##  Communication Methods
Choosing the wrong method leads to "Noise"—confusion, missed deadlines, and lost trust.

<ConceptGrid>
  <ConceptCard title="Interactive">
    **Multi-Directional**
    Real-time meetings, video calls, and workshops. Best for complex problem-solving and trust building.
  </ConceptCard>
  <ConceptCard title="Push">
    **One-Way (Sent)**
    Emails, memos, and newsletters sent to specific people. Best for routine updates and formal documentation.
  </ConceptCard>
  <ConceptCard title="Pull">
    **One-Way (Fetched)**
    Project portals, Wikis, and Jira boards. Best for large audiences and static technical references.
  </ConceptCard>
</ConceptGrid>

---

##  The Sender-Receiver Model
The PMP exam views communication as a technical process. Breakdowns happen when "Noise" disrupts this loop.

<ConceptGrid>
  <ConceptCard title="Encoding & Transmit">
    **Sender** translates thoughts into symbols and sends via a medium (email, voice).
  </ConceptCard>
  <ConceptCard title="Decode & Acknowledge">
    **Receiver** interprets the message and signals receipt (acknowledgment does *not* imply agreement).
  </ConceptCard>
  <ConceptCard title="Feedback Loop">
    **Receiver** responds with their own message, closing the loop and confirming understanding.
  </ConceptCard>
</ConceptGrid>

::: warning  The "Noise" Factor
**Noise** is anything that distorts the message: language barriers, cultural differences, technical jargon, or physical distractions. As a PM, your primary job is to **Minimize Noise**.
:::

---

##  Global Communication: Cultural Context
In 2026, teams are global. You must adjust your style based on Hall's Cultural Context model.

<ConceptGrid>
  <ConceptCard title="High-Context">
    **Cultures**: Japan, Arab world, Latin America.
    **Dynamics**: Meaning is in the *relationship* and *tone*. Trust must be built before business.
  </ConceptCard>
  <ConceptCard title="Low-Context">
    **Cultures**: USA, Germany, Scandinavia.
    **Dynamics**: Meaning is explicit and direct. Speed and clarity are valued over ritual.
  </ConceptCard>
</ConceptGrid>

---

##  Meeting Management (2026 Focus)
Meetings are the most common—and most abused—communication method.

<ConceptGrid>
  <ConceptCard title="Virtual Equity">
    "If one is remote, all are remote." Use chat-first strategies and digital workspaces for equal contribution.
  </ConceptCard>
  <ConceptCard title="Strict Time-Boxing">
    Respect calendars with hard starts/ends and agendas distributed at least 24 hours in advance.
  </ConceptCard>
  <ConceptCard title="Action-Oriented">
    Every meeting must end with clear owners and deadlines assigned to every decision and action.
  </ConceptCard>
</ConceptGrid>
