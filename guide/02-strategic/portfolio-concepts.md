# 2.1b Portfolio Management Concepts

While Project Management is about "doing things right," **Portfolio Management** is about **"doing the right things."** It is the centralized management of one or more portfolios to achieve strategic objectives.

---

## üß≠ Portfolio vs. Program vs. Project
Understanding these distinctions is essential for the PMP exam:

| Aspect      | Project               | Program                   | Portfolio             |
| :---------- | :-------------------- | :------------------------ | :-------------------- |
| **Focus**   | Specific deliverable  | Coordinated benefits      | Strategic value       |
| **Goal**    | "Do the work right"   | "Coordinate related work" | "Do the right work"   |
| **Success** | Quality, time, budget | Benefit realization       | Strategic achievement |
| **Change**  | Managed control       | Adapts to business needs  | Continuous evolution  |

---

## ‚öñÔ∏è Portfolio Balancing
A healthy portfolio isn't just a list; it's a balanced collection of investments. Portfolio managers optimize the mix across several dimensions:

### 1. Risk vs. Return
- **Transform**: High-value, lower-risk strategic pivots.
- **Innovate**: High-value, high-risk "moonshots".
- **Maintain**: Necessary operational maintenance (low value, low risk).

### 2. Time Horizons
Balancing "Quick Wins" (0-6 months) with "Long-term Transformation" (18+ months). A portfolio with only long-term projects risks losing stakeholder support; one with only short-term projects risks obsolescence.

### 3. Investment Categories
Ensuring a mix of **Growth** (new markets), **Efficiency** (cost cutting), and **Compliance** (legal requirements).

---

## üèõÔ∏è Portfolio Governance
**Portfolio Governance** provides the decision-making framework. It is typically led by a **Portfolio Review Board (PRB)** consisting of executive sponsors and functional leaders.

### Key Governance Actions:
- **Prioritize**: Ranking projects by strategic value.
- **Authorize**: Approving funding and resource allocation.
- **Limit WIP**: Reducing the number of active projects to prevent "resource peanut-buttering" (spreading resources too thin).
- **Termination**: Having the discipline to cancel projects that no longer align with shifted strategies.

---

## ‚ö° Resource Capacity & Allocation
One of the most difficult portfolio tasks is matching demand to **Capacity**.

::: info üìä Capacity Constraints
1. **Financial**: The total capital budget.
2. **Human**: The available hours of the workforce.
3. **Specialist**: Scarce skills (e.g., Cybersecurity experts, Architects).
4. **Bandwidth**: The organization's ability to absorb change without breaking.
:::

**Resource Leveling at Portfolio Scale**: If two high-priority projects need the same expert, the PRB must choose to either delay one, hire external help (adding cost), or reduce scope.
