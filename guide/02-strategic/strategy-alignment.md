# 2.1 Strategic Alignment

Strategic alignment is the discipline of ensuring that the **right work** gets done in the first place and stays relevant as the business environment shifts.

---

## üéØ What Strategic Alignment Means (On the PMP Exam)
On the exam, ‚Äústrategic alignment‚Äù is not a slogan. It is a **decision filter** you use every time you:

- Approve, reject, or re-prioritize work
- Recommend a scope change (or *refuse* one)
- Escalate a major risk or regulatory change
- Recommend a project be paused or closed because the expected value is gone

::: tip üí° The PMI Mindset
If a project is ‚Äúgreen‚Äù (on time/budget) but is no longer valuable or compliant, it is **not successful**. The right answer is usually: **raise it to governance and recommend a strategic decision**.
:::

## 2026 Focus: Continuous Validation
Strategic alignment is not a "set it and forget it" activity.
*   **Pivot or Persevere**: Having the courage to recommend project cancellation if the strategic value evaporates.
*   **PESTLE Scanning**: Watching the horizon (Political, Economic, Social) for shifts that impact project viability.

---

## üîÅ The Continuous Alignment Loop (A Simple Decision Model)
Use this when an exam question introduces ‚Äúnew information‚Äù mid-project (market shift, regulatory change, leadership pivot).

1. **Detect**: Identify the change (signal, trigger, new constraint).
2. **Assess**: Evaluate impacts to scope/cost/schedule *and* value/benefits.
3. **Escalate**: Bring options to governance (continue, pivot, pause, stop).
4. **Decide**: Governance chooses; the PM executes the decision.
5. **Update**: Update baselines, risks, and communications; reinforce adoption.

::: warning üö¶ Common Trap
Do not ‚Äúself-cancel‚Äù a project unless you have explicit authority. The PMI-aligned move is to **recommend** cancellation and support an orderly closure.
:::
