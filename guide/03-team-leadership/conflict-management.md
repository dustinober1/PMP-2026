# 3.3 Conflict Management & Negotiation

Conflict is not a sign of failure; it is a sign of passion. If your team never fights, they probably don't care. Your job is to channel that energy into innovation.

---

## üö¶ The Thomas-Kilmann Conflict Mode Instrument (TKI)
The 5 conflict resolution modes are mapped on two dimensions: **Assertiveness** (concern for self) and **Cooperativeness** (concern for others).

## üß† Choosing the Right Mode: The Decision Matrix
The exam often asks for the **best** technique, not the fastest.

| Mode | Use When | What You Gain | What You Risk |
| :--- | :------- | :------------ | :------------ |
| **Collaborate** | Root cause matters and you have time | Best solution + commitment + learning | Slower; requires facilitation skill |
| **Compromise** | Temporary solution is acceptable; time pressure | Quick agreement | Mediocre outcome; may resurface later |
| **Force / Direct** | Emergency, safety, severe compliance | Immediate action | Damaged trust; resentment; no buy-in |
| **Smooth / Accommodate** | Issue is minor or you want to preserve relationship | Short-term harmony | Real issue stays unresolved |
| **Withdraw / Avoid** | Emotions are too high or facts are unclear | Cool-down time | Delays resolution; can fester |

### The Conflict Mode Selection Flowchart
```
Is it urgent & safety-critical?
    YES ‚Üí FORCE (then repair trust later)
    NO ‚Üì

Are emotions too high for productive discussion?
    YES ‚Üí WITHDRAW (temporarily) ‚Üí schedule later
    NO ‚Üì

Is this issue minor or is the relationship more important?
    YES ‚Üí SMOOTH / ACCOMMODATE
    NO ‚Üì

Do we have time to find the best solution?
    YES ‚Üí COLLABORATE / PROBLEM SOLVE
    NO ‚Üí COMPROMISE (temporary fix)
```

::: tip ‚úÖ Exam Pattern: Collaborate is the default "best"
If the scenario is not an emergency, look for choices that involve listening, using data, and jointly creating a solution. Collaborate almost always beats Compromise in non-time-pressured scenarios.
:::

## üî• Types of Conflict: Task vs. Relationship
Not all conflict is the same. Understanding the type helps you respond correctly.

| Type | Description | Impact | PM Action |
| :--- | :---------- | :----- | :-------- |
| **Task Conflict** | Disagreement about work (approach, priorities, quality) | Can be healthy; drives innovation | Facilitate; let team debate; guide to decision |
| **Relationship Conflict** | Personal friction, dislike, personality clash | Always harmful; destroys trust | Intervene early; address privately; de-escalate |
| **Process Conflict** | Disagreement about how to work together | Moderate; can be resolved with clarity | Clarify roles, update working agreements |

### The Personality Trap
::: warning ‚ö†Ô∏è Most friction is caused by Scope, Schedule, or Priorities
What looks like a "personality conflict" is usually a process issue:
- "She never listens to me" ‚Üí Different priorities, unclear decision rights
- "He's impossible to work with" ‚Üí Competing deadlines, no escalation path
- "They're too difficult" ‚Üí Unclear scope, different definitions of done

**Always look for the process issue before blaming the person.**
:::

## ü§ù Negotiation Strategies (Task 8)
Conflict often requires negotiation to resolve. On the exam, you need to know *how* you are negotiating.

### Core Negotiation Concepts

#### BATNA (Best Alternative to a Negotiated Agreement)
Your "Plan B." If you can't reach a deal, what will you do?

**Example**: "If this vendor is too expensive, I can build it in-house for $50k."

**Rules**:
- **Never** negotiate without knowing your BATNA
- The stronger your BATNA, the more leverage you have
- Improve your BATNA before negotiating if possible

#### ZOPA (Zone of Possible Agreement)
The overlap between the seller's minimum price and the buyer's maximum price.

```
Seller's Minimum: $80,000  ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ
                                           ZOPA ($80k-$100k)
Buyer's Maximum: $100,000 ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ
```

If there's no ZOPA, no deal is possible without changing the terms.

#### Reservation Price
The worst acceptable outcome. If the deal is worse than this, walk away.

### The Two Negotiation Strategies
| Strategy | Mindset | Outcome | Use When |
| :------- | :------ | :------ | :------- |
| **Distributive** | "Fixed Pie" (Win-Lose) | I get more, you get less | Buying a car; one-time transaction; no future relationship |
| **Integrative** | "Expand the Pie" (Win-Win) | We find value for both | **Most project situations.** Partnerships, team agreements, long-term contracts |

### Integrative Negotiation Tactics
| Tactic | Description | Example |
| :----- | :---------- | :------ |
| **Interests, not positions** | Ask "why" behind their demand | "Why do you need it by Friday?" ‚Üí They need a demo for Monday |
| **Create options** | Brainstorm ways to meet both needs | "What if we deliver core features Friday, polish by Monday?" |
| **Objective criteria** | Use data, not opinion | "Industry benchmark says 2 weeks is reasonable" |
| **Separate people from problems** | Focus on the issue, not the person | "The timeline is challenging" not "You're being unrealistic" |

## üß≠ The Conflict Resolution Process
When conflict erupts, follow this escalation ladder:

### Step 1: Self-Correction
Let the team members try to solve it themselves first. Many conflicts resolve without PM intervention.

**PM Action**: Monitor but don't jump in immediately.

### Step 2: Private Intervention
If it persists, facilitate a safe, private conversation.

**PM Action**: Meet 1-on-1 with each party first to understand perspectives.

### Step 3: Collaborative Session
Bring parties together. Use data to remove emotion and focus on the problem.

**PM Action**: Facilitate using the Collaboration Agenda below.

### Step 4: Formal Authority
Make a decision if the team remains deadlocked.

**PM Action**: Decide, explain rationale, and document.

### Step 5: Escalation
Involve sponsor or functional managers only if authority is required.

**PM Action**: Present the issue, options considered, and recommendation.

## üß© A Collaboration Agenda (15‚Äì30 Minutes)
Use this structure to turn conflict into a decision:

| Step | Time | Action |
| :--- | :--- | :----- |
| **1. State the problem** | 2 min | Neutral language, facts not accusations |
| **2. Confirm interests** | 5 min | What does each side truly need? (time, quality, risk, autonomy) |
| **3. Generate options** | 10 min | Brainstorm first, evaluate second |
| **4. Agree on criteria** | 3 min | Cost, schedule, risk, customer impact, compliance |
| **5. Decide** | 5 min | Choose the best option based on criteria |
| **6. Assign actions** | 2 min | Owner + due date for each action |
| **7. Document** | 1 min | Capture the decision; update team charter if new rule is needed |

### The Ground Rules for the Session
- **Speak for yourself**: Use "I" statements, not "you always"
- **Listen to understand**: No interrupting; paraphrase before responding
- **Focus on interests, not positions**: "I need reliable data" not "I need that specific report"
- **Critique ideas, not people**: "That approach has risks" not "That's a stupid idea"

## üõ†Ô∏è De-escalation Techniques
When emotions run high, your first job is to calm the situation before solving it.

### Verbal De-escalation
| Technique | What to Say |
| :-------- | :---------- |
| **Acknowledge** | "I can see this is frustrating for you." |
| **Validate** | "Your concerns are legitimate." |
| **Reframe** | "Let's focus on what we're both trying to achieve." |
| **Pause** | "Let's take a 10-minute break and come back fresh." |
| **Summarize** | "Let me make sure I understand your position..." |

### Physical De-escalation
- Move the conversation to a private space
- Ensure people are seated (standing escalates)
- Offer water/coffee (physiological reset)
- Lower your own voice and slow your speech

## üß± Structural Solutions to Recurring Conflict
If the same conflict keeps happening, fix the system, not just the symptom.

| Pattern | Structural Fix |
| :------ | :------------- |
| **Role confusion** | Create/update RACI |
| **Decision paralysis** | Clarify decision model in team charter |
| **Scope creep battles** | Formalize change control process |
| **Priority fights** | Align on prioritization criteria (value, risk, dependency) |
| **Handoff failures** | Define explicit handoff criteria and checklists |
