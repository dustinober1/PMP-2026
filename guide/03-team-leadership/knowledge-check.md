# Knowledge Check: Team Leadership and Development

Test your understanding of team leadership, servant leadership, conflict resolution, and motivation.

## Practice Questions

### Question 1: Team Development Stages

A team is experiencing disagreements about technical approaches and some members are challenging the project manager's authority. According to Tuckman, which stage is the team in?

- A) Forming
- B) Storming ✓
- C) Norming
- D) Performing

**Explanation**: Storming is characterized by conflict, disagreements, and power struggles as team members establish their positions.

---

### Question 2: Servant Leadership

A team member comes to you frustrated about an impediment blocking their work. As a servant leader, what is your primary responsibility?

- A) Tell them to find their own solution to build self-reliance
- B) Remove the impediment or help them remove it ✓
- C) Add it to the risk register and continue monitoring
- D) Escalate immediately to the sponsor

**Explanation**: The primary role of a servant leader is to remove obstacles that prevent the team from doing their work.

---

### Question 3: Conflict Resolution

Two senior developers disagree on the architecture for a critical component. Both approaches have merit, and you need their buy-in for implementation. Which conflict resolution mode is most appropriate?

- A) Force/Compete
- B) Avoid/Withdraw
- C) Compromise
- D) Collaborate/Problem-Solve ✓

**Explanation**: Collaboration is best when both the issue and the relationship are important, and you need commitment from the parties involved.

---

### Question 4: Herzberg's Theory

According to Herzberg's Two-Factor Theory, which of the following is a MOTIVATOR rather than a hygiene factor?

- A) Salary
- B) Job security
- C) Recognition ✓
- D) Working conditions

**Explanation**: Recognition is a motivator that creates satisfaction. Salary, security, and conditions are hygiene factors that only prevent dissatisfaction.

---

### Question 5: Leadership Styles

A project manager leads a team of highly experienced professionals who are experts in their field. The PM focuses on removing obstacles but otherwise lets them work independently. Which leadership style is this?

- A) Autocratic
- B) Laissez-faire/Delegative ✓
- C) Directive
- D) Transactional

**Explanation**: Laissez-faire (or Delegative) leadership gives autonomy to competent, self-motivated team members.

---

### Question 6: Emotional Intelligence

Which component of emotional intelligence involves recognizing and understanding emotions in OTHERS?

- A) Self-awareness
- B) Self-management
- C) Social awareness ✓
- D) Relationship management

**Explanation**: Social awareness is the ability to sense and understand the emotions of others (empathy).

---

### Question 7: Forcing Mode

Time is running out on a critical decision. Two team members cannot agree. As PM, you make the decision for them. Which conflict resolution mode did you use?

- A) Collaborate
- B) Compromise
- C) Force/Compete ✓
- D) Smooth/Accommodate

**Explanation**: Force (or Compete) involves making a decision without the agreement of others. Appropriate in time-critical situations.

---

### Question 8: Pink's Drive Theory

According to Pink's Drive theory, which three elements drive intrinsic motivation?

- A) Money, promotion, recognition
- B) Autonomy, mastery, purpose ✓
- C) Safety, belonging, esteem
- D) Achievement, power, affiliation

**Explanation**: Daniel Pink's research identifies Autonomy (control), Mastery (growth), and Purpose (meaning) as key drivers of intrinsic motivation.

## Exam Tips for the People Domain

::: tip Scenario-Based Questions
The People domain is heavily scenario-based. For each question:
1. Identify the situation type (conflict, motivation, team stage)
2. Consider what a servant leader would do
3. Look for answers that involve listening, empathy, and enablement
4. Avoid answers that are autocratic, dismissive, or escalate prematurely
:::

### Common Wrong Answer Patterns

- ❌ Ignoring team member concerns
- ❌ Immediately escalating to management
- ❌ Using authority when collaboration would work
- ❌ Avoiding conflict when it needs to be addressed
- ❌ Focusing on blame rather than solutions

### Right Answer Patterns

- ✅ Active listening and understanding
- ✅ Addressing root causes, not just symptoms
- ✅ Enabling team members to solve problems
- ✅ Removing obstacles as a servant leader
- ✅ Adapting leadership style to the situation
