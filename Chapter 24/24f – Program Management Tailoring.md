# 24f – Program Management Tailoring

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this section, you will be able to:

- Assess organizational context and program characteristics to guide tailoring decisions
- Tailor development approaches based on program requirements and environmental factors
- Adapt governance structures to fit organizational culture and program complexity
- Customize leadership styles to match program team composition and stakeholder needs
- Adjust tools, metrics, and processes to align with program context and constraints

---

## Introduction

Sarah attended a conference on program management where she heard presentations from three different program managers. The first described a highly structured, phase-gated approach for a construction program. The second explained an agile, iterative approach for a software innovation program. The third detailed a hybrid approach combining detailed planning with adaptive execution.

"All are valid approaches," the conference speaker emphasized. "The key is matching your approach to your context. A construction program with fixed requirements and regulatory constraints needs different management than an innovation program exploring new possibilities. Program management isn't one-size-fits-all—it requires thoughtful tailoring."

This insight helped Sarah understand why some aspects of her digital transformation program felt misaligned. She was applying uniform approaches across very different components—predictive methods for infrastructure, agile approaches for customer experience, and hybrid approaches for process automation. She needed to tailor her program management approach to fit each component's requirements while maintaining overall program coherence.

**Program management tailoring** is the deliberate adaptation of program management approaches, processes, and tools to fit organizational context, program characteristics, and environmental factors. This section explores how to design the right program management approach for each unique situation.

## Understanding Tailoring Context

### Organizational Context Factors

#### 1. Organizational Culture
**Culture Characteristics**:
- **Hierarchy Level**: Degree of organizational hierarchy and formal structure
- **Decision-Making Style**: Centralized vs. distributed decision-making authority
- **Risk Tolerance**: Organization's comfort with risk and uncertainty
- **Change Readiness**: Organization's experience and comfort with change
- **Collaboration Style**: Preference for individual vs. collaborative work

**Culture Impact on Tailoring**:
```
ORGANIZATIONAL CULTURE IMPACT

Hierarchical Culture:
- Tailoring Implications: Formal governance, clear authority, detailed documentation
- Best Practices: Strong governance structures, formal change control, detailed reporting
- Challenges: Slower decision-making, resistance to empowerment

Collaborative Culture:
- Tailoring Implications: Team-based approaches, shared authority, collaborative tools
- Best Practices: Cross-functional teams, collaborative planning, shared governance
- Challenges: Decision-making ambiguity, role confusion

Risk-Averse Culture:
- Tailoring Implications: Detailed planning, risk management, formal controls
- Best Practices: Comprehensive risk management, phased approaches, formal reviews
- Challenges: Resistance to innovation, slow adaptation

Risk-Tolerant Culture:
- Tailoring Implications: Flexible approaches, experimentation, rapid iteration
- Best Practices: Agile methods, innovation frameworks, rapid prototyping
- Challenges: Insufficient control, potential for chaos

Change-Ready Culture:
- Tailoring Implications: Adaptive approaches, continuous improvement, learning focus
- Best Practices: Change management integration, learning organization practices
- Challenges: Constant change can create uncertainty and fatigue
```

#### 2. Organizational Structure
**Structure Types**:
- **Functional Structure**: Traditional hierarchical structure organized by function
- **Divisional Structure**: Structure organized by product, market, or geography
- **Matrix Structure**: Dual reporting structure combining functional and project focus
- **Network Structure**: Flexible structure with extensive outsourcing and partnerships

**Structure Tailoring Considerations**:
```
ORGANIZATIONAL STRUCTURE TAILORING

Functional Structure:
- Characteristics: Clear hierarchy, functional expertise, strong control
- Tailoring Approach: Formal coordination, functional interfaces, clear authority
- Governance: Strong functional governance, clear escalation paths
- Communication: Formal reporting through functional hierarchy

Divisional Structure:
- Characteristics: Division autonomy, local decision-making, market focus
- Tailoring Approach: Division coordination, local adaptation, market responsiveness
- Governance: Balance between central and division governance
- Communication: Both central coordination and local communication

Matrix Structure:
- Characteristics: Dual reporting, shared resources, cross-functional focus
- Tailoring Approach: Strong coordination, clear role definitions, conflict resolution
- Governance: Clear decision-making authority, conflict resolution mechanisms
- Communication: Extensive communication across reporting lines

Network Structure:
- Characteristics: External partnerships, virtual teams, flexible arrangements
- Tailoring Approach: Partner coordination, contract management, relationship focus
- Governance: Contract-based governance, partner relationship management
- Communication: Partner communication, virtual collaboration tools
```

#### 3. Strategic Context
**Strategic Factors**:
- **Strategic Importance**: How critical the program is to organizational strategy
- **Resource Availability**: Organization's capacity to support the program
- **Time Pressure**: Urgency of program delivery and benefits realization
- **Competitive Context**: Competitive pressures and market dynamics

### Program Characteristics

#### 1. Program Type
**Program Types and Tailoring Implications**:

**Transformation Programs**:
- **Characteristics**: Major organizational change, broad scope, extended timeline
- **Tailoring Approach**: Strong change management, phased implementation, stakeholder engagement
- **Governance**: Executive sponsorship, broad stakeholder representation
- **Success Criteria**: Strategic impact, organizational adoption, cultural change

**Innovation Programs**:
- **Characteristics**: High uncertainty, exploration focus, learning orientation
- **Tailoring Approach**: Agile methods, experimentation, iteration, learning integration
- **Governance**: Lightweight governance, empowerment, rapid decision-making
- **Success Criteria**: Innovation outcomes, learning and capability development, market impact

**Infrastructure Programs**:
- **Characteristics**: Technical focus, clear requirements, integration needs
- **Tailoring Approach**: Predictive methods, technical standards, integration planning
- **Governance**: Technical governance, standards compliance, quality focus
- **Success Criteria**: Technical performance, integration success, operational stability

**Compliance Programs**:
- **Characteristics**: Regulatory driven, fixed requirements, high risk tolerance
- **Tailoring Approach**: Formal methods, documentation, audit trails, compliance focus
- **Governance**: Strong compliance governance, audit readiness, formal controls
- **Success Criteria**: Regulatory compliance, audit success, risk mitigation

#### 2. Program Complexity
**Complexity Dimensions**:

**Technical Complexity**:
- **Low**: Well-understood technology, standard solutions, limited integration
- **Medium**: Emerging technology, some integration, moderate technical risk
- **High**: Cutting-edge technology, extensive integration, high technical risk

**Organizational Complexity**:
- **Low**: Single business unit, limited stakeholders, clear authority
- **Medium**: Multiple business units, moderate stakeholders, some authority complexity
- **High**: Cross-organization, extensive stakeholders, complex authority structures

**Environmental Complexity**:
- **Low**: Stable environment, limited change, predictable conditions
- **Medium**: Some change, moderate uncertainty, some volatility
- **High**: Dynamic environment, high uncertainty, constant change

**Complexity-Based Tailoring**:
```
COMPLEXITY-BASED TAILORING

Low Technical Complexity:
- Approach: Predictive methods, standard processes, proven solutions
- Planning: Detailed upfront planning, clear specifications, standard designs
- Risk Management: Standard risk processes, known risks, proven mitigation
- Success Factors: Execution excellence, quality control, schedule management

High Technical Complexity:
- Approach: Adaptive methods, experimentation, learning orientation
- Planning: Rolling wave planning, discovery and validation, flexible designs
- Risk Management: Extensive risk management, emerging risks, adaptive mitigation
- Success Factors: Innovation capability, learning integration, adaptation speed

Low Organizational Complexity:
- Approach: Simple governance, clear authority, direct communication
- Stakeholder Management: Direct engagement, clear roles, simple communication
- Change Management: Standard change processes, limited resistance management
- Success Factors: Clear direction, efficient execution, stakeholder alignment

High Organizational Complexity:
- Approach: Complex governance, stakeholder coalitions, extensive communication
- Stakeholder Management: Coalition building, negotiation, conflict resolution
- Change Management: Comprehensive change management, resistance management, cultural change
- Success Factors: Stakeholder alignment, political navigation, coalition building
```

#### 3. Program Scale
**Scale Factors**:
- **Budget Size**: Small (<$1M), Medium ($1-10M), Large (>$10M)
- **Team Size**: Small (<10 people), Medium (10-50), Large (>50)
- **Timeline**: Short (<6 months), Medium (6-18 months), Long (>18 months)
- **Geographic Distribution**: Single location, multiple locations, global

## Development Approach Tailoring

### Predictive Approach

**When to Use**:
- Requirements are well-understood and stable
- Technology is proven and familiar
- Low uncertainty and risk tolerance
- Regulatory or compliance requirements
- Fixed timeline and budget constraints

**Predictive Tailoring Elements**:

#### 1. Planning Approach
```
PREDICTIVE PLANNING FRAMEWORK

Detailed Upfront Planning:
- Comprehensive requirements analysis and documentation
- Detailed work breakdown structure and scheduling
- Complete resource planning and allocation
- Full risk identification and mitigation planning
- Comprehensive stakeholder analysis and engagement planning

Change Control:
- Formal change control processes
- Change impact analysis and approval
- Baseline maintenance and variance tracking
- Change documentation and communication

Progressive Elaboration:
- Initial detailed planning for near-term work
- Progressive detail for longer-term activities
- Rolling wave planning for uncertainty areas
- Regular planning updates and refinements
```

#### 2. Execution Management
```
PREDICTIVE EXECUTION MANAGEMENT

Schedule Management:
- Detailed project schedules with critical path analysis
- Regular schedule updates and variance analysis
- Schedule compression and optimization techniques
- Milestone tracking and completion reporting

Cost Management:
- Detailed budget development and allocation
- Regular cost tracking and variance analysis
- Earned value management for performance measurement
- Cost forecasting and budget management

Scope Management:
- Detailed scope definition and documentation
- Work breakdown structure development
- Scope verification and control processes
- Configuration management for scope changes
```

### Agile Approach

**When to Use**:
- Requirements evolve and emerge through execution
- High uncertainty and need for adaptation
- Innovation and discovery components
- Rapid market response requirements
- Learning and experimentation value

**Agile Tailoring Elements**:

#### 1. Iterative Planning
```
AGILE PLANNING FRAMEWORK

Release Planning:
- Product vision and roadmap development
- Release definition and prioritization
- High-level release planning and forecasting
- Stakeholder alignment on release objectives

Iteration Planning:
- Iteration goal definition and commitment
- Work breakdown into manageable increments
- Team capacity planning and task allocation
- Iteration risk identification and mitigation

Continuous Planning:
- Regular backlog refinement and prioritization
- Rolling wave planning with adaptive horizons
- Stakeholder feedback incorporation
- Planning based on learning and discovery
```

#### 2. Adaptive Execution
```
AGILE EXECUTION MANAGEMENT

Iterative Delivery:
- Short iteration cycles (2-4 weeks typical)
- Incremental value delivery and demonstration
- Regular stakeholder feedback and validation
- Continuous integration and delivery

Adaptive Management:
- Daily stand-up meetings and coordination
- Regular retrospectives and process improvement
- Backlog grooming and prioritization
- Scope adaptation based on learning

Collaborative Governance:
- Self-organizing teams with clear boundaries
- Collaborative decision-making within frameworks
- Stakeholder involvement in prioritization
- Transparency and information radiators
```

### Hybrid Approach

**When to Use**:
- Mix of stable and evolving requirements
- Combination of technical and organizational components
- Need for both predictability and adaptability
- Multi-disciplinary programs with different component needs

**Hybrid Tailoring Elements**:

#### 1. Component-Based Planning
```
HYBRID PLANNING FRAMEWORK

Component Classification:
- Predictive Components: Stable requirements, proven technology
- Agile Components: Evolving requirements, discovery needs
- Hybrid Components: Mixed characteristics, adaptive needs

Planning Integration:
- Predictive planning for stable components
- Agile planning for adaptive components
- Integration planning for component coordination
- Roadmap planning for overall program alignment

Phase-Based Approach:
- Predictive phases for foundation work
- Agile phases for development and adaptation
- Integration phases for component coordination
- Closure phases for transition and sustainment
```

#### 2. Adaptive Governance
```
HYBRID GOVERNANCE FRAMEWORK

Flexible Governance:
- Predictive governance for stable components
- Agile governance for adaptive components
- Program-level governance for coordination
- Stakeholder governance for alignment

Decision-Making Balance:
- Strategic decisions with predictive approach
- Tactical decisions with agile approach
- Integration decisions with collaborative approach
- Escalation decisions with formal approach

Risk Management Integration:
- Predictive risk management for known risks
- Agile risk management for emerging risks
- Program risk management for coordination
- Continuous risk assessment and adaptation
```

## Governance Tailoring

### Governance Structure Design

#### 1. Governance Complexity Levels
**Simple Governance**:
- **Characteristics**: Clear authority, simple decision-making, limited stakeholders
- **Structure**: Single steering committee, clear decision paths
- **Best For**: Small programs, single business unit, clear objectives

**Moderate Governance**:
- **Characteristics**: Multiple stakeholder groups, some decision complexity
- **Structure**: Steering committee with working groups, clear escalation paths
- **Best For**: Medium programs, multiple business units, moderate complexity

**Complex Governance**:
- **Characteristics**: Many stakeholders, complex decision-making, political factors
- **Structure**: Multi-level governance, coalition building, negotiation mechanisms
- **Best For**: Large programs, cross-organizational, high complexity

#### 2. Decision-Making Authority
```
DECISION-MAKING AUTHORITY TAILORING

Centralized Decision-Making:
- Authority concentrated in senior leadership
- Clear hierarchy and escalation paths
- Fast decisions on strategic issues
- Best for: High-stakes decisions, crisis situations, strategic alignment

Distributed Decision-Making:
- Authority distributed to appropriate levels
- Team autonomy within defined boundaries
- Faster operational decisions
- Best for: Operational decisions, empowered teams, rapid adaptation

Consensus-Based Decision-Making:
- Decisions made through group agreement
- High stakeholder buy-in and commitment
- Slower but more robust decisions
- Best for: Major changes, stakeholder alignment, cultural transformation

Hybrid Decision-Making:
- Strategic decisions centralized, operational distributed
- Clear boundaries and decision frameworks
- Balance of speed and alignment
- Best for: Complex programs, mixed needs, adaptive environments
```

### Governance Process Tailoring

#### 1. Meeting Structure
```
MEETING STRUCTURE TAILORING

Minimal Meeting Approach:
- Monthly steering committee reviews
- Quarterly stakeholder updates
- Ad-hoc issue resolution meetings
- Best for: Simple programs, clear objectives, stable environment

Moderate Meeting Approach:
- Bi-weekly operational reviews
- Monthly strategic reviews
- Quarterly stakeholder workshops
- Best for: Medium programs, moderate complexity, some change

Comprehensive Meeting Approach:
- Weekly operational coordination
- Bi-weekly tactical reviews
- Monthly strategic reviews
- Quarterly stakeholder forums
- Best for: Complex programs, high change, many stakeholders
```

#### 2. Reporting and Communication
```
REPORTING TAILORING

Lightweight Reporting:
- Monthly executive dashboards
- Quarterly stakeholder newsletters
- Annual benefit reports
- Best for: Low-complexity programs, experienced stakeholders

Standard Reporting:
- Monthly performance reports
- Bi-weekly operational updates
- Quarterly business reviews
- Best for: Medium-complexity programs, mixed stakeholder needs

Comprehensive Reporting:
- Weekly operational dashboards
- Monthly performance reports
- Quarterly strategic reviews
- Ad-hoc issue notifications
- Best for: High-complexity programs, regulatory requirements
```

## Leadership Style Tailoring

### Leadership Style Assessment

#### 1. Program Context Leadership
**Directive Leadership**:
- **When to Use**: Crisis situations, compliance requirements, low team experience
- **Characteristics**: Clear direction, strong decision-making, close supervision
- **Focus**: Execution, control, risk management

**Participative Leadership**:
- **When to Use**: Complex problems, experienced teams, need for buy-in
- **Characteristics**: Collaborative decision-making, team involvement, consensus building
- **Focus**: Problem-solving, team development, stakeholder alignment

**Delegative Leadership**:
- **When to Use**: Expert teams, innovation needs, empowerment culture
- **Characteristics**: Team autonomy, empowerment, limited intervention
- **Focus**: Innovation, speed, team capability

**Situational Leadership**:
- **When to Use**: Mixed team experience, changing needs, adaptive requirements
- **Characteristics**: Style adaptation based on situation and team capability
- **Focus**: Flexibility, development, contextual effectiveness

#### 2. Team Composition Leadership
```
TEAM LEADERSHIP TAILORING

Expert Team Leadership:
- Leadership Style: Facilitative, consultative, supportive
- Focus: Coordination, integration, barrier removal
- Decision Approach: Team-based, expertise-driven
- Communication: Collaborative, transparent, knowledge-sharing

Mixed Experience Team Leadership:
- Leadership Style: Situational, coaching, developmental
- Focus: Skill development, mentorship, capability building
- Decision Approach: Guided team decision-making with oversight
- Communication: Educational, supportive, feedback-oriented

Virtual Team Leadership:
- Leadership Style: Trust-based, results-oriented, communicative
- Focus: Connection, collaboration, virtual presence
- Decision Approach: Clear processes, virtual collaboration
- Communication: Frequent, multi-channel, technology-enabled

Cross-Cultural Team Leadership:
- Leadership Style: Culturally aware, inclusive, respectful
- Focus: Cultural integration, communication clarity, relationship building
- Decision Approach: Cultural sensitivity, inclusive processes
- Communication: Clear, simple, culturally appropriate
```

### Stakeholder Leadership

#### 1. Stakeholder Engagement Leadership
```
STAKEHOLDER LEADERSHIP TAILORING

Executive Stakeholder Leadership:
- Style: Strategic partnership, peer relationship, value focus
- Communication: Executive-level, strategic focus, business impact
- Engagement: Advisory role, strategic guidance, resource advocacy
- Success Factors: Strategic alignment, business value, executive confidence

Operational Stakeholder Leadership:
- Style: Collaborative partnership, operational focus, practical solutions
- Communication: Operational detail, practical implications, implementation support
- Engagement: Operational involvement, process ownership, practical guidance
- Success Factors: Operational success, process improvement, team satisfaction

Technical Stakeholder Leadership:
- Style: Technical partnership, expertise respect, innovation focus
- Communication: Technical detail, solution focus, knowledge sharing
- Engagement: Technical collaboration, expertise contribution, innovation support
- Success Factors: Technical excellence, innovation success, knowledge development

External Stakeholder Leadership:
- Style: Relationship building, value creation, mutual benefit
- Communication: External messaging, value proposition, relationship management
- Engagement: Partnership development, value creation, relationship maintenance
- Success Factors: Partnership success, mutual benefit, relationship strength
```

## Tools and Metrics Tailoring

### Tool Selection Framework

#### 1. Project and Program Management Tools
```
TOOL SELECTION CRITERIA

Complexity Matching:
- Simple Tools for Simple Programs: Basic scheduling, budgeting, reporting
- Comprehensive Tools for Complex Programs: Advanced features, integration capabilities
- Specialized Tools for Specific Needs: Industry-specific, domain-specific tools

Organizational Integration:
- Integration with existing systems: ERP, CRM, financial systems
- Organizational standards and preferences: Approved tool sets, IT policies
- User capability and training: Tool complexity vs. user skill levels

Collaboration Requirements:
- Team distribution: Co-located vs. distributed teams
- Communication needs: Real-time collaboration, document sharing
- External collaboration: Partner, vendor, customer access

Scalability Considerations:
- Current team size and growth projections
- Data volume and performance requirements
- Integration needs across program components
```

#### 2. Communication and Collaboration Tools
```
COLLABORATION TOOL TAILORING

Team Size:
- Small Teams (<10): Simple tools, basic collaboration features
- Medium Teams (10-50): Moderate tools, workflow automation
- Large Teams (>50): Advanced tools, enterprise features

Geographic Distribution:
- Single Location: Basic collaboration tools, in-person focus
- Multiple Locations: Virtual collaboration tools, communication focus
- Global Distribution: Advanced virtual tools, time zone coordination

Security Requirements:
- Low Security: Standard commercial tools, basic security
- Medium Security: Enterprise tools, enhanced security
- High Security: Specialized tools, advanced security, compliance features
```

```
METRICS TAILORING FRAMEWORK
```

## Continuous Tailoring Improvement

### Tailoring Effectiveness Assessment

#### 1. Tailoring Review Framework
```
TAILORING EFFECTIVENESS REVIEW

Quarterly Tailoring Reviews:
- Approach effectiveness assessment
- Tool utilization and satisfaction evaluation
- Process efficiency and effectiveness measurement
- Stakeholder feedback and satisfaction analysis

Tailoring Adjustment Triggers:
- Performance issues or problems
- Stakeholder feedback or concerns
- Environmental changes or disruptions
- Learning and improvement opportunities

Continuous Improvement Process:
1. Effectiveness Assessment: Measure current approach effectiveness
2. Gap Analysis: Identify gaps between needs and current approach
3. Improvement Options: Develop potential improvement approaches
4. Pilot Testing: Test improvements in controlled environment
5. Implementation: Roll out successful improvements broadly
6. Monitoring: Track improvement effectiveness and results
```

#### 2. Learning and Adaptation
```
TAILORING LEARNING FRAMEWORK

Experience Capture:
- Lessons learned from tailoring decisions
- Success factors and effective practices
- Challenges and ineffective approaches
- Improvement opportunities and innovations

Knowledge Sharing:
- Best practice documentation and sharing
- Tailoring patterns and templates
- Training and capability development
- Community of practice participation

Adaptation Process:
- Regular environmental scanning and assessment
- Context change identification and analysis
- Tailoring approach evaluation and adjustment
- Continuous learning and improvement integration
```

## Key Takeaways

1. **Tailoring is context-dependent**: Effective program management requires adapting approaches to fit organizational context, program characteristics, and environmental factors.

2. **No one-size-fits-all approach**: Different programs require different approaches based on their unique characteristics and requirements.

3. **Balance consistency and flexibility**: Programs need appropriate balance between standardization for coordination and flexibility for adaptation.

4. **Governance must fit complexity**: Governance structures should match program complexity and organizational culture.

5. **Leadership style matters**: Different situations and team compositions require different leadership approaches.

6. **Tools should enable, not constrain**: Tools and processes should support program success rather than create unnecessary overhead.

7. **Tailoring is continuous**: Regular assessment and adaptation ensure approaches remain effective as conditions change.
