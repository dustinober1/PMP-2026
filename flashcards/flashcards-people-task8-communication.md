## Communication Basics & Formats

### Card 1
**Front**: What percentage of a Project Manager's time is spent communicating?
**Back**: Approximately **90%**.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Plan and manage communication

### Card 2
**Front**: Define "Interactive Communication".
**Back**: Real-time, multi-directional exchange (e.g., meetings, calls, video). Best for ensuring understanding.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Plan and manage communication

### Card 3
**Front**: Define "Push Communication".
**Back**: Sent to specific recipients but ensures no verification of understanding (e.g., Email, Status Reports).
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Plan and manage communication

### Card 4
**Front**: Define "Pull Communication".
**Back**: Information placed in a central location for recipients to access at their own discretion (e.g., Intranet, SharePoint).
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Plan and manage communication

### Card 5
**Front**: When should you use "Formal Written" communication?
**Back**: For complex problems, project management plans, project charter, contracts, and legal notices.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Plan and manage communication

### Card 6
**Front**: When is "Informal Verbal" communication appropriate?
**Back**: For quick status checks, team building, and solving simple problems quickly.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Plan and manage communication

### Card 7
**Front**: What is the formula for "Communication Channels"?
**Back**: **N * (N - 1) / 2**, where N is the number of stakeholders.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Plan and manage communication

### Card 8
**Front**: If a team grows from 4 to 5 people, how many *new* channels are added?
**Back**: Old: 4(3)/2 = 6. New: 5(4)/2 = 10. **4 new channels**.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Plan and manage communication

## The Communications Management Plan

### Card 9
**Front**: What is the primary purpose of the "Communications Management Plan"?
**Back**: To document **How**, **When**, and **By Whom** information will be administered and disseminated.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Plan and manage communication

### Card 10
**Front**: Does the Communications Management Plan contain the actual emails sent?
**Back**: No. It contains the *guidelines*, *templates*, and *flow charts* for sending them.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Plan and manage communication

### Card 11
**Front**: What are "Communication Constraints"?
**Back**: Limitations such as technology availability, security clearance, or legislation (GDPR) that restrict how you communicate.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Plan and manage communication

### Card 12
**Front**: The "Glossary of Terms" is a component of which plan?
**Back**: The Communications Management Plan. It prevents "Semantic Noise" by defining acronyms.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Plan and manage communication

### Card 13
**Front**: Who defines the specific information needs (format, frequency)?
**Back**: The **Stakeholders**. You don't guess; you analyze their requirements from the Stakeholder Register.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Plan and manage communication

## Communication Models & Theory

### Card 14
**Front**: In the basic communication model, who is responsible for the clarity of the message?
**Back**: The **Sender**.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Plan and manage communication

### Card 15
**Front**: What is "Encoding"?
**Back**: Translating thoughts into language/symbols.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Plan and manage communication

### Card 16
**Front**: What is "Decoding"?
**Back**: Translating the language/symbols back into thoughts. (Done by Receiver).
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Plan and manage communication

### Card 17
**Front**: What is "Noise"?
**Back**: Anything that compromises the transmission or understanding of the message (e.g., distance, hostility, language, bad connection).
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Plan and manage communication

### Card 18
**Front**: What constitutes "Feedback" in the model?
**Back**: The signal sent back to the Sender acknowledging receipt or providing a response.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Plan and manage communication

### Card 19
**Front**: What is "Paralinguistic" communication?
**Back**: Tone of voice, pitch, and volume. (Visual cues are "Nonverbal").
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Plan and manage communication

### Card 20
**Front**: In face-to-face communication, words account for approximately what % of meaning?
**Back**: Very low (~7%). Use of Tone (38%) and Body Language (55%) is dominant. (Albert Mehrabian's rule).
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Plan and manage communication

## Virtual & Global Teams

### Card 21
**Front**: What is the biggest challenge for Virtual Teams regarding communication?
**Back**: Loss of nonverbal cues (Body language).
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Plan and manage communication

### Card 22
**Front**: How do you mitigate "Isolation" in remote teams?
**Back**: Schedule regular "face-time" (video) and informal interactions (virtual coffee).
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Plan and manage communication

### Card 23
**Front**: What is "asynchronous communication"?
**Back**: Communication where response is not immediate (e.g., Email, Slack threads). Essential for cross-time-zone teams.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Plan and manage communication

### Card 24
**Front**: What is "High-Context" culture communication?
**Back**: Meaning is embedded in the situation/relationship, not just words. (e.g., Japan/Arab/Latin).
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Plan and manage communication

### Card 25
**Front**: What is "Low-Context" culture communication?
**Back**: Meaning is explicitly in the words. Blunt, direct. (e.g., USA/Germany/Scandinavia).
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Plan and manage communication

### Card 26
**Front**: Scenario: A team member from a High-Context culture says "That will be difficult."
**Back**: They likely mean "No." A Low-Context PM might misinterpret this as "We can do it with effort."
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Plan and manage communication

## Managing Communication (Execution)

### Card 27
**Front**: What is "Manage Communications"?
**Back**: The process of *doing* the work: creating, collecting, distributing, storing, and disposing of project information.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Plan and manage communication

### Card 28
**Front**: "Project Reporting" is an output of which process?
**Back**: Manage Communications. (The Reports themselves).
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Plan and manage communication

### Card 29
**Front**: If you are hosting a Status Meeting, are you Planning or Managing Communication?
**Back**: **Managing**. You are executing the plan.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Plan and manage communication

### Card 30
**Front**: You notice the team is using an outdated template for reports. What do you do?
**Back**: Update the **Organizational Process Assets (OPA)** or enforce the current Communications Management Plan.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Plan and manage communication

### Card 31
**Front**: A stakeholder requests a daily report, but the plan says "Weekly." What do you do?
**Back**: Analyze the request. If valid, update the **Communications Management Plan**. Don't just say yes (Scope Creep of coms).
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Plan and manage communication

## 5 C's of Communication

### Card 32
**Front**: What are the "5 C's" of written communication?
**Back**: **Correct** grammar/spelling, **Concise** expression, **Clear** purpose, **Coherent** logical flow, **Controlling** flow of ideas.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Plan and manage communication

### Card 33
**Front**: Why is "Concise" important?
**Back**: Executives are busy. Long messages are "Noise" and often ignored.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Plan and manage communication

### Card 34
**Front**: "Coherent" means...
**Back**: Using markers like "Introduction" and "Summary" so the reader doesn't get lost.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Plan and manage communication

## Scenarios: Difficult Conversations

### Card 40
**Front**: You have to deliver bad news to the Sponsor. Method?
**Back**: **Face-to-Face** (or Video). Never email bad news if possible. It requires managing the emotional reaction.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Plan and manage communication

### Card 41
**Front**: Two team members are emailing angry messages back and forth.
**Back**: Stop the email chain. Switch to **Interactive** (Phone/Meeting) to resolve the conflict.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Plan and manage communication

### Card 42
**Front**: A senior stakeholder dominates all meetings.
**Back**: Facilitation technique needed. Use specific agendas and time-boxing, or "Round Robin" speaking.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Plan and manage communication

### Card 43
**Front**: You need to record a decision for legal reasons. Method?
**Back**: **Formal Written**. (Meeting minutes, Memo, Email confirmation).
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Plan and manage communication

### Card 44
**Front**: A Virtual Team member is usually silent.
**Back**: actively invite them to speak ("Tag" them). They might be waiting for a pause that never comes (Cultural latency).
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Plan and manage communication

## Hard Scenarios

### Card 66
**Front**: Scenario: You send an email, and the recipient doesn't reply. Is communication complete?
**Back**: No. The "Feedback Loop" (Acknowledgment) is missing. You cannot assume they read/understood it.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Plan and manage communication

### Card 67
**Front**: Scenario: The customer speaks a different language.
**Back**: Plan for translation services (Budget/Schedule impact). This is an "Enterprise Environmental Factor" (EEF).
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Plan and manage communication

### Card 68
**Front**: Scenario: A team member refuses to use the project portal (Pull).
**Back**: Coaching opportunity. Explain the "Why." If resistance continues, it's a performance issue.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Plan and manage communication

### Card 69
**Front**: Scenario: You find out a stakeholder has been getting "secret" updates from a developer.
**Back**: Breakdown in communication plan. Re-align the team on the single source of truth to avoid mixed messages.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Plan and manage communication

### Card 70
**Front**: Scenario: A major milestone is missed. The Sponsor reads about it in the newsletter.
**Back**: **FAILURE**. Bad news must be delivered personally/Interactive *before* the general report. "No Surprises."
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Plan and manage communication

## 2026 Skills: Hybrid & Remote

### Card 71
**Front**: How do you time-box a "Standup" in a virtual team?
**Back**: Strictly enforce the "3 Questions" rule. Take side conversations "Offline" (Post-meeting separate call).
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Plan and manage communication

### Card 72
**Front**: "Video Fatigue" (Zoom Fatigue).
**Back**: A recognized issue. Mitigation: Allow "Camera Off" for non-critical meetings or shorten durations.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Plan and manage communication

### Card 73
**Front**: "Presence Indicator".
**Back**: Status (Available, Busy, DND) in chat tools. Respecting this is part of virtual communication etiquette.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Plan and manage communication

### Card 74
**Front**: "Threaded Conversations".
**Back**: Keeping topics organized in chat (Slack). Preventing the "Wall of Text" chaos.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Plan and manage communication

### Card 75
**Front**: What is the "Golden Rule" of Virtual Communication?
**Back**: Over-communicate (Assume nothing is obvious) and Assume Positive Intent (Text lacks tone).
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Plan and manage communication
