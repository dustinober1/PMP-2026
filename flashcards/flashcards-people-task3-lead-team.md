### Card 4

### Card 5

**Domain**: People | **Task**: Lead the project team

**Domain**: People | **Task**: Lead the project team

### Card 8
**Front**: Describe the **Norming** stage of Tuckman's ladder.
**Back**: Team starts to resolve differences, appreciate strengths, and respect the leader. Productivity increases.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Lead the project team

### Card 9
**Front**: Describe the **Performing** stage of Tuckman's ladder.

## Motivation Theories

### Card 11
**Front**: Summarize **Herzberg's Two-Factor Theory**.
**Back**: Hygiene Factors (pay, safety) prevent dissatisfaction but don't motivate. Motivators (growth, recognition) drive performance.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Lead the project team

### Card 12
**Front**: According to Herzberg, will a salary increase motivate high performance in the long run?
**Back**: No. Money is a Hygiene factor. It stops them from quitting (dissatisfaction), but doesn't create drive (motivation).
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Lead the project team

### Card 13
**Front**: Summarize **McClelland's Acquired Needs Theory**.
**Back**: People are driven by one dominant need: Achievement (nAch), Affiliation (nAff), or Power (nPow).
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Lead the project team

### Card 14
**Front**: How do you motivate someone with high **nAch** (Achievement)?
**Back**: Give them challenging problems, clear feedback, and personal responsibility.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Lead the project team

### Card 15
**Front**: How do you motivate someone with high **nAff** (Affiliation)?
**Back**: Place them in a collaborative team environment; focus on relationships and harmony.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Lead the project team

### Card 16
**Front**: How do you motivate someone with high **nPow** (Power)?
**Back**: Give them leadership roles, influence, or the chance to present to executives.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Lead the project team

### Card 17
**Front**: Summarize **Maslow's Hierarchy of Needs**.
**Back**: Needs are met in order: Physiological -> Safety -> Social/Belonging -> Esteem -> Self-Actualization.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Lead the project team

### Card 18
**Front**: Summarize **McGregor's Theory X vs. Theory Y**.
**Back**: Theory X = "People are lazy; need control" (Dictator). Theory Y = "People want to grow; need support" (Servant Leader).
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Lead the project team

### Card 19
**Front**: Summarize **Pink's Drive** theory (3 elements).
**Back**: Autonomy (control over work), Mastery (getting better), Purpose (meaningful contribution).
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Lead the project team

### Card 20
**Front**: Summarize **Vroom's Expectancy Theory**.
**Back**: Motivation = Expectancy (I can do it) x Instrumentality (I will get the reward) x Valence (I want the reward).
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Lead the project team

### Card 21
**Front**: Summarize **Adams' Equity Theory**.
**Back**: People compare their effort/reward ratio to others. If they perceive unfairness (inequity), motivation drops.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Lead the project team

### Card 22
**Front**: What is **Ouchi's Theory Z**?
**Back**: Japanese management style focused on long-term loyalty, collective decision-making, and holistic well-being.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Lead the project team

## Leadership Styles

### Card 23
**Front**: What is **Servant Leadership**?
**Back**: A leadership philosophy where the leader's primary goal is to serve the team—remove blockers, provide resources, and shield them from distraction.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Lead the project team

### Card 24
**Front**: What is **Laissez-Faire** leadership?
**Back**: "Hands-off." The team makes all decisions. Effective for highly skilled experts; dangerous for inexperienced teams.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Lead the project team

### Card 25
**Front**: What is **Transactional** leadership?
**Back**: Focuses on rewards and punishments to ensure compliance (quid pro quo).
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Lead the project team

### Card 26
**Front**: What is **Transformational** leadership?
**Back**: Inspires the team with a vision and encourages innovation/change.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Lead the project team

### Card 27
**Front**: What is **Situational Leadership**?
**Back**: Adapting your style (Directing, Coaching, Supporting, Delegating) based on the team member's competence and commitment.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Lead the project team

## Leading Virtual Teams

### Card 28
**Front**: What is the #1 challenge for virtual teams?
**Back**: Communication (lack of non-verbal cues) and Trust.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Lead the project team

### Card 29
**Front**: How do you handle timezone differences in a virtual team?
**Back**: Share the pain (rotate meeting times), use asynchronous communication for status, and overlap hours for collaboration.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Lead the project team

### Card 30
**Front**: What defines a "Fishbowl Window" in virtual teams?
**Back**: A scheduled time when all team members (regardless of location) are online simultaneously for real-time collaboration.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Lead the project team

## Performance Management

### Card 31
**Front**: What is the first step when a team member underperforms?
**Back**: Meet privately to understand the root cause (Diagnose before prescribing).
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Lead the project team

### Card 32
**Front**: What is a PIP (Performance Improvement Plan)?
**Back**: A formal document for serious/persistent issues involving clear standards, timelines, and consequences. It is a LAST RESORT on the exam (after coaching fails).
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Lead the project team

### Card 33
**Front**: If a team member lacks the skill to do the job, what is the correct response?
**Back**: Provide training, pair programming, or mentoring (Help them succeed). Do not fire them immediately.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Lead the project team

### Card 34
**Front**: If a team member has the skill but lacks the will (motivation), what is the response?
**Back**: Find out what motivates them (Theories) or if there is a hygiene blocker (burnout, equipment).
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Lead the project team

### Card 35
**Front**: What is the "Recognition Formula" for high impact?
**Back**: Specific + Timely + Personal + Proportional.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Lead the project team

## Agile Leadership & Ceremonies

### Card 36
**Front**: What is the **Sprint Planning** ceremony?
**Back**: Developing the plan for the sprint. PO defines "What", Team defines "How". Output: Sprint Goal + Sprint Backlog.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Lead the project team

### Card 37
**Front**: What is the **Daily Scrum** (Standup)?
**Back**: A 15-minute alignment meeting for the Developers. "What did I do? What will I do? Any blockers?"
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Lead the project team

### Card 38
**Front**: Who runs the Daily Scrum?
**Back**: The Development Team. The Scrum Master ensures it happens but does not "run" it.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Lead the project team

### Card 39
**Front**: What is the **Sprint Review** (Demo)?
**Back**: Inspecting the working increment with stakeholders at the end of the sprint to get feedback.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Lead the project team

### Card 40
**Front**: What is the **Sprint Retrospective**?
**Back**: The team inspects ITSELF (processes, relationships) to identify improvements for the next sprint.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Lead the project team

### Card 41
**Front**: If the team consistently fails to finish work in a sprint, what should happen?
**Back**: Discuss in Retrospective. Likely root causes: Overcommitment, unclear stories, or external interruptions.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Lead the project team

### Card 42
**Front**: What is the "Definition of Ready"?
**Back**: Criteria that a user story must meet BEFORE it is accepted into a sprint (e.g., clear acceptance criteria, dependencies resolved).
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Lead the project team

### Card 43
**Front**: What is the "Definition of Done"?
**Back**: The quality checklist that must be met for a work item to be considered complete (e.g., coded, tested, documented).
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Lead the project team

## Execution & Flow

### Card 44
**Front**: What is a **Work Authorization System** (WAS)?
**Back**: A predictive tool to ensure work is done at the right time and in the right sequence. Prevents "gold plating" or starting without approval.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Lead the project team

### Card 45
**Front**: What is the difference between an **Impediment** and a **Risk**?
**Back**: Impediment = Blocking you NOW. Risk = May block you in the FUTURE.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Lead the project team

### Card 46
**Front**: What is the difference between an **Issue** and a **Risk**?
**Back**: Issue = Has happened (Facts). Risk = Might happen (Uncertainty).
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Lead the project team

### Card 47
**Front**: A risk has occurred. What do you do?
**Back**: 1) Execute the risk response plan, 2) Log it as an Issue, 3) Communicate.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Lead the project team

### Card 48
**Front**: What is **Knowledge Management** in execution?
**Back**: Ensuring "Explicit" knowledge (docs) and "Tacit" knowledge (experience) are shared to prevent repeating mistakes.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Lead the project team

### Card 49
**Front**: How do you transfer Tacit Knowledge?
**Back**: Mentoring, pairing, shadowing, communities of practice, and social interaction. (You can't just write it down).
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Lead the project team

### Card 50
**Front**: When should Lessons Learned be captured?
**Back**: Continuously! (e.g., at every Retrospective or phase gate), not just at the end of the project.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Lead the project team

## Key Terms & Concepts

### Card 51
**Front**: What is **Velocity**?
**Back**: The measure of how much work a team can complete in a sprint (usually story points). Used for forecasting.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Lead the project team

### Card 52
**Front**: What is **Burnup Chart**?
**Back**: Shows work completed vs. total work over time. Tracks progress toward a goal.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Lead the project team

### Card 53
**Front**: What is a **Burndown Chart**?
**Back**: Shows work REMAINING over time. Ideally trends down to zero.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Lead the project team

### Card 54
**Front**: What is a **Kanban Board**?
**Back**: A visual tool to track work flow (To Do -> In Progress -> Done) and limit Work In Progress (WIP).
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Lead the project team

### Card 55
**Front**: What is **WIP Limit**?
**Back**: A constraint on the number of items allowed in a specific stage. Prevents bottlenecks and context switching.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Lead the project team

### Card 56
**Front**: What is **Resource Smoothing**?
**Back**: Adjusting activities within their float to minimize resource fluctuation. Does NOT change the critical path.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Lead the project team

### Card 57
**Front**: What is **Resource Leveling**?
**Back**: Balancing demand with supply. Often extends the schedule (changes critical path) because resources are constrained.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Lead the project team

### Card 58
**Front**: What is **EI** (Emotional Intelligence) and its 4 domains?
**Back**: Self-Awareness, Self-Management, Social Awareness, Relationship Management. Critical for leadership.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Lead the project team

### Card 59
**Front**: What is **Colocation**?
**Back**: Placing team members in the same physical space ("War Room") to enhance communication (Osmotic Communication).
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Lead the project team

### Card 60
**Front**: What is **Osmotic Communication**?
**Back**: Information absorbed by overhearing conversations in a shared space. A benefit of colocation.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Lead the project team

## Problem Solving Scenarios

### Card 61
**Front**: Scenario: A senior developer is refusing to mentor junior staff.
**Back**: Check motivation (WIIFM). Explain the value. Make mentoring part of their performance goals (nPow or nAch).
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Lead the project team

### Card 62
**Front**: Scenario: The team is burnt out and quality is dropping.
**Back**: Protect the team. Reduce scope/WIP. Ensure breaks. Servant Leadership.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Lead the project team

### Card 63
**Front**: Scenario: A functional manager pulls a key resource away.
**Back**: Negotiate. Explain the impact on the project. Do not just "accept" it without communicating the consequence (Risk -> Issue).
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Lead the project team

### Card 64
**Front**: Scenario: Two team members have a "personality clash."
**Back**: It's likely a Process issue (roles/goals) in disguise. Investigate the root cause before treating it as personal only.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Lead the project team

### Card 65
**Front**: Scenario: Stakeholders keep asking team members for "small favors" securely.
**Back**: Stop it. Channel requests through the backlog/PO/Change Control. Protect the team's focus.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Lead the project team

### Card 66
**Front**: Scenario: A team member dominates the Daily Standup.
**Back**: Coach them offline. Remind them of the purpose (alignment, not status report). Enforce the timebox.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Lead the project team

### Card 67
**Front**: Scenario: A team member is silent during Retrospectives.
**Back**: Safety issue. Use anonymous techniques (writing on cards) or small groups to encourage participation.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Lead the project team

### Card 68
**Front**: Scenario: You inherit a team that is used to being micromanaged.
**Back**: Start with "Theory Y" but provide structure. Gradually increase autonomy as they build confidence (Situational Leadership).
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Lead the project team

## Recognition & Rewards

### Card 69
**Front**: Should you reward individual heroics in an Agile team?
**Back**: Be careful. Agile emphasizes TEAM success. Rewarding individual heroes can undermine collaboration. Reward the TEAM.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Lead the project team

### Card 70
**Front**: What is a "Hygiene Factor" regarding rewards?
**Back**: Salary/Safety. If these are missing, people leave. But adding more doesn't necessarily make them work harder.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Lead the project team

### Card 71
**Front**: Why is "Public Recognition" not always the best choice?
**Back**: Introverts may feel embarrassed. Always tailor recognition to the individual's preference.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Lead the project team

## 2026 Skills focus

### Card 72
**Front**: What is the "T-Shaped Skills" concept?
**Back**: Deep expertise in one area (vertical bar) + broad ability to collaborate across other areas (horizontal bar). Ideal for Agile teams.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Lead the project team

### Card 73
**Front**: What is an "I-Shaped" person?
**Back**: Specialist. Deep knowledge in one area only. Can create bottlenecks.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Lead the project team

### Card 74
**Front**: What is "Swarming"?
**Back**: When multiple team members work together on a single blocked or high-priority item to finish it faster.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Lead the project team

### Card 75
**Front**: What is "Facilitation"?
**Back**: The art of guiding a group to a decision or outcome without dictating the content. A core PM skill.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Lead the project team

### Card 76
**Front**: What is "Coaching"?
**Back**: Helping someone solve their own problems and grow, rather than giving them the answers.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Lead the project team

### Card 77
**Front**: What is "Mentoring"?
**Back**: Sharing experience and advice to help someone develop professionally (usually a longer-term relationship than coaching).
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Lead the project team

## Training & Growth

### Card 90
**Front**: When is Training a project cost?
**Back**: If the skills are needed specifically for the project deliverables, training costs are part of the project budget.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Lead the project team

### Card 91
**Front**: What is "Cross-Training"?
**Back**: Teaching team members each other's jobs. Reduces risk (key person dependency) and increases flexibility (T-shaped skills).
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Lead the project team

### Card 92
**Front**: If a team member asks for training unrelated to the project, what do you do?
**Back**: Check policy. If it motivates them (Growth) and fits the budget/time, it can be a good reward. If strictly project-focused, clarify the boundary.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Lead the project team

## Exam "Mindset" Checks

### Card 93
**Front**: Mindset: A problem arises. What is your FIRST instinct?
**Back**: Analyze/Understand. (Don't Act/Fire/Replace/Escalate until you understand).
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Lead the project team

### Card 94
**Front**: Mindset: You have a choice between "Following the Plan" and "Responding to Change" (Agile value).
**Back**: Respond to change (if it adds value). The plan serves the project; the project doesn't serve the plan.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Lead the project team

### Card 95
**Front**: Mindset: You have a choice between "Emailing" and "Talking Face-to-Face".
**Back**: Face-to-Face (or video). High-bandwidth communication is preferred.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Lead the project team

### Card 96
**Front**: Mindset: A team member makes a mistake. Do you punish them?
**Back**: No. You use it as a learning opportunity (Retrospective/Lesson Learned). Punishment kills psychological safety.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Lead the project team

### Card 97
**Front**: Mindset: The sponsor wants to cut quality to meet a deadline.
**Back**: Push back. Quality is usually non-negotiable (Scope/Time/Cost trade-offs are okay, but professional standards must be maintained).
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Lead the project team

### Card 98
**Front**: Mindset: Who is accountable for the *quality* of the work?
**Back**: The Team (Agile) or the person doing the work (Predictive). The PM is accountable for the *process* of quality.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Lead the project team

### Card 99
**Front**: Mindset: Who defines "Value"?
**Back**: The Customer (or Product Owner representing them). Not the PM.
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Lead the project team

### Card 100
**Front**: Mindset: Lead the ______; Manage the ______.
**Back**: Lead the **People**; Manage the **Process/Work**. (You don't "manage" people like inventory).
**Domain**: People | **Task**: Lead the project team
