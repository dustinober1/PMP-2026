# PMP Study Progress Tracking Template

## Overview

This template helps you track your PMP exam preparation progress across multiple practice exams, study sessions, and knowledge areas. Use it to identify trends, monitor improvement, and make data-driven decisions about your study approach.

## How to Use This Template

1. **Record each practice exam** in the Practice Exam Log
2. **Update domain performance** after each exam
3. **Track study activities** in the Study Log
4. **Review trends monthly** to adjust your study plan
5. **Use readiness indicators** to determine when you're exam-ready

## Readiness Assessment Framework

### Quantitative Indicators

**Practice Exam Performance** (Check when achieved):
- [ ] Overall score 70%+ on at least 3 different practice exams
- [ ] Overall score 75%+ on at least 2 recent practice exams
- [ ] All domains at 60%+ (Target level or above)
- [ ] No domain below 50% in last 2 exams
- [ ] Consistent performance (scores within 10% range)

**Time Management** (Check when achieved):
- [ ] Completing 185 questions in 230 minutes or less
- [ ] Average 1.2 minutes per question or faster
- [ ] Time remaining for review (at least 15 minutes)
- [ ] Comfortable pacing without rushing

**Knowledge Mastery** (Check when achieved):
- [ ] Confident in all major formulas (EVM, PERT, Communication Channels)
- [ ] Can distinguish between similar concepts (e.g., different leadership styles)
- [ ] Understand PMI's preferred approaches to common scenarios
- [ ] Can analyze scenario-based questions effectively

### Study Plan Adjustments

Based on performance trends, adjust your study approach:

**If Improving Consistently**:
- [ ] Continue current approach
- [ ] Gradually increase practice exam frequency
- [ ] Focus on maintaining strong areas
- [ ] Begin final review phase

- [ ] Increase practice question volume
- [ ] Consider study group or tutor

**If Performance Declining**:
- [ ] Review study effectiveness
- [ ] Reduce study session length, increase frequency
- [ ] Return to fundamental concepts
- [ ] Take break to avoid burnout

## Final Exam Preparation Checklist

### Two Weeks Before Exam
- [ ] Consistently scoring 75%+ on practice exams
- [ ] All weak areas addressed
- [ ] Time management under control
- [ ] Exam logistics confirmed (date, time, location)

### One Week Before Exam
- [ ] Final practice exam completed
- [ ] Light review of weak areas only
- [ ] Day-before summary reviewed
- [ ] Relaxation and confidence building

### Day Before Exam
- [ ] No intensive studying
- [ ] Light review of key formulas and concepts
- [ ] Good night's sleep
- [ ] Positive mindset and confidence

### Exam Day
- [ ] Arrive early and relaxed
- [ ] Bring required identification
- [ ] Trust preparation and instincts
- [ ] Use practiced time management strategies

---

*Remember: Progress is not always linear. Focus on overall trends rather than individual exam results. Consistent preparation and gradual improvement lead to exam success.*
