### The Process Groups Framework

The Process Groups framework consists of five groups that represent the typical lifecycle of a project:

1. **Initiating**: Defining and authorizing the project or phase
2. **Planning**: Establishing the scope, refining objectives, and defining actions
3. **Executing**: Completing work defined in the project management plan
4. **Monitoring & Controlling**: Tracking, reviewing, and revising progress
5. **Closing**: Finalizing all activities across all Process Groups

These Process Groups are **not project phases** - they are overlapping activities that occur throughout the project lifecycle.

### Mapping Process Groups to Exam Domains

| Process Group | People Domain | Process Domain | Business Environment Domain |
|---------------|---------------|----------------|----------------------------|
| **Initiating** | Stakeholder identification, team formation | Charter development, high-level planning | Business case validation, strategic alignment |
| **Planning** | Team building, communication planning | Detailed planning processes | Compliance, risk assessment, organizational change |
| **Executing** | Team leadership, stakeholder engagement | Work execution, quality assurance | Organizational change management, value delivery |
| **Monitoring & Controlling** | Communication, conflict resolution | Performance measurement, change control | Governance, compliance monitoring, benefits tracking |
| **Closing** | Team transition, lessons learned | Administrative closure, contract closure | Benefits realization, organizational learning |
