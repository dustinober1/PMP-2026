## Introduction

While often treated separately in theory, Executing and Monitoring & Controlling Process Groups work in parallel in practice. As work is performed, it must be continuously monitored, controlled, and adjusted. This section covers both groups together to reflect their iterative, interconnected nature in real project management.

### Domain Connections

**People Domain (35% of executing/monitoring activities):**
- Team leadership and development
- Stakeholder engagement and communication
- Conflict resolution and negotiation
- Performance management and feedback

**Process Domain (45% of executing/monitoring activities):**
- Work execution and quality assurance
- Performance measurement and reporting
- Change control and configuration management
- Risk monitoring and response

**Business Environment Domain (20% of executing/monitoring activities):**
- Value delivery measurement
- Compliance monitoring
- Benefits realization tracking
- Organizational change management

## Part 1: Executing Process Group

The Executing Process Group completes the work defined in the project management plan to satisfy project requirements. This is where value is actually created and delivered.

### 1. Directing and Managing Project Work

**Purpose and Activities:**
- Execute planned activities and deliverables
- Manage technical and organizational interfaces
- Implement approved changes
- Collect work performance data
- Manage workflow and processes

**Key Execution Activities:**
1. **Task Assignment**: Distribute work to team members
2. **Work Coordination**: Ensure smooth workflow
3. **Issue Resolution**: Address immediate problems
4. **Status Reporting**: Collect and report progress
5. **Quality Assurance**: Ensure quality standards are met

**Work Performance Data Collection:**
- **Technical Performance**: Metrics on product/service quality
- **Schedule Performance**: Activity completion dates
- **Cost Performance**: Actual costs incurred
- **Resource Utilization**: How resources are being used
- **Risk Status**: New risks and risk response effectiveness

**Sarah's Execution Examples:**

*Office Renovation (Predictive):*
- Daily construction activities management
- Subcontractor coordination
- Inspection scheduling and facilitation
- Progress reporting to steering committee
- Quality inspections at each milestone

*Expense Reporting System (Agile):*
- Daily sprint execution
- User story implementation
- Sprint reviews and demos
- Daily standup meetings
- Continuous integration and deployment

*Global Transformation (Hybrid):*
- Phased rollout execution
- Training delivery
- Local implementation support
- Progress tracking across regions
- Stakeholder feedback collection

### 2. Managing Quality Assurance

**Quality Assurance vs. Quality Control:**
- **Quality Assurance (QA)**: Process-focused, preventive
- **Quality Control (QC)**: Product-focused, detective
- **QA**: Are we doing the right things?
- **QC**: Did we get the desired results?

**Quality Assurance Activities:**
- **Process Audits**: Verify adherence to processes
- **Quality Audits**: Systematic review of quality activities
- **Process Analysis**: Identify process improvements
- **Quality Training**: Build team quality capabilities
- **Root Cause Analysis**: Address systemic issues

**Quality Management Tools:**
- **Flowcharting**: Visualize processes
- **Check Sheets**: Data collection tools
- **Pareto Charts**: Identify vital few issues
- **Cause-and-Effect Diagrams**: Identify root causes
- **Histograms**: Display frequency distributions
- **Control Charts**: Monitor process variation
- **Scatter Diagrams**: Show relationships between variables

**Continuous Improvement:**
- **Kaizen Events**: Focused improvement activities
- **Lessons Learned**: Capture and share knowledge
- **Best Practices**: Document and share successful approaches
- **Process Optimization**: Streamline processes

### 3. Acquiring and Developing the Project Team

**Team Acquisition Process:**
1. **Pre-assignment**: Resources assigned in charter
2. **Negotiation**: Obtain resources from functional managers
3. **Acquisition**: Hire or contract resources
4. **Virtual Teams**: Assemble geographically distributed teams

**Team Development Activities:**
- **Training**: Skill development and knowledge transfer
- **Team Building**: Activities to build cohesion
- **Recognition**: Reward and acknowledgment
- **Collocation**: Physical or virtual co-location
- **Ground Rules**: Establish team norms and expectations

**Team Development Stages (Tuckman Model):**
1. **Forming**: Team comes together, polite and uncertain
2. **Storming**: Conflict and disagreement emerge
3. **Norming**: Establish processes and procedures
4. **Performing**: High performance and effectiveness
5. **Adjourning**: Team disbands (for temporary teams)

**Virtual Team Management:**
- **Communication Planning**: Extra attention to communication needs
- **Technology Infrastructure**: Ensure proper tools and systems
- **Cultural Awareness**: Understand cultural differences
- **Trust Building**: Create trust without physical proximity
- **Time Zone Management**: Coordinate across time zones

### 4. Managing Communications

**Information Distribution Methods:**
- **Formal Written**: Reports, memos, emails
- **Informal Written**: Notes, messages
- **Formal Verbal**: Presentations, meetings
- **Informal Verbal**: Conversations, discussions

**Communication Management:**
- **Information Distribution**: Ensure information reaches stakeholders
- **Communication Effectiveness**: Verify understanding
- **Stakeholder Engagement**: Maintain stakeholder involvement
- **Knowledge Management**: Capture and share project knowledge

**Communication Tools and Techniques:**
- **Communication Technology**: Email, messaging, video conferencing
- **Project Management Information System (PMIS)**: Central information hub
- **Collaboration Platforms**: Shared workspaces and documents
- **Social Media**: Professional networking and information sharing

**Managing Communication Challenges:**
- **Information Overload**: Filter and prioritize information
- **Cultural Differences**: Adapt communication styles
- **Language Barriers**: Use clear, simple language
- **Technical Issues**: Ensure reliable communication systems

### 5. Managing Stakeholder Engagement

**Engagement Activities:**
- **Engagement Strategy Execution**: Implement planned engagement approaches
- **Issue Resolution**: Address stakeholder concerns
- **Expectation Management**: Align expectations with project reality
- **Relationship Building**: Develop trust and credibility
- **Feedback Collection**: Gather and respond to stakeholder feedback

**Stakeholder Engagement Monitoring:**
- **Engagement Level Assessment**: Track current engagement levels
- **Effectiveness Evaluation**: Assess engagement strategy effectiveness
- **Adjustment**: Modify approaches as needed
- **Escalation**: Escalate unresolved issues

**Engagement Techniques:**
- **Regular Meetings**: Status updates and information sharing
- **Workshops**: Collaborative problem-solving
- **Surveys**: Collect stakeholder opinions
- **Focus Groups**: Detailed discussions with stakeholder groups
- **One-on-One Meetings**: Individual stakeholder discussions

### 6. Conducting Procurements

**Procurement Execution Process:**
1. **Seller Selection**: Evaluate and select vendors
2. **Contract Award**: Formalize agreements
3. **Contract Administration**: Manage vendor performance
4. **Performance Monitoring**: Track vendor deliverables
5. **Payment Processing**: Ensure timely payment
6. **Claims Administration**: Handle claims and disputes

**Source Selection Criteria:**
- **Cost**: Total cost of ownership
- **Technical Capability**: Ability to meet technical requirements
- **Past Performance**: Historical performance on similar projects
- **Management Approach**: How vendor will manage work
- **Financial Stability**: Vendor's financial health
- **Capacity**: Resource availability

**Contract Administration:**
- **Performance Reporting**: Monitor vendor performance
- **Quality Control**: Ensure deliverables meet requirements
- **Change Control**: Manage contract changes
- **Risk Management**: Monitor and mitigate vendor risks
- **Relationship Management**: Maintain positive vendor relationship

**Procurement Documentation:**
- **Procurement Management Plan**: How procurements will be managed
- **Procurement Statements of Work**: Detailed work requirements
- **Source Selection Criteria**: How vendors will be evaluated
- **Procurement Documents**: RFPs, RFQs, contracts
- **Change Requests**: Contract modifications
- **Performance Reports**: Vendor performance documentation

## Part 2: Monitoring and Controlling Process Group

The Monitoring and Controlling Process Group tracks, reviews, and regulates the progress and performance of the project, identifies areas requiring changes, and initiates changes.

### 1. Monitoring and Controlling Project Work

**Monitoring Activities:**
- **Performance Data Collection**: Gather work performance information
- **Analysis**: Compare actual performance to planned performance
- **Forecasting**: Predict future performance based on trends
- **Variance Analysis**: Identify deviations from plan
- **Trend Analysis**: Identify patterns in performance

**Control Activities:**
- **Change Control**: Manage changes to baselines
- **Corrective Action**: Address performance problems
- **Preventive Action**: Prevent future problems
- **Defect Repair**: Fix product defects
- **Performance Reporting**: Report project status

**Performance Measurement:**
- **Earned Value Management (EVM)**: Comprehensive performance measurement
- **Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)**: Specific performance metrics
- **Balanced Scorecard**: Multi-dimensional performance measurement
- **Dashboard Reporting**: Visual performance indicators

### 2. Integrated Change Control

**Change Control Process:**
1. **Change Request**: Submit change request
2. **Change Assessment**: Evaluate impact and benefits
3. **Change Review**: CCB or designated authority reviews
4. **Change Decision**: Approve, reject, or defer
5. **Change Implementation**: Implement approved changes
6. **Change Communication**: Inform stakeholders of changes

**Change Request Types:**
- **Corrective Action**: Address actual performance problems
- **Preventive Action**: Prevent potential problems
- **Defect Repair**: Fix product defects
- **Document Updates**: Update project documents
- **Baseline Changes**: Modify project baselines

**Change Control Board (CCB):**
- **Composition**: Relevant stakeholders and decision makers
- **Authority**: Defined decision-making authority
- **Process**: Defined review and approval process
- **Documentation**: Document all decisions and rationale

**Configuration Management:**
- **Configuration Identification**: Identify configuration items
- **Configuration Control**: Manage changes to configuration items
- **Configuration Status Accounting**: Track configuration status
- **Configuration Audits**: Verify configuration integrity

### 3. Scope, Schedule, and Cost Control

**Scope Control:**
- **Scope Verification**: Formal acceptance of completed deliverables
- **Scope Creep Management**: Prevent unauthorized scope changes
- **Change Impact Analysis**: Assess impact of scope changes
- **WBS Updates**: Maintain accurate work breakdown structure

**Schedule Control:**
- **Schedule Performance Analysis**: Compare actual to planned schedule
- **Schedule Compression**: Reduce project duration
- **Resource Optimization**: Balance resource usage
- **Milestone Tracking**: Monitor key project milestones

**Cost Control:**
- **Cost Performance Analysis**: Compare actual to planned costs
- **Forecasting**: Estimate final project cost
- **Cost Variance Analysis**: Identify cost overruns/underruns
- **Budget Management**: Manage project budget effectively

### 4. Quality Control

**Quality Control Activities:**
- **Inspection**: Examine deliverables for defects
- **Testing**: Verify functionality and performance
- **Review**: Examine processes and deliverables
- **Measurement**: Collect quality data
- **Analysis**: Analyze quality data for trends

**Quality Control Tools:**
- **Statistical Process Control (SPC)**: Monitor process variation
- **Control Charts**: Track process performance over time
- **Acceptance Sampling**: Evaluate quality of batches
- **Pareto Analysis**: Identify significant quality issues
- **Root Cause Analysis**: Identify underlying causes of problems

**Quality Metrics:**
- **Defect Density**: Number of defects per unit
- **First Pass Yield**: Percentage of products passing inspection first time
- **Customer Satisfaction**: Measure of customer happiness
- **Process Capability**: Ability of process to meet requirements

### 5. Risk Monitoring

**Risk Monitoring Activities:**
- **Risk Audits**: Examine effectiveness of risk responses
- **Risk Reassessment**: Reevaluate existing risks
- **Trend Analysis**: Identify new risk patterns
- **Technical Performance Measurement**: Track technical achievement
- **Reserve Analysis**: Monitor risk reserve usage

**Risk Response Monitoring:**
- **Effectiveness Assessment**: Evaluate if risk responses are working
- **Secondary Risks**: Identify new risks from risk responses
- **Residual Risks**: Monitor risks that remain after responses
- **Risk Trigger Monitoring**: Watch for risk warning signs

**Risk Reporting:**
- **Risk Register Updates**: Maintain current risk information
- **Risk Reports**: Communicate risk status to stakeholders
- **Risk Dashboards**: Visual risk indicators
- **Risk Meeting Minutes**: Document risk discussions

### 6. Procurement Control

**Procurement Control Activities:**
- **Performance Monitoring**: Track vendor performance
- **Inspection and Audits**: Verify vendor compliance
- **Payment Processing**: Ensure accurate payment processing
- **Claims Administration**: Handle vendor claims and disputes
- **Contract Closure**: Close out contracts when complete

**Vendor Performance Evaluation:**
- **Quality Assessment**: Evaluate quality of deliverables
- **Schedule Performance**: Track on-time delivery
- **Cost Performance**: Monitor cost against contract
- **Relationship Assessment**: Evaluate working relationship
- **Overall Satisfaction**: Assess overall satisfaction

**Contract Modifications:**
- **Change Requests**: Process contract changes
- **Negotiations**: Negotiate changes with vendors
- **Documentation**: Document all contract modifications
- **Approval**: Obtain required approvals for changes

### 7. Stakeholder Engagement Monitoring

**Engagement Monitoring:**
- **Engagement Assessment**: Measure current engagement levels
- **Strategy Evaluation**: Assess effectiveness of engagement strategies
- **Adjustment Planning**: Plan strategy adjustments
- **Communication Review**: Evaluate communication effectiveness

**Stakeholder Satisfaction:**
- **Surveys**: Collect stakeholder feedback
- **Interviews**: Conduct detailed stakeholder discussions
- **Focus Groups**: Facilitate group discussions
- **Observation**: Observe stakeholder behavior

**Issue Management:**
- **Issue Identification**: Identify stakeholder issues
- **Issue Prioritization**: Prioritize issues based on impact
- **Issue Resolution**: Address and resolve issues
- **Issue Documentation**: Document issue resolution

## Integration Between Executing and Monitoring

### Continuous Improvement Cycle

1. **Plan**: Execute planned activities
2. **Do**: Perform the work
3. **Check**: Monitor and measure performance
4. **Act**: Make adjustments and improvements

### Real-Time Decision Making

**Decision Points:**
- **Performance Thresholds**: When to take corrective action
- **Risk Triggers**: When to implement risk responses
- **Change Points**: When to change approach
- **Resource Re-allocation**: When to reassign resources

**Agile Iterative Approach:**
- **Sprint Execution**: Perform work in iterations
- **Daily Monitoring**: Daily standups and progress tracking
- **Sprint Review**: Demonstrate progress and get feedback
- **Retrospective**: Improve processes and approach
- **Adaptation**: Adjust based on feedback and learning

## Key Takeaways

1. **Execution and Monitoring are Parallel**: Not sequential activities
2. **Real-Time Adjustment**: Continuous monitoring enables real-time course correction
3. **Integration is Essential**: All monitoring activities must be integrated
4. **Communication is Critical**: Regular communication keeps stakeholders engaged
5. **Quality is Built In**: Not inspected in after the fact
6. **Change is Inevitable**: Effective change control enables project success

## Common Exam Question Patterns

**Scenario Type 1: Change Control**
- Questions about change request processing
- Focus on change control board authority
- May involve configuration management

**Scenario Type 2: Performance Measurement**
- Questions about earned value calculations
- Focus on variance analysis and forecasting
- May involve performance reporting

**Scenario Type 3: Quality Management**
- Questions about quality assurance vs. control
- Focus on quality tools and techniques
- May involve process improvement

**Scenario Type 4: Risk Monitoring**
- Questions about risk response effectiveness
- Focus on risk triggers and secondary risks
- May involve reserve analysis

**Scenario Type 5: Stakeholder Management**
- Questions about engagement strategy effectiveness
- Focus on moving between engagement levels
- May involve issue escalation
