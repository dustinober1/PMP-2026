## Key Findings

### ✅ Strengths

1. **100% Task Coverage**: All 26 ECO tasks are addressed in the study guide
2. **Well-Distributed**: Content is appropriately distributed across People (33%), Process (41%), Business Environment (26%)
3. **Integrated Approach**: Tasks are woven through multiple chapters, reflecting real-world project management
4. **Narrative Focus**: Story-driven approach with Sarah's three projects makes content relatable and integrated
5. **Methodological Balance**: Predictive, agile, and hybrid approaches are woven throughout
6. **Reference Materials**: Appendix A provides formulas and definitions aligned with exam requirements

### ⚠️ Opportunities for Enhancement

1. **Explicit Task Mapping**: While all tasks are covered, chapters don't explicitly label which ECO tasks they address
   - **Recommendation**: Add "ECO Task Alignment" section to key chapters

2. **Procurement Depth**: Task Process 5 (Plan and Manage Procurement) is covered but somewhat dispersed
   - **Recommendation**: Could benefit from more dedicated procurement content

3. **External Environment Monitoring**: Task Business 8 (Evaluate External Business Environment Changes) is covered but could be more prominent
   - **Recommendation**: Add explicit section on external monitoring and scenario analysis

4. **Continuous Improvement Prominence**: Task Business 6 is adequately covered but somewhat scattered
   - **Recommendation**: Add explicit continuous improvement process section

5. **Knowledge Transfer Explicitness**: Task People 7 is covered through mentoring and closure but could be a dedicated section
   - **Recommendation**: Make knowledge transfer a more explicit focus in Chapter 9
