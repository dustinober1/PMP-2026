# 23f – Knowledge Check

## Introduction

This knowledge check tests your understanding of Process Groups implementation and how they integrate with the three PMP exam domains (People, Process, and Business Environment). The questions are designed to test both conceptual understanding and practical application.

### Question Format

Each question follows this structure:
- **Scenario**: Realistic project management situation
- **Question**: Specific question about the scenario
- **Four Options**: One correct answer and three plausible distractors
- **Explanation**: Why the correct answer is best and why others are not

## Process Groups Questions

### Question 1: Initiating Domain Distribution

**Scenario:**
Sarah is starting her office renovation project. She has been working with stakeholders to identify their requirements, create the business case, and develop the project charter. During the initiating phase, she estimates that she spends about 40% of her time on people-related activities like stakeholder identification and team formation.

**Question:**
What is the approximate percentage of initiating activities that typically relate to the People domain?

**Options:**
A. 23%
B. 35%
C. 42%
D. 52%

**Correct Answer: C. 42%**

**Explanation:**
In the Initiating Process Group, approximately 42% of activities relate to the People domain. This includes stakeholder identification and analysis, team formation, leadership engagement, and communication channel establishment. The Process domain accounts for about 35% (business case validation, charter development), and the Business Environment domain accounts for about 23% (strategic alignment, regulatory assessment).

**Why other options are incorrect:**
- A. 23% represents the Business Environment domain portion
- B. 35% represents the Process domain portion
- D. 52% exceeds the total of all three domains

### Question 2: WBS Creation

**Scenario:**
Michael is creating a Work Breakdown Structure for his software development project. He has identified the major deliverables at Level 1 and is now breaking them down into smaller components. He's wondering how detailed the decomposition should be.

**Question:**
According to WBS best practices, at what level should Michael stop decomposing the work?

**Options:**
A. When activities can be reliably estimated
B. When work packages reach 8-80 hours of effort
C. When all stakeholders approve the structure
D. When the lowest level contains nouns, not verbs

**Correct Answer: B. When work packages reach 8-80 hours of effort**

**Explanation:**
The 8/80 rule (8-80 hours per work package) is a common guideline for WBS decomposition. This level provides adequate detail for planning and control while remaining manageable. Work packages at this level can be assigned to a single individual or team and reliably estimated.

**Why other options are incorrect:**
- A. Reliable estimation is important but not the primary stopping criterion
- C. Stakeholder approval is important but should happen throughout the process
- D. WBS should use nouns (deliverables) but this describes format, not detail level

### Question 3: Critical Path Analysis

**Scenario:**
Emily has calculated the following for activities on the critical path of her project:
- Activity A: Duration 5 days, ES=0, EF=5, LS=0, LF=5
- Activity B: Duration 3 days, ES=5, EF=8, LS=7, LF=10
- Activity C: Duration 4 days, ES=5, EF=9, LS=5, LF=9

**Question:**
Which activities are on the critical path?

**Options:**
A. Activities A, B, and C
B. Activities A and C only
C. Activities B and C only
D. Only Activity C

**Correct Answer: B. Activities A and C only**

**Explanation:**
Activities on the critical path have zero float (LS-ES=0 or LF-EF=0). Activity A has zero float (LS=ES=0, LF=EF=5). Activity B has 2 days of float (LS-ES=7-5=2). Activity C has zero float (LS=ES=5, LF=EF=9). Therefore, Activities A and C are on the critical path.

**Why other options are incorrect:**
- A. Activity B has float, so it's not critical
- C. Activity A is also critical
- D. Activity A is also critical

### Question 4: Risk Response Strategies

**Scenario:**
David has identified a risk that a key supplier might go bankrupt during his construction project. The probability is low (0.3), but the impact would be high (9 on a 1-10 scale). He wants to develop a response strategy.

**Question:**
What is the most appropriate risk response strategy for this high-impact, low-probability threat?

**Options:**
A. Mitigate - Reduce the probability of the supplier going bankrupt
B. Transfer - Purchase insurance or have backup suppliers ready
C. Accept - Create a contingency reserve to handle if it occurs
D. Avoid - Change the project to not use this supplier

**Correct Answer: B. Transfer - Purchase insurance or have backup suppliers ready**

**Explanation:**
For high-impact, low-probability threats, transfer is often the most appropriate strategy. In this case, having backup suppliers or purchasing supply chain insurance transfers the financial risk to another party. The risk score (2.7) suggests this is a significant risk that warrants more than acceptance.

**Why other options are incorrect:**
- A. Mitigating the probability of a supplier bankruptcy is usually not possible
- C. While acceptance is an option, the high impact suggests a more proactive approach
- D. Avoidance might not be practical if this is the best/only supplier

### Question 5: Change Control

**Scenario:**
Maria is the project manager for a systems implementation project. A stakeholder has requested a significant change to the system requirements that would impact the schedule and budget. The change request has been documented and submitted to the Change Control Board (CCB).

**Question:**
What is the first action Maria should take regarding this change request?

**Options:**
A. Implement the change immediately since the stakeholder requested it
B. Wait for the CCB to review and approve the change before taking action
C. Analyze the impact of the change on scope, schedule, and cost
D. Reject the change since it's not in the original requirements

**Correct Answer: C. Analyze the impact of the change on scope, schedule, and cost**

**Explanation:**
Before submitting to the CCB or implementing any change, the project manager should analyze the change's impact on the project's triple constraint (scope, schedule, cost) and other factors. This analysis provides the CCB with the information needed to make an informed decision.

**Why other options are incorrect:**
- A. Changes should not be implemented without proper analysis and approval
- B. Analysis should be done before CCB review
- D. Changes shouldn't be rejected without proper evaluation

### Question 6: Quality Assurance vs. Quality Control

**Scenario:**
Tom is reviewing his project's quality management approach. He wants to ensure his team is following the defined processes and that the final product meets requirements.

**Question:**
Which of the following activities is part of Quality Assurance rather than Quality Control?

**Options:**
A. Testing the software to find defects
B. Inspecting the completed work package for compliance
C. Auditing the project processes to ensure compliance
D. Measuring the dimensions of manufactured components

**Correct Answer: C. Auditing the project processes to ensure compliance**

**Explanation:**
Quality Assurance (QA) focuses on processes and is preventive, while Quality Control (QC) focuses on the product and is detective. Auditing processes to ensure they're being followed correctly is a QA activity. Testing, inspection, and measurement are QC activities focused on the product.

**Why other options are incorrect:**
- A, B, and D are all QC activities focused on testing or inspecting the product

### Question 7: Stakeholder Engagement

**Scenario:**
Jennifer is managing a project that will significantly impact the finance department. The finance stakeholders are currently unaware of the project (Unaware engagement level), but their active support is needed for success.

**Question:**
What is the most appropriate engagement strategy to move finance stakeholders from Unaware to Support?

**Options:**
A. Involve them in decision-making processes
B. Demonstrate how the project benefits their department
C. Address their specific concerns and issues
D. Empower them to lead project activities

**Correct Answer: B. Demonstrate how the project benefits their department**

**Explanation:**
To move stakeholders from Unaware to Support, they first need to be informed about the project and understand its benefits to them. Demonstrating benefits creates awareness and builds initial support. Once they're aware and supportive, more collaborative approaches can be used.

**Why other options are incorrect:**
- A. Involving in decision-making is better for moving from Support to Leading
- C. Addressing concerns is for moving from Resistant to Neutral
- D. Empowering leadership is for moving from Support to Leading

### Question 8: Project Closure

**Scenario:**
Alex's project has just completed all deliverables and received formal acceptance from the client. He's now preparing to close the project and has several activities to complete.

**Question:**
Which of the following activities is NOT typically part of project closure?

**Options:**
A. Documenting lessons learned
B. Conducting procurement closure activities
C. Updating the project management plan
D. Measuring initial benefits realization

**Correct Answer: C. Updating the project management plan**

**Explanation:**
During project closure, the project management plan is archived as part of the final records, not updated. The plan is a living document during the project but becomes historical at closure. Lessons learned, procurement closure, and benefits measurement are all typical closure activities.

**Why other options are incorrect:**
- A, B, and D are all essential closure activities

### Question 9: Integrated Change Control

**Scenario:**
Robert is implementing an integrated change control system for his project. He needs to ensure all changes are properly evaluated and approved before implementation.

**Question:**
What is the primary purpose of integrated change control?

**Options:**
A. Prevent all changes to the project baseline
B. Ensure changes are evaluated and approved before implementation
C. Document all changes for historical purposes
D. Allow stakeholders to request changes easily

**Correct Answer: B. Ensure changes are evaluated and approved before implementation**

**Explanation:**
Integrated change control's primary purpose is to review, evaluate, and approve changes before they're implemented. This ensures changes are beneficial, properly understood, and their impacts are known. It doesn't prevent changes but manages them effectively.

**Why other options are incorrect:**
- A. The goal is to manage changes, not prevent them
- C. Documentation is important but not the primary purpose
- D. Easy request access is not the main objective

### Question 10: Procurement Management

**Scenario:**
Lisa is selecting a vendor for her IT infrastructure project. She has received three proposals and needs to select the best vendor.

**Question:**
Which of the following is the most important selection criterion when all vendors appear technically capable?

**Options:**
A. Lowest price bid
B. Past performance on similar projects
C. Largest company with most resources
D. Best written proposal

**Correct Answer: B. Past performance on similar projects**

**Explanation:**
Past performance is often the best predictor of future success, especially when technical capability is established among all vendors. Historical performance shows reliability, quality, and ability to meet commitments. Price is important but shouldn't be the sole criterion if it risks project success.

**Why other options are incorrect:**
- A. Lowest price doesn't guarantee best value or success
- C. Company size doesn't guarantee project performance
- D. Proposal quality doesn't always translate to execution quality

### Question 11: Earned Value Management

**Scenario:**
Mike is reviewing his project's performance using Earned Value Management:
- PV (Planned Value) = $500,000
- EV (Earned Value) = $450,000
- AC (Actual Cost) = $550,000
- BAC (Budget at Completion) = $1,000,000

**Question:**
What is the Estimate at Completion (EAC) if the current performance continues?

**Options:**
A. $1,000,000
B. $1,100,000
C. $1,222,222
D. $1,500,000

**Correct Answer: C. $1,222,222**

**Explanation:**
Using the formula EAC = BAC / CPI (Cost Performance Index):
- CPI = EV / AC = $450,000 / $550,000 = 0.818
- EAC = $1,000,000 / 0.818 = $1,222,222

This shows the project will cost approximately $222,222 more than planned if current performance continues.

*For all EAC calculation methods and when to use each, see [Appendix A: Forecasting Formulas](../Chapter%2098/Appendix%20A%20–%20Key%20Formulas%20and%20Definitions.md#forecasting-formulas)*

**Why other options are incorrect:**
- A. $1,000,000 is the original budget (BAC)
- B. $1,100,000 doesn't account for the CPI calculation
- D. $1,500,000 is incorrect calculation

### Question 12: Communication Management

**Scenario:**
Samantha is developing a communications management plan for her international project with stakeholders in multiple countries and time zones.

**Question:**
What is the most important factor to consider when selecting communication technology for this project?

**Options:**
A. Cost of the technology
B. Ease of use for all stakeholders
C. Urgency of information need
D. Availability of the technology

**Correct Answer: D. Availability of the technology**

**Explanation:**
For an international project, technology availability across all locations is the most critical factor. If stakeholders cannot access the technology, communication will fail regardless of other factors. After availability is ensured, other factors like cost, ease of use, and urgency can be considered.

**Why other options are incorrect:**
- A, B, and C are important considerations but secondary to availability

### Question 13: Resource Management

**Scenario:**
David is managing resources for his construction project. He has several work packages that require the same skilled workers, creating conflicts in the schedule.

**Question:**
What is the best technique to resolve resource over-allocation without extending the project duration?

**Options:**
A. Resource leveling
B. Resource smoothing
C. Crashing the schedule
D. Fast-tracking the project

**Correct Answer: B. Resource smoothing**

**Explanation:**
Resource smoothing optimizes resource usage without changing the critical path or project end date. It adjusts activities within their float to balance resource demands. This is the appropriate technique when you want to resolve resource conflicts without extending duration.

**Why other options are incorrect:**
- A. Resource leveling may extend the project duration
- C. Crashing adds cost to reduce duration, not resolve resource conflicts
- D. Fast-tracking changes activity relationships, not resource allocation

### Question 14: Risk Monitoring

**Scenario:**
Maria is monitoring risks on her project. A risk she identified with a probability of 0.4 and impact of $50,000 has occurred, causing a $40,000 impact.

**Question:**
What is the difference between the planned risk cost and actual impact called?

**Options:**
A. Contingency reserve
B. Management reserve
C. Risk variance
D. Secondary risk

**Correct Answer: C. Risk variance**

**Explanation:**
Risk variance is the difference between the planned (expected) risk cost and the actual impact. Expected value = 0.4 × $50,000 = $20,000. Actual impact was $40,000, so variance = $40,000 - $20,000 = $20,000.

**Why other options are incorrect:**
- A. Contingency reserve is planned for known risks
- B. Management reserve is for unknown risks
- D. Secondary risk is a new risk from risk response
