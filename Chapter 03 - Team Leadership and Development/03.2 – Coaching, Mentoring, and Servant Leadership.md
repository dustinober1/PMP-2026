# 3.2 – Coaching, Mentoring, and Servant Leadership

## Overview

This section dives deeper into the specific leadership behaviors required for PMP success. It moves beyond "managing the team" to "developing the team."

### Key Concepts

1.  **Situational Leadership:** Adapting your style (Directing, Coaching, Supporting, Delegating) based on team capability and commitment.
2.  **Emotional Intelligence (EQ):** The ability to identify, assess, and control the emotions of oneself, of others, and of groups.
3.  **Coaching vs. Mentoring:**
    *   **Coaching:** Helping team members find their own solutions (short-term, skill-focused).
    *   **Mentoring:** Passing on experience and knowledge (long-term, career-focused).

### Structure

*   **3.2a – Leadership Styles:** Laissez-faire, Transactional, Servant, Transformational, Charismatic, Interactional.
*   **3.2b – Emotional Intelligence:** Self-awareness, self-management, social awareness, relationship management.
*   **3.2c – Training and Development:** Assessing skills gaps and creating training plans.

### 3.2d – Knowledge Check

1.  **Leadership Style:** A project manager is leading a team of highly experienced senior engineers who are experts in their field. The project manager focuses on removing roadblocks but otherwise stays out of their way. Which leadership style is this?
    *   A) Directive
    *   B) Laissez-faire / Delegative
    *   C) Transactional
    *   D) Autocratic

2.  **Emotional Intelligence:** A team member bursts into a meeting, angry about a decision made by another department. As a project manager with high EQ, what is your first reaction?
    *   A) Tell them to calm down and be professional.
    *   B) Acknowledge their frustration and ask to understand the impact of the decision.
    *   C) Ignore the outburst to keep the meeting on track.
    *   D) Report them to HR for unprofessional conduct.

**Answers:**
1.  **B** - Delegative/Laissez-faire is effective with high-performing experts.
2.  **B** - Acknowledging emotion (Social Awareness) and seeking to understand is the high-EQ response.
