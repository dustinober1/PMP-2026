## ðŸ’¡ Stakeholder Engagement Pro-Tips

1.  **Analysis Before Action**: When a new stakeholder appears or an existing one changes attitude, the *first* step is always to update the Register and analyze before changing the plan or escalating.
2.  **The Salience Model is for Priority**: Use Power, Legitimacy, and Urgency to decide who gets your immediate attention (Definitive) and who needs an advocate (Dependent).
3.  **Trust is a Calculation**: Use the Trust Equation to diagnose friction. If a stakeholder is skeptical, increase Credibility (data) and Intimacy (rapport) while lowering Self-Orientation (WIIFM).
4.  **Shadow Stakeholders are Risks**: Always ask your known stakeholders: "Who else should I be talking to?" This uncovers the hidden influencers who can kill a project.
5.  **Alignment Requires Interests, Not Positions**: When high-power stakeholders conflict, move the conversation from *what* they want (Position) to *why* they want it (Interest).
6.  **Agile is Collaborative, Not Just Reporting**: In Agile, stakeholders are participants. If they are surprised by a result, the engagement loop (Sprint Reviews/Refinement) is broken.
