# 5.2 – Business Analysis Fundamentals for Project Managers

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this section, you will be able to:
- Distinguish between business analysis and project management roles and responsibilities
- Apply the Business Analysis Core Concept Model (BACCM) to project scenarios
- Select appropriate business analysis techniques based on project context
- Integrate business analysis activities into the project lifecycle
- Evaluate solutions using business analysis metrics and methods

---

## Introduction: Why Business Analysis Matters for Project Managers

The lines between business analysis and project management are blurring. Modern project managers are expected to understand business needs deeply, facilitate requirements discovery, and ensure solutions deliver real business value. The PMI Guide to Business Analysis defines business analysis as:

> **Business Analysis:** The practice of enabling change in an enterprise by defining needs and recommending solutions that deliver value to stakeholders.

For PMP-certified professionals, business analysis competencies are no longer optional—they're essential. Understanding business analysis helps you:

- **Ask better questions** during project initiation and planning
- **Facilitate effective requirements gathering** even without a dedicated business analyst
- **Bridge the gap** between business stakeholders and technical teams
- **Validate that solutions** actually solve the business problem
- **Demonstrate value delivery** beyond just meeting scope, schedule, and cost constraints

This section introduces the fundamental concepts of business analysis that every project manager should know.

## Section Structure

This section covers the following topics:

- **BACCM and Core Concepts:** Understanding Change, Need, Solution, Value, Stakeholder, Context

**Remember:** The PMP exam increasingly tests business analysis concepts because effective project managers must understand and apply business analysis thinking. Master these concepts, and you'll not only pass the exam—you'll deliver more successful projects.

## Knowledge Check: Business Analysis Fundamentals

1.  **BACCM**: You are analyzing an existing manual process to identify opportunities for automation. This relates to which core concept of the BACCM?
    *   A) Solution
    *   B) Value
    *   C) Need
    *   D) Change

2.  **Requirements Classification**: A requirement states: "The system must be available 99.9% of the time." This is an example of a:
    *   A) Business Requirement
    *   B) Functional Requirement
    *   C) Non-Functional Requirement
    *   D) Transition Requirement

3.  **BA Techniques**: Your project has a highly uncertain scope, and you need to prioritize features for the next release based on what's most essential. Which technique would be most appropriate?
    *   A) MoSCoW Prioritization
    *   B) Use Case Modeling
    *   C) State Transition Diagram
    *   D) 5 Whys

**Answers:**
1.  **C** - Identifying opportunities for automation is about defining the underlying "Need" or problem to be solved.
2.  **C** - Non-Functional Requirements describe *how* a solution performs, such as reliability (availability).
3.  **A** - MoSCoW (Must, Should, Could, Won't have) is a common technique for prioritizing requirements, especially when scope is flexible.
